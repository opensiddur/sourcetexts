Then David and the elders, clothed in sackcloth, fell upon their faces. {{verse|21|17}}And David said unto God: 'Is it not I that commanded the people to be numbered? even I it is that have sinned and done very wickedly; but these sheep, what have they done? let Thy hand, I pray Thee, O {{sc|Lord}} my God, be against me, and against my father's house; but not against Thy people, that they should be plagued.'

{{verse|21|18}}Then the angel of the {{sc|Lord}} commanded Gad to say to David, that David should go up, and rear an altar unto the {{sc|Lord}} in the threshing-floor of Ornan the Jebusite. {{verse|21|19}}And David went up at the saying of Gad, which he spoke in the name of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|21|20}}And Ornan turned back, and saw the angel; and his four sons that were with him hid themselves. Now Ornan was threshing wheat. {{verse|21|21}}And as David came to Ornan, Ornan looked and saw David, and went out of the threshing-floor, and bowed down to David with his face to the ground. {{verse|21|22}}Then David said to Ornan: 'Give me the place of this threshing-floor, that I may build thereon an altar unto the {{sc|Lord}}; for the full price shalt thou give it me; that the plague may be stayed from the people.' {{verse|21|23}}And Ornan said unto David: 'Take it to thee, and let my lord the king do that which is good in his eyes; lo, I give thee the oxen for burnt-offerings, and the threshing-instruments for wood, and the wheat for the meal-offering; I give it all'{{verse|21|24}}And king David said to Ornan: 'Nay, but I will verily buy it for the full price; for I will not take that which is thine for the {{sc|Lord}}, nor offer a burnt-offering without cost.' {{verse|21|25}}So David gave to Ornan for the place six hundred shekels of gold by weight. {{verse|21|26}}And David built there an altar unto the {{sc|Lord}}, and offered burnt-offerings and peace-offerings, and called upon the {{sc|Lord}}; and He answered him from heaven by fire upon the altar of burnt-offering. {{verse|21|27}}And the {{sc|Lord}} commanded the angel; and he put up his sword back into the sheath thereof.

{{verse|21|28}}At that time, when David saw that the {{sc|Lord}} had answered him in the threshing-floor of Ornan the Jebusite, then he sacrificed there. {{verse|21|29}}For the tabernacle of the {{sc|Lord}}, which Moses made in the wilderness, and the altar of burnt-offering, were at that time in the high place at Gibeon. {{verse|21|30}}But David could not go before it to inquire of God; for he was terrified because of the sword of the angel of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{dropinitial|22}}{{verse|22|1}}Then David said: 'This is the house of the {{sc|Lord}} God, and this is the altar of burnt-offering for Israel.'

{{verse|22|2}}And David commanded to gather together the strangers that were in the land of Israel; and he set masons to hew wrought stones to build the house of God. {{verse|22|3}}And David prepared iron in abundance for the nails for the doors of the gates, and for the couplings; and brass in abundance without weight; {{verse|22|4}}and cedar-trees without number; for the Zidonians and they of Tyre brought cedar-trees in abundance to David. {{verse|22|5}}And David said: 'Solomon my son is young and tender, and the house that is to be builded for the {{sc|Lord}} must be exceeding magnificent, of fame and of glory throughout all countries; I will therefore make preparation for him.' So David prepared abundantly before his death.

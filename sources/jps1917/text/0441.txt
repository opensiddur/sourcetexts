{{verse|10|18}}Moreover the king made a great throne of ivory, and overlaid it with the finest gold. {{verse|10|19}}There were six steps to the throne, and the top of the throne was round behind; and there were arms on either side by the place of the seat, and two lions standing beside the arms. {{verse|10|20}}And twelve lions stood there on the one side and on the other upon the six steps; there was not the like made in any kingdom.

{{verse|10|21}}And all king Solomon's drinking-vessels were of gold, and all the vessels of the house of the forest of Lebanon were of pure gold; none were of silver; it was nothing accounted of in the days of Solomon. {{verse|10|22}}For the king had at sea a navy of Tarshish with the navy of Hiram; once every three years came the navy of Tarshish, bringing gold, and silver, ivory, and apes, and peacocks.

{{verse|10|23}}So king Solomon exceeded all the kings of the earth in riches and in wisdom. {{verse|10|24}}And all the earth sought the presence of Solomon, to hear his wisdom, which God had put in his heart. {{verse|10|25}}And they brought every man his present, vessels of silver, and vessels of gold, and raiment, and armour, and spices, horses, and mules, a rate year by year.

{{verse|10|26}}And Solomon gathered together chariots and horsemen; and he had a thousand and four hundred chariots, and twelve thousand horsemen, that he bestowed in the chariot cities, and with the king at Jerusalem. {{verse|10|27}}And the king made silver to be in Jerusalem as stones, and cedars made he to be as the sycamore-trees that are in the Lowland, for abundance. {{verse|10|28}}And the horses which Solomon had were brought out of Egypt; also out of Keveh, the king's merchants buying them of the men of Keveh at a price. {{verse|10|29}}And a chariot came up and went out of Egypt for six hundred shekels of silver, and a horse for a hundred and fifty; and so for all the kings of the Hittites, and for the kings of Aram, did they bring them out by their means.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|11:1}}11}}Now king Solomon loved many foreign women, besides the daughter of Pharaoh, women of the Moabites, Ammonites, Edomites, Zidonians, and Hittites; {{verse|11|2}}of the nations concerning which the {{sc|Lord}} said unto the children of Israel: 'Ye shall not go among them, neither shall they come among you; for surely they will turn away your heart after their gods'; Solomon did cleave unto these in love. {{verse|11|3}}And he had seven hundred wives, princesses, and three hundred concubines; and his wives turned away his heart. {{verse|11|4}}For it came to pass, when Solomon was old, that his wives turned away his heart after other gods; and his heart was not whole with the {{sc|Lord}} his God, as was the heart of David his father. {{verse|11|5}}For Solomon went after Ashtoreth the goddess of the Zidonians, and after Milcom the detestation of the Ammonites. {{verse|11|6}}And Solomon did that which was evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}}, and went not fully after the {{sc|Lord}}, as did David his father. {{verse|11|7}}Then did Solomon build a high place for Chemosh the detestation of Moab, in the mount that is before Jerusalem, and for Molech the detestation of the children of Ammon. {{verse|11|8}}And so did he for all his foreign wives, who offered and sacrificed unto their gods.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|1:1}}1}}{{sc|Now}} there was a certain man of Ramathaim-zophim, of the hill-country of Ephraim, and his name was Elkanah, the son of Jeroham, the son of Elihu, the son of Tohu, the son of Zuph, an Ephraimite. {{verse|1|2}}And he had two wives: the name of the one was Hannah, and the name of the other Peninnah; and Peninnah had children, but Hannah had no children. {{verse|1|3}}And this man went up out of his city from year to year to worship and to sacrifice unto the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts in Shiloh. And the two sons of Eli, Hophni and Phinehas, were there priests unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|1|4}}And it came to pass upon a day, when Elkanah sacrificed, that he gave to Peninnah his wife, and to all her sons and her daughters, portions; {{verse|1|5}}but unto Hannah he gave a double portion; for he loved Hannah, but the {{sc|Lord}} had shut up her womb. {{verse|1|6}}And her rival vexed her sore, to make her fret, because the {{sc|Lord}} had shut up her womb. {{verse|1|7}}And as he did so year by year, when she went up to the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, so she vexed her; therefore she wept, and would not eat. {{verse|1|8}}And Elkanah her husband said unto her: 'Hannah, why weepest thou? and why eatest thou not? and why is thy heart grieved? am not I better to thee than ten sons?' {{verse|1|9}}So Hannah rose up after they had eaten in Shiloh, and after they had drunk—now Eli the priest sat upon his seat by the door-post of the temple of the {{sc|Lord}}; {{verse|1|10}}and she was in bitterness of soul—and prayed unto the {{sc|Lord}}, and wept sore. {{verse|1|11}}And she vowed a vow, and said: 'O {{sc|Lord}} of hosts, if Thou wilt indeed look on the affliction of Thy handmaid, and remember me, and not forget Thy handmaid, but wilt give unto Thy handmaid a man-child, then I will give him unto the {{sc|Lord}} all the days of his life, and there shall no razor come upon his head.' {{verse|1|12}}And it came to pass, as she prayed long before the {{sc|Lord}}, that Eli watched her mouth. {{verse|1|13}}Now Hannah, she spoke in her heart; only her lips moved, but her voice could not be heard; therefore, Eli thought she had been drunken. {{verse|1|14}}And Eli said unto her: 'How long wilt thou be drunken? put away thy wine from thee.' {{verse|1|15}}And Hannah answered and said: 'No, my lord, I am a woman of a sorrowful spirit; I have drunk neither wine nor strong drink, but I poured out my soul before the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|1|16}}Count not thy handmaid for a wicked woman: for out of the abundance of my complaint and my vexation have I spoken hitherto.' {{verse|1|17}}Then Eli answered and said: 'Go in peace, and the God of Israel grant thy petition that thou hast asked of Him.' {{verse|1|18}}And she said: 'Let thy servant find favour in thy sight.'

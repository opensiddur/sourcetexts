{{verse|chapter=3|verse=21}}Of Gershon was the family of the Libnites, and the family of the Shimeites; these are the families of the Gershonites. {{verse|chapter=3|verse=22}}Those that were numbered of them, according to the number of all the males, from a month old and upward, even those that were numbered of them were seven thousand and five hundred. {{verse|chapter=3|verse=23}}The families of the Gershonites were to pitch behind the tabernacle westward; {{verse|chapter=3|verse=24}}the prince of the fathers' house of the Gershonites being Eliasaph the son of Lael, {{verse|chapter=3|verse=25}}and the charge of the sons of Gershon in the tent of meeting the tabernacle, and the Tent, the covering thereof, and the screen for the door of the tent of meeting, {{verse|chapter=3|verse=26}}and the hangings of the court, and the screen for the door of the court—which is by the tabernacle, and by the altar, round about—and the cords of it, even whatsoever pertaineth to the service thereof.

{{verse|chapter=3|verse=27}}And of Kohath was the family of the Amramites, and the family of the Izharites, and the family of the Hebronites, and the family of the Uzzielites; these are the families of the Kohathites: {{verse|chapter=3|verse=28}}according to the number of all the males, from a month old and upward, eight thousand and six hundred, keepers of the charge of the sanctuary. {{verse|chapter=3|verse=29}}The families of the sons of Kohath were to pitch on the side of the tabernacle southward; {{verse|chapter=3|verse=30}}the prince of the fathers' house of the families of the Kohathites being Elizaphan the son of Uzziel, {{verse|chapter=3|verse=31}}and their charge the ark, and the table, and the candlestick, and the altars, and the vessels of the sanctuary wherewith the priests minister, and the screen, and all that pertaineth to the service thereof; {{verse|chapter=3|verse=32}}Eleazar the son of Aaron the priest being prince of the princes of the Levites, and having the oversight of them that keep the charge of the sanctuary.

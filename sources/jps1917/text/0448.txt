<noinclude><pagequality level="3" user="Outlier59" />{{rh|{{larger|14}}.17|FIRST KINGS|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{verse|14|17}}And Jeroboam's wife arose, and departed, and came to Tirzah; and as she came to the threshold of the house, the child died. {{verse|14|18}}And all Israel buried him, and made lamentation for him; according to the word of the {{sc|Lord}}, which He spoke by the hand of His servant Ahijah the prophet.

{{verse|14|19}}And the rest of the acts of Jeroboam, how he warred, and how he reigned, behold, they are written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel. {{verse|14|20}}And the days which Jeroboam reigned were two and twenty years; and he slept with his fathers, and Nadab his son reigned in his stead.

{{verse|14|21}}And Rehoboam the son of Solomon reigned in Judah. Rehoboam was forty and one years old when he began to reign, and he reigned seventeen years in Jerusalem, the city which the {{sc|Lord}} had chosen out of all the tribes of Israel, to put His name there; and his mother's name was Naamah the Ammonitess. {{verse|14|22}}And Judah did that which was evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}}; and they moved Him to jealousy with their sins which they committed, above all that their fathers had done. {{verse|14|23}}For they also built them high places, and pillars, and Asherim, on every high hill, and under every leafy tree; {{verse|14|24}}and there were also sodomites in the land; they did according to all the abominations of the nations which the {{sc|Lord}} drove out before the children of Israel.

{{verse|14|25}}And it came to pass in the fifth year of king Rehoboam, that Shishak king of Egypt came up against Jerusalem; {{verse|14|26}}and he took away the treasures of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and the treasures of the king's house; he even took away all; and he took away all the shields of gold which Solomon had made. {{verse|14|27}}And king Rehoboam made in their stead shields of brass, and committed them to the hands of the captains of the guard, who kept the door of the king's house. {{verse|14|28}}And it was so, that as oft as the king went into the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, the guard bore them, and brought them back into the guard-chamber.

{{verse|14|29}}Now the rest of the acts of Rehoboam, and all that he did, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Judah? {{verse|14|30}}And there was war between Rehoboam and Jeroboam continually. {{verse|14|31}}And Rehoboam slept with his fathers, and was buried with his fathers in the city of David; and his mother's name was Naamah the Ammonitess. And Abijam his son reigned in his stead.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|15:1}}15}}Now in the eighteenth year of king Jeroboam the son of Nebat began Abijam to reign over Judah. {{verse|15|2}}Three years reigned he in Jerusalem; and his mother's name was Maacah the daughter of Abishalom. {{verse|15|3}}And he walked in all the sins of his father, which he had done before him; and his heart was not whole with the {{sc|Lord}} his God, as the heart of David his father. {{verse|15|4}}Nevertheless for David's sake did the {{sc|Lord}} his God give him a lamp in Jerusalem, to set up his son after him, and to establish Jerusalem; {{verse|15|5}}because David did that which was right in the eyes of the {{sc|Lord}}, and turned not aside from any thing that He commanded him all the days of his life, save only in the matter of Uriah the Hittite. {{verse|15|6}}Now there was war between Rehoboam and Jeroboam all the days of his life.

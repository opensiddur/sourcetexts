{{verse|18|23}}Then Zedekiah the son of Chenaanah came near, and smote Micaiah upon the check, and said: 'Which way went the spirit of the {{sc|Lord}} from me to speak unto thee?' {{verse|18|24}}And Micaiah said: 'Behold, thou shalt see on that day, when thou shalt go into an inner chamber to hide thyself.' {{verse|18|25}}And the king of Israel said: 'Take ye Micaiah; and carry him back unto Amon the governor of the city, and to Joash the king's son; {{verse|18|26}}and say: Thus saith the king: Put this fellow in the prison, and feed him with scant bread and with scant water, until I return in peace.' {{verse|18|27}}And Micaiah said: 'If thou return at all in peace, the {{sc|Lord}} hath not spoken by me.' And he said: 'Hear, ye peoples, all of you.'

{{verse|18|28}}So the king of Israel and Jehoshaphat the kind of Judah went up to Ramoth-gilead. {{verse|18|29}}And the king of Israel said unto Jehoshaphat: 'I will disguise myself, and go into the battle; but put thou on thy robes.' So the king of Israel disguised himself; and they went into the battle. {{verse|18|30}}Now the king of Aram had commanded the captains of his chariots, saying: 'Fight neither with small nor great, save only with the king of Israel.' {{verse|18|31}}And it came to pass, when the captains of the chariots saw Jehoshaphat, that they said: 'It is the king of Israel.' Therefore they turned about to fight against him; but Jehoshaphat cried out, and the {{sc|Lord}} helped him; and God moved them to depart from him. {{verse|18|32}}And it came to pass, when the captains of the chariots saw that it was not the king of Israel, that they turned back from pursuing him. {{verse|18|33}}And a certain man drew his bow at a venture, and smote the king of Israel between the lower armour and the breastplate; wherefore he said to the driver of the chariot: 'Turn thy hand, and carry me out of the host; for I am sore wounded.' {{verse|18|34}}And the battle increased that day; howbeit the king of Israel stayed himself up in his chariot against the Arameans until the even; and about the time of the going down of the sun he died.

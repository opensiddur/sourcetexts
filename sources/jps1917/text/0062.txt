And he divided the people that was with him, and the flocks, and the herds, and the camels, into two camps. {{verse|chapter=32|verse=9}}And he said: 'If Esau come to the one camp, and smite it, then the camp which is left shall escape.' {{verse|chapter=32|verse=10}}And Jacob said: 'O God of my father Abraham, and God of my father Isaac, O {{sc|Lord}}, who saidst unto me: Return unto thy country, and to thy kindred, and I will do thee good; {{verse|chapter=32|verse=11}}I am not worthy of all the mercies, and of all the truth, which Thou hast shown unto Thy servant; for with my staff I passed over this Jordan; and now I am become two camps. {{verse|chapter=32|verse=12}}Deliver me, I pray Thee, from the hand of my brother, from the hand of Esau; for I fear him, lest he come and smite me, the mother with the children. {{verse|chapter=32|verse=13}}And Thou saidst: I will surely do thee good, and make thy seed as the sand of the sea, which cannot be numbered for multitude.' {{verse|chapter=32|verse=14}}And he lodged there that night; and took of that which he had with him a present for Esau his brother: {{verse|chapter=32|verse=15}}two hundred she-goats and twenty he-goats, two hundred ewes and twenty rams, {{verse|chapter=32|verse=16}}thirty milch camels and their colts, forty kine and ten bulls, twenty she-asses and ten foals. {{verse|chapter=32|verse=17}}And he delivered them into the hand of his servants, every drove by itself; and said unto his servants: 'Pass over before me, and put a space betwixt drove and drove.' {{verse|chapter=32|verse=18}}And he commanded the foremost, saying: 'When Esau my brother meeteth thee, and asketh thee, saying: Whose art thou? and whither goest thou? and whose are these before thee? {{verse|chapter=32|verse=19}}then thou shalt say: They are thy servant Jacob's; it is a present sent unto my lord, even unto Esau; and, behold, he also is behind us.' {{verse|chapter=32|verse=20}}And he commanded also the second, and the third, and all that followed the droves, saying: 'In this manner shall ye speak unto Esau, when ye find him; {{verse|chapter=32|verse=21}}and ye shall say: Moreover, behold, thy servant Jacob is behind us.' For he said: 'I will appease him with the present that goeth before me, and afterward I will see his face; peradventure he will accept me.' {{verse|chapter=32|verse=22}}So the present passed over before him; and he himself lodged that night in the camp.

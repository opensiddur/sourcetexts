{{verse|10|28}}Thus Jehu destroyed Baal out of Israel. {{verse|10|29}}Howbeit from the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, wherewith he made Israel to sin, Jehu departed not from after them, the golden calves that were in Beth-el, and that were in Dan. {{verse|10|30}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Jehu: 'Because thou hast done well in executing that which is right in Mine eyes, and hast done unto the house of Ahab according to all that was in My heart, thy sons of the fourth generation shall sit on the throne of Israel.' {{verse|10|31}}But Jehu took no heed to walk in the law of the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, with all his heart; he departed not from the sins of Jeroboam, wherewith he made Israel to sin.

{{verse|10|32}}In those days the {{sc|Lord}} began to cut Israel short; and Hazael smote them in all the borders of Israel: {{verse|10|33}}from the Jordan eastward, all the land of Gilead, the Gadites, and the Reubenites, and the Manassites, from Aroer, which is by the valley of Arnon, even Gilead and Bashan. {{verse|10|34}}Now the rest of the acts of Jehu, and all that he did, and all his might, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel? {{verse|10|35}}And Jehu slept with his fathers; and they buried him in Samaria. And Jehoahaz his son reigned in his stead. {{verse|10|36}}And the time that Jehu reigned over Israel in Samaria was twenty and eight years.

And Zedekiah rebelled against the king of Babylon. {{dropinitial|25}}{{verse|25|1}}And it came to pass in the ninth year of his reign, in the tenth month, in the tenth day of the month, that Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon came, he and all his army, against Jerusalem, and encamped against it; and they built forts against it round about. {{verse|25|2}}So the city was besieged unto the eleventh year of king Zedekiah. {{verse|25|3}}On the ninth day of the [fourth] month the famine was sore in the city, so that there was no bread for the people of the land. {{verse|25|4}}Then a breach was made in the city, and all the men of war [fled] by night by the way of the gate between the two walls, which was by the king's garden—now the Chaldeans were against the city round about—and the king went by the way of the Arabah. {{verse|25|5}}But the army of the Chaldeans pursued after the king, and overtook him in the plains of Jericho; and all his army was scattered from him. {{verse|25|6}}Then they took the king, and carried him up unto the king of Babylon to Riblah; and they gave judgment upon him. {{verse|25|7}}And they slew the sons of Zedekiah before his eyes, and put out the eyes of Zedekiah, and bound him in fetters, and carried him to Babylon.

{{verse|25|8}}Now in the fifth month, on the seventh day of the month, which was the nineteenth year of king Nebuchadnezzar, king of Babylon, came Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard, a servant of the king of Babylon, unto Jerusalem. {{verse|25|9}}And he burnt the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and the king's house; and all the houses of Jerusalem, even every great man's house, burnt he with fire. {{verse|25|10}}And all the army of the Chaldeans, that were with the captain of the guard, broke down the walls of Jerusalem round about. {{verse|25|11}}And the residue of the people that were left in the city, and those that fell away, that fell to the king of Babylon, and the residue of the multitude, did Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard carry away captive. {{verse|25|12}}But the captain of the guard left of the poorest of the land to be vinedressers and husbandmen.

{{verse|25|13}}And the pillars of brass that were in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and the bases and the brazen sea that were in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, did the Chaldeans break in pieces, and carried the brass of them to Babylon. {{verse|25|14}}And the pots, and the shovels, and the snuffers, and the pans, and all the vessels of brass wherewith they ministered, took they away. {{verse|25|15}}And the fire-pans, and the basins, that which was of gold, in gold, and that which was of silver, in silver, the captain of the guard took away. {{verse|25|16}}The two pillars, the one sea, and the bases, which Solomon had made for the house of the {{sc|Lord}}; the brass of all these vessels was without weight. {{verse|25|17}}The height of the one pillar was eighteen cubits, and a capital of brass was upon it; and the height of the capital was three cubits; with network and pomegranates upon the capital round about, all of brass; and like unto these had the second pillar with network.

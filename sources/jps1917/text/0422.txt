{{verse|24|18}}And Gad came that day to David, and said unto him: 'Go up, rear an altar unto the {{sc|Lord}} in the threshing-floor of Araunah the Jebusite.' {{verse|24|19}}And David went up according to the saying of Gad, as the {{sc|Lord}} commanded. {{verse|24|20}}And Araunah looked forth, and saw the king and his servants coming on toward him; and Araunah went out, and bowed down before the king with his face to the ground. {{verse|24|21}}And Araunah said: 'Wherefore is my lord the king come to his servant?' And David said: 'To buy the threshing-floor of thee, to build an altar unto the {{sc|Lord}}, that the plague may be stayed from the people.' {{verse|24|22}}And Araunah said unto David: 'Let my lord the king take and offer up what seemeth good unto him; behold the oxen for the burnt-offering, and the threshing-instruments and the furniture of the oxen for the wood.' {{verse|24|23}}All this did Araunah the king give unto the king. And Araunah said unto the king: 'The {{sc|Lord}} thy God accept thee.' {{verse|24|24}}And the king said unto Araunah: 'Nay; but I will verily buy it of thee at a price; neither will I offer burnt-offerings unto the {{sc|Lord}} my God which cost me nothing.' So David bought the threshing-floor and the oxen for fifty shekels of silver. {{verse|24|25}}And David built there an altar unto the {{sc|Lord}}, and offered burnt-offerings and peace-offerings.

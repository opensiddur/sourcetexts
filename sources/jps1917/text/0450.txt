{{verse|15|33}}In the third year of Asa king of Judah began Baasa the son of Ahijah to reign over all Israel in Tirzah, and reigned twenty and four years. {{verse|15|34}}And he did that which was evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}}, and walked in the way of Jeroboam, and in his sin wherewith he made Israel to sin.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|16:1}}16}}And the word of the {{sc|Lord}} came to Jehu the son of Hanani against Baasa, saying: {{verse|16|2}}'Forasmuch as I exalted thee out of the dust, and made thee prince over My people Israel; and thou hast walked in the way of Jeroboam, and hast made My people Israel to sin, to provoke Me with their sins; {{verse|16|3}}behold, I will utterly sweep away Baasa and his house; and I will make thy house like the house of Jeroboam the son of Nebat. {{verse|16|4}}Him that dieth of Baasa in the city shall the dogs eat; and him that dieth of his in the field shall the fowls of the air eat.'

{{verse|16|5}}Now the rest of the acts of Baasa, and what he did, and his might, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel? {{verse|16|6}}And Baasa slept with his fathers, and was buried in Tirzah; and Elah his son reigned in his stead.

{{verse|16|7}}And moreover by the hand of the prophet Jehu the son of Hanani came the word of the {{sc|Lord}} against Baasa, and against his house, both because of all the evil that he did in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}}, to provoke Him with the work of his hands, in being like the house of Jeroboam, and because he smote him.

{{verse|16|8}}In the twenty and sixth year of Asa king of Judah began Elah the son of Baasa to reign over Israel in Tirzah, and reigned two years. {{verse|16|9}}And his servant Zimri, captain of half his chariots, conspired against him; now he was in Tirzah, drinking himself drunk in the house of Arza, who was over the household in Tirzah; {{verse|16|10}}and Zimri went in and smote him, and killed him, in the twenty and seventh year of Asa king of Judah, and reigned in his stead. {{verse|16|11}}And it came to pass, when he began to reign, as soon as he sat on his throne, that he smote all the house of Baasa; he left him not a single man-child, neither of his kinsfolks, nor of his friends. {{verse|16|12}}Thus did Zimri destroy all the house of Baasa, according to the word of the {{sc|Lord}}, which He spoke against Baasa by Jehu the prophet, {{verse|16|13}}for all the sins of Baasa, and the sins of Elah his son, which they sinned, and wherewith they made Israel to sin, to provoke the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, with their vanities.

{{verse|16|14}}Now the rest of the acts of Elah, and all that he did, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?

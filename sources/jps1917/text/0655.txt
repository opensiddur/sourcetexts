{{verse|49|23}}Of Damascus.


:Hamath is ashamed, and Arpad;
:For they have heard evil tidings, they are melted away;
:There is trouble in the sea;
:It cannot be quiet.
:{{verse|49|24|align=float}}Damascus is waxed feeble, she turneth herself to flee,
:And trembling hath seized on her;
:Anguish and pangs have taken hold of her, as of a woman in travail.
:{{verse|49|25|align=float}}'How is the city of praise left unrepaired,
:The city of my joy?'
:{{verse|49|26|align=float}}Therefore her young men shall fall in her broad places,

:{{verse|49|27|align=float}}And I will kindle a fire in the wall of Damascus,

<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh|{{larger|21}}.12|SECOND KINGS|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>Judah, that whosoever heareth of it, both his ears shall tingle. {{verse|21|13}}And I will stretch over Jerusalem the line of Samaria, and the plummet of the house of Ahab; and I will wipe Jerusalem as a man wipeth a dish, wiping it and turning it upside down. {{verse|21|14}}And I will cast off the remnant of Mine inheritance, and deliver them into the hand of their enemies; and they shall become a prey and a spoil to all their enemies; {{verse|21|15}}because they have done that which is evil in My sight, and have provoked Me, since the day their fathers came forth out of Egypt, even unto this day.'

{{verse|21|16}}Moreover Manasseh shed innocent blood very much, till he had filled Jerusalem from one end to another; beside his sin wherewith he made Judah to sin, in doing that which was evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|21|17}}Now the rest of the acts of Manasseh, and all that he did, and his sin that he sinned, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Judah? {{verse|21|18}}And Manasseh slept with his fathers, and was buried in the garden of his own house, in the garden of Uzza; and Amon his son reigned in his stead.

{{verse|21|19}}Amon was twenty and two years old when he began to reign; and he reigned two years in Jerusalem; and his mother's name was Meshullemeth the daughter of Haruz of Jotbah. {{verse|21|20}}And he did that which was evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}}, as did Manasseh his father. {{verse|21|21}}And he walked in all the way that his father walked in, and served the idols that his father served, and worshipped them. {{verse|21|22}}And he forsook the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of his fathers, and walked not in the way of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|21|23}}And the servants of Amon conspired against him, and put the king to death in his own house. {{verse|21|24}}But the people of the land slew all them that had conspired against king Amon; and the people of the land made Josiah his son king in his stead. {{verse|21|25}}Now the rest of the acts of Amon which he did, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Judah? {{verse|21|26}}And he was buried in his sepulchre in the garden of Uzza; and Josiah his son reigned in his stead.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|22:1}}22}}Josiah was eight years old when he began to reign; and he reigned thirty and one years in Jerusalem; and his mother's name was Jedidah the daughter of Adaiah of Bozkath. {{verse|22|2}}And he did that which was right in the eyes of the {{sc|Lord}}, and walked in all the way of David his father, and turned not aside to the right hand or to the left.

{{verse|22|3}}And it came to pass in the eighteenth year of king Josiah, that the king sent Shaphan the son of Azaliah, the son of Meshullam, the scribe, to the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, saying. {{verse|22|4}}'Go up to Hilkiah the high priest, that he may sum the money which is brought into the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, which the keepers of the door have gathered of the people; {{verse|22|5}}and let them deliver it into the hand of the workmen that have the oversight of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}; and let them give it to the workmen that are in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, to repair the breaches of the house; {{verse|22|6}}unto the carpenters, and to the builders, and to the masons; and for buying timber and hewn stone to repair the house.'—{{verse|22|7}}Howbeit there was no reckoning made with them of the money that was delivered into their hand; for they dealt faithfully.

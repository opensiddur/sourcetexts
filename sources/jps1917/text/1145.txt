{{dropinitial|{{anchor|28:1}}28}} Ahaz was twenty years old when he began to reign; and he reigned sixteen years in Jerusalem; and he did not that which was right in the eyes of the {{sc|Lord}}, like David his father; {{verse|28|2}}but he walked in the ways of the kings of Israel, and made also molten images for the Baalim. {{verse|28|3}}Moreover he offered in the valley of the son of Hinnom, and burnt his children in the fire, according to the abominations of the heathen, whom the {{sc|Lord}} cast out before the children of Israel. {{verse|28|4}}And he sacrificed and offered in the high places, and on the hills, and under every leafy tree. {{verse|28|5}}Wherefore the {{sc|Lord}} his God delivered him into the hand of the king of Aram; and they smote him, and carried away of his a great multitude of captives, and brought them to Damascus. And he was also delivered into the hand of the king of Israel, who smote him with a great slaughter. {{verse|28|6}}For Pekah the son of Remaliah slew in Judah a hundred and twenty thousand in one day, all of them valiant men; because they had forsaken the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers. {{verse|28|7}}And Zichri, a mighty man of Ephraim, slew Maaseiah the king's son, and Azrikam the ruler of the house, and Elkanah that was next to the king. {{verse|28|8}}And the children of Israel carried away captive of their brethren two hundred thousand women, sons, and daughters, and took also away much spoil from them, and brought the spoil to Samaria.

{{verse|28|9}}But a prophet of the {{sc|Lord}} was there, whose name was Oded; and he went out to meet the host that came to Samaria, and said unto them: 'Behold, because the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of your fathers, was wroth with Judah, He hath delivered them into your hand, and ye have slain them in a rage which hath reached up unto heaven. {{verse|28|10}}And now ye purpose to bring the children of Judah and Jerusalem into subjection for bondmen and bondwomen unto you; but are there not even with you acts of guilt of your own against the {{sc|Lord}} your God? {{verse|28|11}}Now hear me therefore, and send back the captives, that ye have taken captive of your brethren; for the fierce wrath of the {{sc|Lord}} is upon you.' {{verse|28|12}}Then certain of the heads of the children of Ephraim, Azariah the son of Jehohanan, Berechiah the son of Meshillemoth, and Jehizkiah the son of Shallum, and Amasa the son of Hadlai, stood up against them that came from the war, {{verse|28|13}}and said unto them: 'Ye shall not bring in the captives hither; for ye purpose that which will bring upon us guilt against the {{sc|Lord}}, to add unto our sins and to our guilt; for our guilt is great, and there is fierce wrath against Israel.' {{verse|28|14}}So the armed men left the captives and the spoil before the princes and all the congregation. {{verse|28|15}}And the men that have been mentioned by name rose up, and took the captives, and with the spoil clothed all that were naked among them, and arrayed them, and shod them, and gave them to eat and to drink, and anointed them, and carried all the feeble of them upon asses, and brought them to Jericho, the city of palm-trees, unto their brethren; then they returned to Samaria.

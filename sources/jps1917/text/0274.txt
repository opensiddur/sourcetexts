{{verse|31|9}}And Moses wrote this law, and delivered it unto the priests the sons of Levi, that bore the ark of the covenant of the {{sc|Lord}}, and unto all the elders of Israel. {{verse|31|10}}And Moses commanded them, saying: 'At the end of every seven years, in the set time of the year of release, in the feast of tabernacles, {{verse|31|11}}when all Israel is come to appear before the {{sc|Lord}} thy God in the place which He shall choose, thou shalt read this law before all Israel in their hearing. {{verse|31|12}}Assemble the people, the men and the women and the little ones, and thy stranger that is within thy gates, that they may hear, and that they may learn, and fear the {{sc|Lord}} your God, and observe to do all the words of this law; {{verse|31|13}}and that their children, who have not known, may hear, and learn to fear the {{sc|Lord}} your God, as long as ye live in the land whither ye go over the Jordan to possess it.'

{{verse|31|14}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'Behold, thy days approach that thou must die; call Joshua, and present yourselves in the tent of meeting, that I may give him a charge.' And Moses and Joshua went, and presented themselves in the tent of meeting. {{verse|31|15}}And the {{sc|Lord}} appeared in the Tent in a pillar of cloud; and the pillar of cloud stood over the door of the Tent. {{verse|31|16}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'Behold, thou art about to sleep with thy fathers; and this people will rise up, and go astray after the foreign gods of the land, whither they go to be among them, and will forsake Me, and break My covenant which I have made with them. {{verse|31|17}}Then My anger shall be kindled against them in that day, and I will forsake them, and I will hide My face from them, and they shall be devoured, and many evils and troubles shall come upon them; so that they will say in that day: Are not these evils come upon us because our God is not among us? {{verse|31|18}}And I will surely hide My face in that day for all the evil which they shall have wrought, in that they are turned unto other gods. {{verse|31|19}}Now therefore write ye this song for you, and teach thou it the children of Israel; put it in their mouths, that this song may be a witness for Me against the children of Israel. {{verse|31|20}}For when I shall have brought them into the land which I swore unto their fathers, flowing with milk and honey; and they shall have eaten their fill, and waxen fat; and turned unto other gods, and served them, and despised Me, and broken My covenant; {{verse|31|21}}then it shall come to pass, when many evils and troubles are come upon them, that this song shall testify before them as a witness; for it shall not be forgotten out of the mouths of their seed; for I know their imagination how they do even now, before I have brought them into the land which I swore.' {{verse|31|22}}So Moses wrote this song the same day, and taught it the children of Israel. {{verse|31|23}}And he gave Joshua the son of Nun a charge, and said: 'Be strong and of good courage; for thou shalt bring the children of Israel into the land which I swore unto them; and I will be with thee.'

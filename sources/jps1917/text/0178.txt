<noinclude><pagequality level="3" user="Outlier59" />{{rh|{{larger|27}}.26|LEVITICUS|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>whether it be ox or sheep, it is the {{sc|Lord's}}. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=27}}And if it be of an unclean beast, then he shall ransom it according to thy valuation, and shall add unto it the fifth part thereof; or if it be not redeemed, then it shall be sold according to thy valuation.

{{verse|chapter=27|verse=28}}Notwithstanding, no devoted thing, that a man may devote unto the {{sc|Lord}} of all that he hath, whether of man or beast, or of the field of his possession, shall be sold or redeemed; every devoted thing is most holy unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=29}}None devoted, that may be devoted of men, shall be ransomed; he shall surely be put to death.

{{verse|chapter=27|verse=30}}And all the tithe of the land, whether of the seed of the land, or of the fruit of the tree, is the {{sc|Lord}}'s; it is holy unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=31}}And if a man will redeem aught of his tithe, he shall add unto it the fifth part thereof. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=32}}And all the tithe of the herd or the flock, whatsoever passeth under the rod, the tenth shall be holy unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=33}}He shall not inquire whether it be good or bad, neither shall he change it; and if he change it at all, then both it and that for which it is changed shall be holy; it shall not be redeemed.

{{verse|chapter=27|verse=34}}These are the commandments, which the {{sc|Lord}} commanded Moses for the children of Israel in mount Sinai.<noinclude>{{c|156}}</noinclude>

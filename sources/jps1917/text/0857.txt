{{dropinitial|{{anchor|81:1}}81}} For the Leader; upon the Gittith. [A Psalm] of Asaph.


:{{verse|81|2|align=float}}Sing aloud unto God our strength;
:Shout unto the God of Jacob.
:{{verse|81|3|align=float}}Take up the melody, and sound the timbrel,
:The sweet harp with the psaltery.
:{{verse|81|4|align=float}}Blow the horn at the new moon,
:At the full moon for our feast-day.
:{{verse|81|5|align=float}}For it is a statute for Israel,
:An ordinance of the God of Jacob.
:{{verse|81|6|align=float}}He appointed it in Joseph for a testimony,
:When He went forth against the land of Egypt.
:The speech of one that I knew not did I hear:
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|81|7|align=float}}'I removed his shoulder from the burden; 
:His hands were freed from the basket.
:{{verse|81|8|align=float}}Thou didst call in trouble, and I rescued thee;
:I answered thee in the secret place of thunder;
:I proved thee at the waters of Meribah. {{float right|Selah}}
:{{verse|81|9|align=float}}Hear, O My people, and I will admonish thee:
:O Israel, if thou wouldest hearken unto Me!
:{{verse|81|10|align=float}}There shall no strange god be in thee; 
:Neither shalt thou worship any foreign god.
:{{verse|81|11|align=float}}I am the {{sc|Lord}} thy God,
:Who brought thee up out of the land of Egypt;
:Open thy mouth wide, and I will fill it.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|81|12|align=float}}But My people hearkened not to My voice;
:And Israel would none of Me.
:{{verse|81|13|align=float}}So I let them go after the stubbornness of their heart,
:That they might walk in their own counsels.
:{{verse|81|14|align=float}}Oh that My people would hearken unto Me,
:That Israel would walk in My ways!
:{{verse|81|15|align=float}}I would soon subdue their enemies,
:And turn My hand against their adversaries.
:{{verse|81|16|align=float}}The haters of the {{sc|Lord}} should dwindle away before Him;
:And their punishment should endure for ever.
:{{verse|81|17|align=float}}They should also be fed with the fat of wheat;
:And with honey out of the rock would I satisfy thee.'

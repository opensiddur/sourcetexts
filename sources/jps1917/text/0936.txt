{{dropinitial|{{anchor|25:1}}25}} These also are proverbs of Solomon, which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied out.

:{{verse|25|2|align=float}}It is the glory of God to conceal a thing;
:But the glory of kings is to search out a matter.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|3|align=float}}The heaven for height, and the earth for depth,
:And the heart of kings is unsearchable.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|4|align=float}}Take away the dross from the silver,
:And there cometh forth a vessel for the refiner;
:{{verse|25|5|align=float}}Take away the wicked from before the king,
:And his throne shall be established in righteousness.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|6|align=float}}Glorify not thyself in the presence of the king,
:And stand not in the place of great men;
:{{verse|25|7|align=float}}For better is it that it be said unto thee: 'Come up hither',
:Than that thou shouldest be put lower in the presence of the prince,
:Whom thine eyes have seen.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|8|align=float}}Go not forth hastily to strive,
:Lest thou know not what to do in the end thereof,
:When thy neighbour hath put thee to shame.
:{{verse|25|9|align=float}}Debate thy cause with thy neighbour,
:But reveal not the secret of another;
:{{verse|25|10|align=float}}Lest he that heareth it revile thee,
:And thine infamy turn not away.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|11|align=float}}A word fitly spoken
:Is like apples of gold in settings of silver.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|12|align=float}}As an ear-ring of gold, and an ornament of fine gold,
:So is a wise reprover upon an obedient ear.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|13|align=float}}As the cold of snow in the time of harvest,
:So is a faithful messenger to him that sendeth him;
:For he refresheth the soul of his master.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|14|align=float}}As vapours and wind without rain,
:So is he that boasteth himself of a false gift.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|15|align=float}}By long forbearing is a ruler persuaded,
:And a soft tongue breaketh the bone.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|25|16|align=float}}Hast thou found honey? eat so much as is sufficient for thee,
:Lest thou be filled therewith, and vomit it.

Then did I eat it; and it was in my mouth as honey for sweetness.

{{verse|3|4}}And He said unto me: 'Son of man, go, get thee unto the house of Israel, and speak with My words unto them. {{verse|3|5}}For thou art not sent to a people of an unintelligible speech and of a slow tongue, but to the house of Israel; {{verse|3|6}}not to many peoples of an unintelligible speech and of a slow tongue, whose words thou canst not understand. Surely, if I sent thee to them, they would hearken unto thee. {{verse|3|7}}But the house of Israel will not consent to hearken unto thee; for they consent not to hearken unto Me; for all the house of Israel are of a hard forehead and of a stiff heart. {{verse|3|8}}Behold, I have made thy face hard against their faces, and thy forehead hard against their foreheads. {{verse|3|9}}As an adamant harder than flint have I made thy forehead; fear them not, neither be dismayed at their looks, for they are a rebellious house.'

{{verse|3|10}}Moreover He said unto me: 'Son of man, all My words that I shall speak unto thee receive in thy heart, and hear with thine ears. {{verse|3|11}}And go, get thee to them of the captivity, unto the children of thy people, and speak unto them, and tell them: Thus saith the Lord {{sc|God}}; whether they will hear, or whether they will forbear.'

{{verse|3|12}}Then a spirit lifted me up, and I heard behind me the voice of a great rushing: 'Blessed be the glory of the {{sc|Lord}} from His place'; {{verse|3|13}}also the noise of the wings of the living creatures as they touched one another, and the noise of the wheels beside them, even the noise of a great rushing. {{verse|3|14}}So a spirit lifted me up, and took me away; and I went in bitterness, in the heat of my spirit, and the hand of the {{sc|Lord}} was strong upon me. {{verse|3|15}}Then I came to them of the captivity at Tel-abib, that dwelt by the river Chebar, and I sat where they sat; and I remained there appalled among them seven days.

{{verse|3|16}}And it came to pass at the end of seven days, that the word of the {{sc|Lord}} came unto me, saying: {{verse|3|17}}'Son of man, I have appointed thee a watchman unto the house of Israel; and when thou shalt hear a word at My mouth, thou shalt give them warning from Me. {{verse|3|18}}When I say unto the wicked: Thou shalt surely die; and thou givest him not warning, nor speakest to warn the wicked from his wicked way, to save his life; the same wicked man shall die in his iniquity, but his blood will I require at thy hand. {{verse|3|19}}Yet if thou warn the wicked, and he turn not from his wickedness, nor from his wicked way, he shall die in his iniquity; but thou hast delivered thy soul. {{verse|3|20}}Again, when a righteous man doth turn from his righteousness, and commit iniquity, I will lay a stumblingblock before him, he shall die; because thou hast not given him warning, he shall die in his sin, and his righteous deeds which he hath done shall not be remembered; but his blood will I require at thy hand. {{verse|3|21}}Nevertheless if thou warn the righteous man, that the righteous sin not, and he doth not sin, he shall surely live, because he took warning; and thou hast delivered thy soul.'

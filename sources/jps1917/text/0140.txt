{{dropinitial|{{anchor|1:1}}1}}{{sc|And}} the {{sc|Lord}} called unto Moses, and spoke unto him out of the tent of meeting, saying: {{verse|chapter=1|verse=2}}Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them:

When any man of you bringeth an offering unto the {{sc|Lord}}, ye shall bring your offering of the cattle, even of the herd or of the flock.

{{verse|chapter=1|verse=3}}If his offering be a burnt-offering of the herd, he shall offer it a male without blemish; he shall bring it to the door of the tent of meeting, that he may be accepted before the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=4}}And he shall lay his hand upon the head of the burnt-offering; and it shall be accepted for him to make atonement for him. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=5}}And he shall kill the bullock before the {{sc|Lord}}; and Aaron's sons, the priests, shall present the blood, and dash the blood round about against the altar that is at the door of the tent of meeting. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=6}}And he shall flay the burnt-offering, and cut it into its pieces. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=7}}And the sons of Aaron the priest shall put fire upon the altar, and lay wood in order upon the fire. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=8}}And Aaron's sons, the priests, shall lay the pieces, and the head, and the suet, in order upon the wood that is on the fire which is upon the altar; {{verse|chapter=1|verse=9}}but its inwards and its legs shall he wash with water; and the priest shall make the whole smoke on the altar, for a burnt-offering, an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the {{sc|Lord}}.

{{verse|chapter=1|verse=10}}And if his offering be of the flock, whether of the sheep, or of the goats, for a burnt-offering, he shall offer it a male without blemish. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=11}}And he shall kill it on the side of the altar northward before the {{sc|Lord}}; and Aaron's sons, the priests, shall dash its blood against the altar round about. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=12}}And he shall cut it into its pieces; and the priest shall lay them, with its head and its suet, in order on the wood that is on the fire which is upon the altar. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=13}}But the inwards and the legs shall he wash with water; and the priest shall offer the whole, and make it smoke upon the altar; it is a burnt-offering, an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the {{sc|Lord}}.

{{verse|chapter=1|verse=14}}And if his offering to the {{sc|Lord}} be a burnt-offering of fowls, then he shall bring his offering of turtle-doves, or of young pigeons. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=15}}And the priest shall bring it unto the altar, and pinch off its head, and make it smoke on the altar; and the blood thereof shall be drained out on the side of the altar. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=16}}And he shall take away its crop with the feathers thereof, and cast it beside the altar on the east part, in the place of the ashes. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=17}}And he shall rend it by the wings thereof, but shall not divide it asunder; and the priest shall make it smoke upon the altar, upon the wood that is upon the fire; it is a burnt-offering, an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the {{sc|Lord}}.

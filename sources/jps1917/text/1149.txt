{{verse|30|13}}And there assembled at Jerusalem much people to keep the feast of unleavened bread in the second month, a very great congregation. {{verse|30|14}}And they arose and took away the altars that were in Jerusalem, and all the altars for incense took they away, and cast them into the brook Kidron. {{verse|30|15}}Then they killed the passover lamb on the fourteenth day of the second month; and the priests and the Levites were ashamed, and sanctified themselves, and brought burnt-offerings into the house of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|30|16}}And they stood in their place after their order, according to the law of Moses the man of God; the priests dashed the blood, which they received of the hand of the Levites. {{verse|30|17}}For there were many in the congregation that had not sanctified themselves; therefore the Levites had the charge of killing the passover lambs for every one that was not clean, to sanctify them unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|30|18}}For a multitude of the people, even many of Ephraim and Manasseh, Issachar and Zebulun, had not cleansed themselves, yet did they eat the passover otherwise than it is written. For Hezekiah had prayed for them, saying: 'The good {{sc|Lord}} pardon {{verse|30|19}}every one that setteth his heart to seek God, the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of his fathers, though [he be] not [cleansed] according to the purification that pertaineth to holy things.' {{verse|30|20}}And the {{sc|Lord}} hearkened to Hezekiah, and healed the people. {{verse|30|21}}And the children of Israel that were present at Jerusalem kept the feast of unleavened bread seven days with great gladness; and the Levites and the priests praised the {{sc|Lord}} day by day, singing with loud instruments unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|30|22}}And Hezekiah spoke encouragingly unto all the Levites that were well skilled in the service of the {{sc|Lord}}. So they did eat throughout the feast for the seven days, offering sacrifices of peace-offerings, and giving thanks to the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers.

{{verse|30|23}}And the whole congregation took counsel to keep other seven days; and they kept other seven days with gladness. {{verse|30|24}}For Hezekiah king of Judah did give to the congregation for offerings a thousand bullocks and seven thousand sheep; and the princes gave to the congregation a thousand bullocks and ten thousand sheep; and priests sanctified themselves in great numbers. {{verse|30|25}}And all the congregation of Judah, with the priests and the Levites, and all the congregation that came out of Israel, and the strangers that came out of the land of Israel, and that dwelt in Judah, rejoiced. {{verse|30|26}}So there was great joy in Jerusalem; for since the time of Solomon the son of David king of Israel there was not the like in Jerusalem. {{verse|30|27}}Then the priests the Levites arose and blessed the people; and their voice was heard [of the {{sc|Lord}}], and their prayer came up to His holy habitation, even unto heaven.

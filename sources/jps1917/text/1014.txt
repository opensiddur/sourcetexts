<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh|{{larger|7}}.4|ECCLESIATES|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>:{{verse|7|4|align=float}}The heart of the wise is in the house of mourning;
:But the heart of fools is in the house of mirth.
:{{verse|7|5|align=float}}It is better to hear the rebuke of the wise,
:Than for a man to hear the song of fools.
:{{verse|7|6|align=float}}For as the crackling of thorns under a pot,
:So is the laughter of the fool; this also is vanity.
:{{verse|7|7|align=float}}Surely oppression turneth a wise man into a fool;
:And a gift destroyeth the understanding.
:{{verse|7|8|align=float}}Better is the end of a thing than the beginning thereof;
:And the patient in spirit is better than the proud in spirit.
:{{verse|7|9|align=float}}Be not hasty in thy spirit to be angry;
:For anger resteth in the bosom of fools.

{{verse|7|10}}Say not thou: 'How was it that the former days were better than these?' for it is not out of wisdom that thou inquirest concerning this.

:{{verse|7|11|align=float}}Wisdom is good with an inheritance,
:Yea, a profit to them that see the sun.

{{verse|7|12}}For wisdom is a defence, even as money is a defence; but the excellency of knowledge is, that wisdom preserveth the life of him that hath it.

{{verse|7|13}}Consider the work of God; for who can make that straight, which He hath made crooked? {{verse|7|14}}In the day of prosperity be joyful, and in the day of adversity consider; God hath made even the one as well as the other, to the end that man should find nothing after him.

{{verse|7|15}}All things have I seen in the days of my vanity; there is a righteous man that perisheth in his righteousness, and there is a wicked man that prolongeth his life in his evil-doing. {{verse|7|16}}Be not righteous overmuch; neither make thyself overwise; why shouldest thou destroy thyself? {{verse|7|17}}Be not overmuch wicked, neither be thou foolish; why shouldest thou die before thy time? {{verse|7|18}}It is good that thou shouldest take hold of the one; yea, also from the other withdraw not thy hand; for he that feareth God shall discharge himself of them all.

{{verse|7|19}}Wisdom is a stronghold to the wise man more than ten rulers that are in a city. {{verse|7|20}}For there is not a righteous man upon earth, that doeth good, and sinneth not.

{{verse|7|21}}Also take not heed unto all words that are spoken, lest thou hear thy servant curse thee; {{verse|7|22}}for oftentimes also thine own heart knoweth that thou thyself likewise hast cursed others.

{{verse|7|23}}All this have I tried by wisdom; I said: 'I will get wisdom'; but it was far from me. {{verse|7|24}}That which is is far off, and exceeding deep; who can find it out? {{verse|7|25}}I turned about, and applied my heart to know and to search out, and to seek wisdom and the reason of things, and to know wickedness to be folly, and foolishness to be madness; {{verse|7|26}}and I find more bitter than death the woman, whose heart is snares and nets, and her hands as bands; whoso pleaseth God shall escape from her; but the sinner shall be taken by her. {{verse|7|27}}Behold, this have I found, saith Koheleth, adding one thing to another, to find out the account; {{verse|7|28}}which yet my soul sought, but I found not; one man among a thousand have I found; but a woman among all those have I not found. {{verse|7|29}}Behold, this only have I found, that God made man upright; but they have sought out many inventions.

{{verse|chapter=9|verse=22}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'Stretch forth thy hand toward heaven, that there may be hail in all the land of Egypt, upon man, and upon beast, and upon every herb of the field, throughout the land of Egypt.' {{verse|chapter=9|verse=23}}And Moses stretched forth his rod toward heaven; and the {{sc|Lord}} sent thunder and hail, and fire ran down unto the earth; and the {{sc|Lord}} caused to hail upon the land of Egypt. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=24}}So there was hail, and fire flashing up amidst the hail, very grievous, such as had not been in all the land of Egypt since it became a nation. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=25}}And the hail smote throughout all the land of Egypt all that was in the field, both man and beast; and the hail smote every herb of the field, and broke every tree of the field. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=26}}Only in the land of Goshen, where the children of Israel were, was there no hail. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=27}}And Pharaoh sent, and called for Moses and Aaron, and said unto them: 'I have sinned this time; the thunders shall cease, neither shall there be any more hail; that thou mayest know that the earth is the {{sc|Lord}}'s. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=30}}But as for thee and thy servants, I know that ye will not yet fear the {{sc|Lord}} God.'— {{verse|chapter=9|verse=31}}And the flax and the barley were smitten; and the thunders and hail ceased, and the rain was not poured upon the earth. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=34}}And when Pharaoh saw that the rain and the hail and the thunders were ceased, he sinned yet more, and hardened his heart, he and his servants. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=35}}And the heart of Pharaoh was hardened, and he did not let the children of Israel go;

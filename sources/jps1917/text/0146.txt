I have given it as their portion of My offerings made by fire; it is most holy, as the sin-offering, and as the guilt-offering. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=11}}Every male among the children of Aaron may eat of it, as a due for ever throughout your generations, from the offerings of the {{sc|Lord}} made by fire; whatsoever toucheth them shall be holy.

{{verse|chapter=6|verse=12}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses, saying: {{verse|chapter=6|verse=13}}This is the offering of Aaron and of his sons, which they shall offer unto the {{sc|Lord}} in the day when he is anointed: the tenth part of an ephah of fine flour for a meal-offering perpetually, half of it in the morning, and half thereof in the evening. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=14}}On a griddle it shall be made with oil; when it is soaked, thou shalt bring it in; in broken pieces shalt thou offer the meal-offering for a sweet savour unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=15}}And the anointed priest that shall be in his stead from among his sons shall offer it, it is a due for ever; it shall be wholly made to smoke unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=16}}And every meal-offering of the priest shall be wholly made to smoke; it shall not be eaten.

{{verse|chapter=6|verse=17}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses, saying: {{verse|chapter=6|verse=18}}Speak unto Aaron and to his sons, saying:

This is the law of the sin-offering: in the place where the burnt-offering is killed shall the sin-offering be killed before the {{sc|Lord}}; it is most holy. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=19}}The priest that offereth it for sin shall eat it; in a holy place shall it be eaten, in the court of the tent of meeting. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=20}}Whatsoever shall touch the flesh thereof shall be holy; and when there is sprinkled of the blood thereof upon any garment, thou shalt wash that whereon it was sprinkled in a holy place. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=21}}But the earthen vessel wherein it is sodden shall be broken; and if it be sodden in a brazen vessel, it shall be scoured, and rinsed in water. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=22}}Every male among the priests may eat thereof; it is most holy. {{verse|chapter=6|verse=23}}And no sin-offering, whereof any of the blood is brought into the tent of meeting to make atonement in the holy place, shall be eaten; it shall be burnt with fire.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|7:1}}7}}And this is the law of the guilt-offering: it is most holy. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=2}}In the place where they kill the burnt-offering shall they kill the guilt-offering: and the blood thereof shall be dashed against the altar round about. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=3}}And he shall offer of it all the fat thereof: the fat tail, and the fat that covereth the inwards, {{verse|chapter=7|verse=4}}and the two kidneys, and the fat that is on them, which is by the loins, and the lobe above the liver, which he shall take away by the kidneys. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=5}}And the priest shall make them smoke upon the altar for an offering made by fire unto the {{sc|Lord}}; it is a guilt-offering. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=6}}Every male among the priests may eat thereof; it shall be eaten in a holy place; it is most holy. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=7}}As is the sin-offering, so is the guilt-offering; there is one law for them; the priest that maketh atonement therewith, he shall have it. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=8}}And the priest that offereth any man's burnt-offering, even the priest shall have to himself the skin of the burnt-offering which he hath offered. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=9}}And every meal-offering that is baked in the oven, and all that is dressed in the stewing-pan, and on the griddle, shall be the priest's that offereth it. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=10}}And every meal-offering, mingled with oil, or dry, shall all the sons of Aaron have, one as well as another.

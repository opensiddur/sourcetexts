<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh||JEREMIAH|{{larger|26}}.9}}{{rule}}</noinclude>that Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon had carried away captive Jeconiah the son of Jehoiakim, king of Judah, and the princes of Judah, with the craftsmen and smiths, from Jerusalem, and had brought them to Babylon. {{verse|24|2}}One basket had very good figs, like the figs that are first-ripe; and the other basket had very bad figs, which could not be eaten, they were so bad. {{verse|24|3}}Then said the {{sc|Lord}} unto me: 'What seest thou, Jeremiah?' And I said: 'Figs; the good figs, very good; and the bad, very bad, that cannot be eaten, they are so bad.' {{verse|24|4}}And the word of the {{sc|Lord}} came unto me, saying: {{verse|24|5}}'Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel: Like these good figs, so will I regard the captives of Judah, whom I have sent out of this place into the land of the Chaldeans, for good. {{verse|24|6}}And I will set Mine eyes upon them for good, and I will bring them back to this land; and I will build them, and not pull them down; and I will plant them, and not pluck them up. {{verse|24|7}}And I will give them a heart to know Me, that I am the {{sc|Lord}}; and they shall be My people, and I will be their God; for they shall return unto Me with their whole heart. {{verse|24|8}}And as the bad figs, which cannot be eaten, they are so bad; surely thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: So will I make Zedekiah the king of Judah, and his princes, and the residue of Jerusalem, that remain in this land, and them that dwell in the land of Egypt; {{verse|24|9}}I will even make them a horror among all the kingdoms of the earth for evil; a reproach and a proverb, a taunt and a curse, in all places whither I shall drive them. {{verse|24|10}}And I will send the sword, the famine, and the pestilence, among them, till they be consumed from off the land that I gave unto them and to their fathers.'


{{dropinitial|{{anchor|25:1}}25}}The word that came to Jeremiah concerning all the people of Judah in the fourth year of Jehoiakim the son of Josiah, king of Judah, that was the first year of Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon; {{verse|25|2}}which Jeremiah the prophet spoke unto all the people of Judah, and to all the inhabitants of Jerusalem, saying:

{{verse|25|3}}From the thirteenth year of Josiah the son of Amon, king of Judah, even unto this day, these three and twenty years, the word of the {{sc|Lord}} hath come unto me, and I have spoken unto you, speaking betimes and often; but ye have not hearkened. {{verse|25|4}}And the {{sc|Lord}} hath sent unto you all His servants the prophets, sending them betimes and often—but ye have not hearkened, nor inclined your ear to hear—{{verse|25|5}}saying: 
'Return ye now every one from his evil way, and from the evil of your doings, and dwell in the land that the {{sc|Lord}} hath given unto you and to your fathers, for ever and ever; {{verse|25|6}}and go not after other gods to serve them, and to worship them, and provoke Me not with the work of your hands, and I will do you no hurt.' {{verse|25|7}}Yet ye have not hearkened unto Me, saith the {{sc|Lord}}; that ye might provoke Me with the work of your hands to your own hurt. {{verse|25|8}}Therefore thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts: Because ye have not heard My words, {{verse|25|9}}behold, I will send and take all the families of the north, saith the {{sc|Lord}}, and I will send unto Nebuchadrezzar the king of Babylon, My servant, and will bring them against this land, and against the inhabitants thereof, and against all these nations round about; and I will utterly destroy them, and make them an astonishment, and a hissing, and perpetual desolations.<noinclude>{{smallrefs}}

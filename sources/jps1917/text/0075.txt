And their heart failed them, and they turned trembling one to another, saying: 'What is this that God hath done unto us?' {{verse|chapter=42|verse=29}}And they came unto Jacob their father unto the land of Canaan, and told him all that had befallen them, saying: {{verse|chapter=42|verse=30}}'The man, the lord of the land, spoke roughly with us, and took us for spies of the country. {{verse|chapter=42|verse=31}}And we said unto him: We are upright men; we are no spies. {{verse|chapter=42|verse=32}}We are twelve brethren, sons of our father; one is not, and the youngest is this day with our father in the land of Canaan. {{verse|chapter=42|verse=33}}And the man, the lord of the land, said unto us: Hereby shall I know that ye are upright men: leave one of your brethren with me, and take corn for the famine of your houses, and go your way. {{verse|chapter=42|verse=34}}And bring your youngest brother unto me; then shall I know that ye are no spies, but that ye are upright men; so will I deliver you your brother, and ye shall traffic in the land.' {{verse|chapter=42|verse=35}}And it came to pass as they emptied their sacks, that, behold, every man's bundle of money was in his sack; and when they and their father saw their bundles of money, they were afraid. {{verse|chapter=42|verse=36}}And Jacob their father said unto them: 'Me have ye bereaved of my children: Joseph is not, and Simeon is not, and ye will take Benjamin away; upon me are all these things come.' {{verse|chapter=42|verse=37}}And Reuben spoke unto his father, saying: 'Thou shalt slay my two sons, if I bring him not to thee; deliver him into my hand, and I will bring him back to thee.' {{verse|chapter=42|verse=38}}And he said: 'My son shall not go down with you; for his brother is dead, and he only is left; if harm befall him by the way in which ye go, then will ye bring down my gray hairs with sorrow to the grave.

{{verse|51|59}}The word which Jeremiah the prophet commanded Seraiah the son of Neriah, the son of Mahseiah, when he went with Zedekiah the king of Judah to Babylon in the fourth year of his reign. Now Seraiah was quartermaster. {{verse|51|60}}And Jeremiah wrote in one book all the evil that should come upon Babylon, even all these words that are written concerning Babylon. {{verse|51|61}}And Jeremiah said to Seraiah: 'When thou comest to Babylon, then see that thou read all these words, {{verse|51|62}}and say: O {{sc|Lord}}, Thou hast spoken concerning this place, to cut it off, that none shall dwell therein, neither man nor beast, but that it shall be desolate for ever. {{verse|51|63}}And it shall be, when thou hast made an end of reading this book, that thou shalt bind a stone to it, and cast it into the midst of the Euphrates; {{verse|51|64}}and thou shalt say: Thus shall Babylon sink, and shall not rise again because of the evil that I will bring upon her; and they shall be weary.'


Thus far are the words of Jeremiah.

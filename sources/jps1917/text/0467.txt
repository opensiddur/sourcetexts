{{verse|4|8}}And it fell on a day, that Elisha passed to Shunem, where was a great woman; and she constrained him to eat bread. And so it was, that as oft as he passed by, he turned in thither to eat bread. {{verse|4|9}}And she said unto her husband: 'Behold now, I perceive that this is a holy man of God, that passeth by us continually. {{verse|4|10}}Let us make, I pray thee, a little chamber on the roof; and let us set for him there a bed, and a table, and a stool, and a candlestick; and it shall be, when he cometh to us, that he shall turn in thither.' {{verse|4|11}}And it fell on a day, that he came thither, and he turned into the upper chamber and lay there. {{verse|4|12}}And he said to Gehazi his servant: 'Call this Shunammite.' And when he had called her, she stood before him. {{verse|4|13}}And he said unto him: 'Say now unto her: Behold, thou hast been careful for us with all this care; what is to be done for thee? wouldest thou be spoken for to the king, or to the captain of the host?' And she answered: 'I dwell among mine own people.' {{verse|4|14}}And he said: 'What then is to be done for her?' And Gehazi answered: 'Verily she hath no son, and her husband is old.' {{verse|4|15}}And he said: 'Call her.' And when he had called her, she stood in the door. {{verse|4|16}}And he said: 'At this season, when the time cometh round, thou shalt embrace a son.' And she said: 'Nay, my lord, thou man of God, do not lie unto thy handmaid.' {{verse|4|17}}And the woman conceived, and bore a son at that season, when the time came round, as Elisha had said unto her.

{{verse|4|18}}And when the child was grown, it fell on a day, that he went out to his father to the reapers. {{verse|4|19}}And he said unto his father: 'My head, my head.' And he said to his servant: 'Carry him to his mother.' {{verse|4|20}}And when he had taken him, and brought him to his mother, he sat on her knees till noon, and then died. {{verse|4|21}}And she went up, and laid him on the bed of the man of God, and shut the door upon him, and went out. {{verse|4|22}}And she called unto her husband, and said: 'Send me, I pray thee, one of the servants, and one of the asses, that I may run to the man of God, and come back.' {{verse|4|23}}And he said: 'Wherefore wilt thou go to him to-day? it is neither new moon nor sabbath.' And she said: 'It shall be well.' {{verse|4|24}}Then she saddled an ass, and said to her servant: 'Drive, and go forward; slacken me not the riding, except I bid thee.' {{verse|4|25}}So she went, and came unto the man of God to mount Carmel.

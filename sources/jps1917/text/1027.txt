{{verse|9|11}}On that day the number of those that were slain in Shushan the castle was brought before the king. {{verse|9|12}}And the king said unto Esther the queen: 'The Jews have slain and destroyed five hundred men in Shushan the castle, and the ten sons of Haman; what then have they done in the rest of the king's provinces! Now whatever thy petition, it shall be granted thee; and whatever thy request further, it shall be done.' {{verse|9|13}}Then said Esther: 'If it please the king, let it be granted to the Jews that are in Shushan to do to-morrow also according unto this day's decree, and let Haman's ten sons be hanged upon the gallows.' {{verse|9|14}}And the king commanded it so to be done; and a decree was given out in Shushan; and they hanged Haman's ten sons. {{verse|9|15}}And the Jews that were in Shushan gathered themselves together on the fourteenth day also of the month Adar, and slew three hundred men in Shushan; but on the spoil they laid not their hand.

{{verse|9|16}}And the other Jews that were in the king's provinces gathered themselves together, and stood for their lives, and had rest from their enemies, and slew of them that hated them seventy and five thousand—but on the spoil they laid not their hand—{{verse|9|17}}on the thirteenth day of the month Adar, and on the fourteenth day of the same they rested, and made it a day of feasting and gladness. {{verse|9|18}}But the Jews that were in Shushan assembled together on the thirteenth day thereof, and on the fourteenth thereof; and on the fifteenth day of the same they rested, and made it a day of feasting and gladness. {{verse|9|19}}Therefore do the Jews of the villages, that dwell in the unwalled towns, make the fourteenth day of the month Adar a day of gladness and feasting, and a good day, and of sending portions one to another.

{{verse|9|20}}And Mordecai wrote these things, and sent letters unto all the Jews that were in all the provinces of the king Ahasuerus, both nigh and far, {{verse|9|21}}to enjoin them that they should keep the fourteenth day of the month Adar, and the fifteenth day of the same, yearly, {{verse|9|22}}the days wherein the Jews had rest from their enemies, and the month which was turned unto them from sorrow to gladness, and from mourning into a good day; that they should make them days of feasting and gladness, and of sending portions one to another, and gifts to the poor. {{verse|9|23}}And the Jews took upon them to do as they had begun, and as Mordecai had written unto them; {{verse|9|24}}because Haman the son of Hammedatha, the Agagite, the enemy of all the Jews, had devised against the Jews to destroy them, and had cast pur, that is, the lot, to discomfit them, and to destroy them; {{verse|9|25}}but when <ref>That is, Esther.</ref>she came before the king, he commanded by letters that his wicked device, which he had devised against the Jews, should return upon his own head; and that he and his sons should be hanged on the gallows. {{verse|9|26}}Wherefore they called these days Purim, after the name of pur.

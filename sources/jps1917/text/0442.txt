<noinclude><pagequality level="3" user="Outlier59" />{{rh|{{larger|11}}.9|FIRST KINGS|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{verse|11|9}}And the {{sc|Lord}} was angry with Solomon, because his heart was turned away from the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, who had appeared unto him twice, {{verse|11|10}}and had commanded him concerning this thing, that he should not go after other gods; but he kept not that which the {{sc|Lord}} commanded. {{verse|11|11}}Wherefore the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Solomon: 'Forasmuch as this hath been in thy mind, and thou hast not kept My covenant and My statutes, which I have commanded thee, I will surely rend the kingdom from thee, and will give it to thy servant. {{verse|11|12}}Notwithstanding in thy days I will not do it, for David thy father's sake; but I will rend it out of the hand of thy son. {{verse|11|13}}Howbeit I will not rend away all the kingdom; but I will give one tribe to thy son; for David My servant's sake, and for Jerusalem's sake which I have chosen.'

{{verse|11|14}}And the {{sc|Lord}} raised up an adversary unto Solomon, Hadad the Edomite; he was of the king's seed in Edom. {{verse|11|15}}For it came to pass, when David was in Edom, and Joab the captain of the host was gone up to bury the slain, and had smitten every male in Edom—{{verse|11|16}}for Joab and all Israel remained there six months, until he had cut off every male in Edom—{{verse|11|17}}that <ref>Heb. ''Adad.''</ref>Hadad fled, he and certain Edomites of his father's servants with him, to go into Egypt; Hadad being yet a little child. {{verse|11|18}}And they arose out of Midian, and came to Paran; and they took men with them out of Paran, and they came to Egypt, unto Pharaoh king of Egypt, who gave him a house, and appointed him victuals, and gave him land. {{verse|11|19}}And Hadad found great favour in the sight of Pharaoh, so that he gave him to wife the sister of his own wife, the sister of Tahpenes the queen. {{verse|11|20}}And the sister of Tahpenes bore him Genubath his son, whom Tahpenes weaned in Pharaoh's house; and Genubath was in Pharaoh's house among the sons of Pharaoh. {{verse|11|21}}And when Hadad heard in Egypt that David slept with his fathers, and that Joab the captain of the host was dead, Hadad said to Pharaoh: 'Let me depart, that I may go to mine own country.' {{verse|11|22}}Then Pharaoh said unto him: 'But what hast thou lacked with me, that, behold, thou seekest to go to thine own country?' And he answered: 'Nothing; howbeit let me depart in any wise.'

{{verse|11|23}}And God raised up another adversary unto him, Rezon the son of Eliada, who had fled from his lord Hadadezer king of Zobah. {{verse|11|24}}And he gathered men unto him, and became captain over a troop, when David slew them [of Zobah]; and they went to Damascus, and dwelt therein, and reigned in Damascus. {{verse|11|25}}And he was an adversary to Israel all the days of Solomon, beside the mischief that Hadad did; and he abhorred Israel, and reigned over Aram.

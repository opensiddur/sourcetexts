{{verse|1|17}}So he died according to the word of the {{sc|Lord}} which Elijah had spoken. And Jehoram began to reign in his stead in the second year of Jehoram the son of Jehoshaphat king of Judah; because he had no son. {{verse|1|18}}Now the rest of the acts of Ahaziah which he did, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?


{{dropinitial|{{anchor|2:1}}2}}And it came to pass, when the {{sc|Lord}} would take up Elijah by a whirlwind into heaven, that Elijah went with Elisha from Gilgal. {{verse|2|2}}And Elijah said unto Elisha: 'Tarry here, I pray thee; for the {{sc|Lord}} hath sent me as far as Beth-el.' And Elisha said: 'As the {{sc|Lord}} liveth, and as thy soul liveth, I will not leave thee.' So they went down to Beth-el.—{{verse|2|3}}And the sons of the prophets that were at Beth-el came forth to Elisha, and said unto him: 'Knowest thou that the {{sc|Lord}} will take away thy master from thy head to-day?' And he said: 'Yea, I know it; hold ye your peace.'— {{verse|2|4}}And Elijah said unto him: 'Elisha, tarry here, I pray thee; for the {{sc|Lord}} hath sent me to Jericho.' And he said: 'As the {{sc|Lord}} liveth, and as thy soul liveth, I will not leave thee.' So they came to Jericho.— {{verse|2|5}}And the sons of the prophets that were at Jericho came near to Elisha, and said unto him: 'Knowest thou that the {{sc|Lord}} will take away thy master from thy head to-day?' And he answered: 'Yea, I know it; hold ye your peace.'— {{verse|2|6}}And Elijah said unto him: 'Tarry here, I pray thee; for the {{sc|Lord}} hath sent me to the Jordan.' And he said: 'As the {{sc|Lord}} liveth, and as thy soul liveth, I will not leave thee.' And they two went on.

{{verse|2|7}}And fifty men of the sons of the prophets went, and stood over against them afar off; and they two stood by the Jordan. {{verse|2|8}}And Elijah took his mantle, and wrapped it together, and smote the waters, and they were divided hither and thither, so that they two went over on dry ground. {{verse|2|9}}And it came to pass, when they were gone over, that Elijah said unto Elisha: 'Ask what I shall do for thee, before I am taken from thee.' And Elisha said: 'I pray thee, let a double portion of thy spirit be upon me.' {{verse|2|10}}And he said: 'Thou hast asked a hard thing; nevertheless, if thou see me when I am taken from thee, it shall be so unto thee; but if not, it shall not be so.' {{verse|2|11}}And it came to pass, as they still went on, and talked, that, behold, there appeared a chariot of fire, and horses of fire, which parted them both assunder; and Elijah went up by a whirlwind into heaven.

{{verse|2|12}}And Elisha saw it, and he cried: 'My father, my father, the chariots of Israel and the horsemen thereof! ' And he saw him no more; and he took hold of his own clothes, and rent them in two pieces. {{verse|2|13}}He took up also the mantle of Elijah that fell from him, and went back, and stood by the bank of the Jordan. {{verse|2|14}}And he took the mantle of Elijah that fell from him, and smote the waters, and said: 'Where is the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Elijah?' and when he also had smitten the waters, they were divided hither and thither; and Elisha went over.

:{{verse|20|5|align=float}}That the triumphing of the wicked is short,
:And the joy of the godless but for a moment?
:{{verse|20|6|align=float}}Though his excellency mount up to the heavens,
:And his head reach unto the clouds;
:{{verse|20|7|align=float}}Yet he shall perish for ever like his own dung;
:They that have seen him shall say: 'Where is he?'
:{{verse|20|8|align=float}}He shall fly away as a dream, and shall not be found;

:Neither shall his place any more behold him.
:{{verse|20|10|align=float}}His children shall appease the poor,
:And his hands shall restore his wealth.
:{{verse|20|11|align=float}}His bones are full of his youth,
:But it shall lie down with him in the dust.
:{{verse|20|12|align=float}}Though wickedness be sweet in his mouth,
:Though he hide it under his tongue;
:{{verse|20|13|align=float}}Though he spare it, and will not let it go,

:{{verse|20|14|align=float}}Yet his food in his bowels is turned,

:{{verse|20|15|align=float}}He hath swallowed down riches, and he shall vomit them up again;
:God shall cast them out of his belly.
:{{verse|20|16|align=float}}He shall suck the poison of asps;

:{{verse|20|17|align=float}}He shall not look upon the rivers,

:{{verse|20|18|align=float}}That which he laboured for shall he give back, and shall not swallow it down;

:{{verse|20|21|align=float}}There was nothing left that he devoured not—

:{{verse|20|22|align=float}}In the fulness of his sufficiency he shall be in straits;

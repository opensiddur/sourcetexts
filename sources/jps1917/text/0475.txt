And he said: 'To thee, O captain.' {{verse|9|6}}And he arose, and went into the house; and he poured the oil on his head, and said unto him: 'Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel: I have anointed thee king over the people of the {{sc|Lord}}, even over Israel. {{verse|9|7}}And thou shalt smite the house of Ahab thy master, that I may avenge the blood of My servants the prophets, and the blood of all the servants of the {{sc|Lord}}, at the hand of Jezebel. {{verse|9|8}}For the whole house of Ahab shall perish; and I will cut off from Ahab every man-child, and him that is shut up and him that is left at large in Israel. {{verse|9|9}}And I will make the house of Ahab like the house of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, and like the house of Baasa the son of Ahijah. {{verse|9|10}}And the dogs shall eat Jezebel in the portion of Jezreel, and there shall be none to bury her.' And he opened the door, and fled.

{{verse|9|11}}Then Jehu came forth to the servants of his lord; and one said unto him: 'Is all well? wherefore came this mad fellow to thee?' And he said unto them: 'Ye know the man and what his talk was.' {{verse|9|12}}And they said: 'It is false; tell us now.' And he said: 'Thus and thus spoke he to me, saying: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: I have anointed thee king over Israel.' {{verse|9|13}}Then they hastened, and took every man his garment, and put it under him on the top of the stairs, and blew the horn, saying: 'Jehu is king.'

{{verse|9|14}}So Jehu the son of Jehoshaphat the son of Nimshi conspired against Joram.—Now Joram had been guarding Ramoth-gilead, he and all Israel, because of Hazael king of Aram; {{verse|9|15}}but king <ref>Heb. ''Jehoram'', and in verses [[#9:17|17]], [[#9:21|21]], [[#9:22|22]], [[#9:23|23]], [[#9:24|24]].</ref>Joram was returned to be healed in Jezreel of the wounds which the Arameans had given him, when he fought with Hazael king of Aram.—And Jehu said: 'If this be your mind, then let none escape and go forth out of the city, to go to tell it in Jezreel.' {{verse|9|16}}So Jehu rode in a chariot, and went to Jezreel; for Joram lay there. And Ahaziah king of Judah was come down to see Joram.

{{verse|9|17}}Now the watchman stood on the tower in Jezreel, and he spied the company of Jehu as he came, and said: 'I see a company.' And Joram said: 'Take a horseman, and send to meet them, and let him say: Is it peace?' {{verse|9|18}}So there went one on horseback to meet him, and said: 'Thus saith the king: Is it peace?' And Jehu said: 'What hast thou to do with peace? turn thee behind me.' And the watchman told, saying: 'The messenger came to them, but he cometh not back.' {{verse|9|19}}Then he sent out a second on horseback, who came to them, and said: 'Thus saith the king: Is it peace?' And Jehu answered: 'What hast thou to do with peace? turn thee behind me.' {{verse|9|20}}And the watchman told, saying: 'He came even unto them, and cometh not back; and the driving is like the driving of Jehu the son of Nimshi; for he driveth furiously.'

{{verse|9|21}}And Joram said: 'Make ready.' And they made ready his chariot.

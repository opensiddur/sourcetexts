<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh|{{larger|29}}.6|FIRST CHRONICLES|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{verse|29|6}}Then the princes of the fathers' houses, and the princes of the tribes of Israel, and the captains of thousands and of hundreds, with the rulers over the king's work, offered willingly; {{verse|29|7}}and they gave for the service of the house of God of gold five thousand talents and ten thousand darics, and of silver ten thousand talents, and of brass eighteen thousand talents, and of iron a hundred thousand talents. {{verse|29|8}}And they with whom precious stones were found gave them to the treasure of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, under the hand of Jehiel the Gershonite. {{verse|29|9}}Then the people rejoiced, for that they offered willingly, because with a whole heart they offered willingly to the {{sc|Lord}}; and David the king also rejoiced with great joy.

{{verse|29|10}}Wherefore David blessed the {{sc|Lord}} before all the congregation; and David said: 'Blessed be Thou, O {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel our father, for ever and ever. {{verse|29|11}}Thine, O {{sc|Lord}}, is the greatness, and the power, and the glory, and the victory, and the majesty; for all that is in the heaven and in the earth is Thine; Thine is the kingdom, O {{sc|Lord}}, and Thou art exalted as head above all. {{verse|29|12}}Both riches and honour come of Thee, and Thou rulest over all; and in Thy hand is power and might; and in Thy hand it is to make great, and to give strength unto all. {{verse|29|13}}Now therefore, our God, we thank Thee, and praise Thy glorious name. {{verse|29|14}}But who am I, and what is my people, that we should be able to offer so willingly after this sort? for all things come of Thee, and of Thine own have we given Thee. {{verse|29|15}}For we are strangers before Thee, and sojourners, as all our fathers were: our days on the earth are as a shadow, and there is no abiding. {{verse|29|16}}O {{sc|Lord}} our God, all this store that we have prepared to build Thee a house for Thy holy name cometh of Thy hand, and is all Thine own. {{verse|29|17}}I know also, my God, that Thou triest the heart, and hast pleasure in uprightness. As for me, in the uprightness of my heart I have willingly offered all these things; and now have I seen with joy Thy people, that are present here, offer willingly unto Thee. {{verse|29|18}}O {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Abraham, of Isaac, and of Israel, our fathers, keep this for ever, even the imagination of the thoughts of the heart of Thy people, and direct their heart unto Thee; {{verse|29|19}}and give unto Solomon my son a whole heart, to keep Thy commandments, Thy testimonies, and Thy statutes, and to do all these things, and to build the palace, for which I have made, provision.'

{{verse|29|20}}And David said to all the congregation: 'Now bless the {{sc|Lord}} your God.' And all the congregation blessed the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers, and bowed down their heads, and prostrated themselves before the {{sc|Lord}}, and before the king. {{verse|29|21}}And they sacrificed sacrifices unto the {{sc|Lord}}, and offered burnt-offerings unto the {{sc|Lord}}, on the morrow after that day, even a thousand bullocks, a thousand rams, and a thousand lambs, with their drink-offerings, and sacrifices in abundance for all Israel; {{verse|29|22}}and did eat and drink before the {{sc|Lord}} on that day with great gladness. And they made Solomon the son of David king the second time, and anointed him unto the {{sc|Lord}} to be prince, and Zadok to be priest. {{verse|29|23}}Then Solomon sat on the throne of the {{sc|Lord}} as king instead of David his father, and prospered; and all Israel hearkened to him. {{verse|29|24}}And all the princes, and the mighty men, and all the sons likewise of king David, submitted themselves unto Solomon the king.<noinclude>{{smallrefs}}

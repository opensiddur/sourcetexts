And the people were gathered together after Saul to Gilgal.

{{verse|13|5}}And the Philistines assembled themselves together to fight with Israel, thirty thousand chariots, and six thousand horsemen, and people as the sand which is on the sea-shore in multitude; and they came up, and pitched in Michmas, eastward of Beth-aven. {{verse|13|6}}When the men of Israel saw that they were in a strait—for the people were distressed—then the people did hide themselves in caves, and in thickets, and in rocks, and in holds, and in pits. {{verse|13|7}}Now some of the Hebrews had gone over the Jordan to the land of Gad and Gilead; but as for Saul, he was yet in Gilgal, and all the people followed him trembling.

{{verse|13|8}}And he tarried seven days, according to the set time that Samuel had appointed; but Samuel came not to Gilgal; and the people were scattered from him. {{verse|13|9}}And Saul said: 'Bring hither to me the burnt-offering and the peace-offerings.' And he offered the burnt-offering. {{verse|13|10}}And it came to pass that, as soon as he had made an end of offering the burnt-offering, behold, Samuel came; and Saul went out to meet him, that he might salute him. {{verse|13|11}}And Samuel said: 'What hast thou done?' And Saul said: 'Because I saw that the people were scattered from me, and that thou camest not within the days appointed, and that the Philistines assembled themselves together against Michmas; {{verse|13|12}}therefore said I: Now will the Philistines come down upon me to Gilgal, and I have not entreated the favour of the {{sc|Lord}}; I forced myself therefore, and offered the burnt-offering.' {{verse|13|13}}And Samuel said to Saul: 'Thou hast done foolishly; thou hast not kept the commandment of the {{sc|Lord}} thy God, which He commanded thee; for now would the {{sc|Lord}} have established thy kingdom upon Israel for ever. {{verse|13|14}}But now thy kingdom shall not continue; the {{sc|Lord}} hath sought him a man after His own heart, and the {{sc|Lord}} hath appointed him to be prince over His people, because thou hast not kept that which the {{sc|Lord}} commanded thee.'

{{verse|chapter=3|verse=8}}And we took the land at that time out of the hand of the two kings of the Amorites that were beyond the Jordan, from the valley of Arnon unto mount Hermon—{{verse|chapter=3|verse=9}}which Hermon the Sidonians call Sirion, and the Amorites call it Senir—{{verse|chapter=3|verse=10}}all the cities of the plain, and all Gilead, and all Bashan, unto Salcah and Edrei, cities of the kingdom of Og in Bashan.—{{verse|chapter=3|verse=11}}For only Og king of Bashan remained of the remnant of the Rephaim; behold, his bedstead was a bedstead of iron; is it not in Rabbah of the children of Ammon? nine cubits was the length thereof, and four cubits the breadth of it, after the cubit of a man.—{{verse|chapter=3|verse=12}}And this land we took in possession at that time; from Aroer, which is by the valley of Arnon, and half the hill-country of Gilead, and the cities thereof, gave I unto the Reubenites and to the Gadites; {{verse|chapter=3|verse=13}}and the rest of Gilead, and all Bashan, the kingdom of Og, gave I unto the half-tribe of Manasseh; all the region of Argob—all that Bashan is called the land of Rephaim. {{verse|chapter=3|verse=14}}Jair the son of Manasseh took all the region of Argob, unto the border of the Geshurites and the Maacathites, and called them, even Bashan, after his own name, Havvoth-jair, unto this day.—{{verse|chapter=3|verse=15}}And I gave Gilead unto Machir. {{verse|chapter=3|verse=16}}And unto the Reubenites and unto the Gadites I gave from Gilead even unto the valley of Arnon, the middle of the valley for a border; even unto the river Jabbok, which is the border of the children of Ammon; {{verse|chapter=3|verse=17}}the Arabah also, the Jordan being the border thereof, from Chinnereth even unto the sea of the Arabah, the Salt Sea, under the slopes of Pisgah eastward.

{{verse|chapter=3|verse=18}}And I commanded you at that time, saying: 'The {{sc|Lord}} your God hath given you this land to possess it; ye shall pass over armed before your brethren the children of Israel, all the men of valour. {{verse|chapter=3|verse=19}}But your wives, and your little ones, and your cattle—I know that ye have much cattle—shall abide in your cities which I have given you; {{verse|chapter=3|verse=20}}until the {{sc|Lord}} give rest unto your brethren, as unto you, and they also possess the land which the {{sc|Lord}} your God giveth them beyond the Jordan; then shall ye return every man unto his possession, which I have given you. {{verse|chapter=3|verse=21}}And I commanded Joshua at that time, saying: 'Thine eyes have seen all that the {{sc|Lord}} your God hath done unto these two kings; so shall the {{sc|Lord}} do unto all the kingdoms whither thou goest over. {{verse|chapter=3|verse=22}}Ye shall not fear them; for the {{sc|Lord}} your God, He it is that fighteth for you.'

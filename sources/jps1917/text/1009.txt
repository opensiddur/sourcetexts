{{dropinitial|{{anchor|1:1}}1}} {{sc|The}} words of Koheleth, the son of David, king in Jerusalem.


:{{verse|1|2|align=float}}Vanity of vanities, saith Koheleth;
:Vanity of vanities, all is vanity.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|1|3|align=float}}What profit hath man of all his labour
:Wherein he laboureth under the sun?
:{{verse|1|4|align=float}}One generation passeth away, and another generation cometh;
:And the earth abideth for ever.
:{{verse|1|5|align=float}}The sun also ariseth,
:And the sun goeth down,
:And hasteth to his place where he ariseth.
:{{verse|1|6|align=float}}The wind goeth toward the south,
:And turneth about unto the north;
:It turneth about continually in its circuit,
:And the wind returneth again to its circuits.
:{{verse|1|7|align=float}}All the rivers run into the sea,
:Yet the sea is not full;
:Unto the place whither the rivers go,
:Thither they go again.
:{{verse|1|8|align=float}}All things toil to weariness;
:Man cannot utter it,
:The eye is not satisfied with seeing,
:Nor the ear filled with hearing.
:{{verse|1|9|align=float}}That which hath been is that which shall be,
:And that which hath been done is that which shall be done;
:And there is nothing new under the sun.

{{verse|1|10}}Is there a thing whereof it is said: 'See, this is new'?—it hath been already, in the ages which were before us. {{verse|1|11}}There is no remembrance of them of former times; neither shall there be any remembrance of them of latter times that are to come, among those that shall come after.


{{verse|1|12}}I Koheleth have been king over Israel in Jerusalem. {{verse|1|13}}And I applied my heart to seek and to search out by wisdom concerning all things that are done under heaven; it is a sore task that God hath given to the sons of men to be exercised therewith. {{verse|1|14}}I have seen all the works that are done under the sun; and, behold, all is vanity and a striving after wind.

:{{verse|1|15|align=float}}That which is crooked cannot be made straight;
:And that which is wanting cannot be numbered.

{{verse|1|16}}I spoke with my own heart, saying: 'Lo, I have gotten great wisdom, more also than all that were before me over Jerusalem'; yea, my heart hath had great experience of wisdom and knowledge. {{verse|1|17}}And I applied my heart to know wisdom, and to know madness and folly—I perceived that this also was a striving after wind.

:{{verse|1|18|align=float}}For in much wisdom is much vexation;
:And he that increaseth knowledge increaseth sorrow.

{{verse|13|20}}And it came to pass, as they sat at the table, that the word of the {{sc|Lord}} came unto the prophet that brought him back. {{verse|13|21}}And he cried unto the man of God that came from Judah, saying: 'Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: Forasmuch as thou hast rebelled against the word of the {{sc|Lord}}, and hast not kept the commandment which the {{sc|Lord}} thy God commanded thee, {{verse|13|22}}but camest back, and hast eaten bread and drunk water in the place of which He said to thee: Eat no bread, and drink no water; thy carcass shall not come unto the sepulchre of thy fathers.' {{verse|13|23}}And it came to pass, after he had eaten bread, and after he had drunk, that he saddled for him the ass, namely, for the prophet whom he had brought back. {{verse|13|24}}And when he was gone, a lion met him by the way, and slew him; and his carcass was cast in the way, and the ass stood by it; the lion also stood by the carcass. {{verse|13|25}}And, behold, men passed by, and saw the carcass cast in the way, and the lion standing by the carcass; and they came and told it in the city where the old prophet dwelt.

{{verse|13|26}}And when the prophet that brought him back from the way heard thereof, he said: 'It is the man of God, who rebelled against the word of the {{sc|Lord}}; therefore the {{sc|Lord}} hath delivered him unto the lion, which hath torn him, and slain him, according to the word of the {{sc|Lord}}, which He spoke unto him.' {{verse|13|27}}And he spoke to his sons, saying: 'Saddle me the ass.' And they saddled it. {{verse|13|28}}And he went and found his carcass cast in the way, and the ass and the lion standing by the carcass; the lion had not eaten the carcass, nor torn the ass. {{verse|13|29}}And the prophet took up the carcass of the man of God, and laid it upon the ass, and brought it back; and he came to the city of the old prophet, to lament, and to bury him. {{verse|13|30}}And he laid his carcass in his own grave; and they made lamentation for him: 'Alas, my brother!' {{verse|13|31}}And it came to pass, after he had buried him, that he spoke to his sons, saying: 'When I am dead, then bury me in the sepulchre wherein the man of God is buried; lay my bones beside his bones. {{verse|13|32}}For the saying which he cried by the word of the {{sc|Lord}} against the altar in Beth-el, and against all<noinclude>{{c|424}}</noinclude>

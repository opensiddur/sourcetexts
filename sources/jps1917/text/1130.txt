<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh|{{larger|13}}.18|SECOND CHRONICLES|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>Judah prevailed, because they relied upon the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers. {{verse|13|19}}And Abijah pursued after Jeroboam, and took cities from him, Bethel with the towns thereof, and Jeshanah with the towns thereof, and Ephrain with the towns thereof. {{verse|13|20}}Neither did Jeroboam recover strength again in the days of Abijah; and the {{sc|Lord}} smote him, and he died. {{verse|13|21}}But Abijah waxed mighty, and took unto himself fourteen wives, and begot twenty and two sons, and sixteen daughters. {{verse|13|22}}And the rest of the acts of Abijah, and his ways, and his sayings, are written in the commentary of the prophet Iddo.

{{verse|13|23}}So Abijah slept with his fathers, and they buried him in the city of David, and Asa his son reigned in his stead; in his days the land was quiet ten years. {{dropinitial|14}}{{verse|14|1}}And Asa did that which was good and right in the eyes of the {{sc|Lord}} his God; {{verse|14|2}}for he took away the strange altars, and the high places, and broke down the pillars, and hewed down the Asherim; {{verse|14|3}}and commanded Judah to seek the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers, and to do the law and the commandment. {{verse|14|4}}Also he took away out of all the cities of Judah the high places and the sun-images; and the kingdom was quiet before him. {{verse|14|5}}And he built fortified cities in Judah; for the land was quiet, and he had no war in those years; because the {{sc|Lord}} had given him rest. {{verse|14|6}}For he said unto Judah: 'Let us build these cities, and make about them walls, and towers, gates, and bars; the land is yet before us, because we have sought the {{sc|Lord}} our God; we have sought Him, and He hath given us rest on every side.' So they built and prospered. {{verse|14|7}}And Asa had an army that bore bucklers and spears, out of Judah three hundred thousand; and out of Benjamin, that bore shields and drew bows, two hundred and fourscore thousand; all these were mighty men of valour.

{{verse|14|8}}And there came out against them Zerah the Ethiopian with an army of a thousand thousand, and three hundred chariots; and he came unto Mareshah. {{verse|14|9}}Then Asa went out to meet him, and they set the battle in array in the valley of Zephath at Mareshah. {{verse|14|10}}And Asa cried unto the {{sc|Lord}} his God, and said: '{{sc|Lord}}, there is none beside Thee to help, between the mighty and him that hath no strength; help us, O {{sc|Lord}} our God; for we rely on Thee, and in Thy name are we come against this multitude. Thou art the {{sc|Lord}} our God; let not man prevail against Thee.' {{verse|14|11}}So the {{sc|Lord}} smote the Ethiopians before Asa, and before Judah; and the Ethiopians fled. {{verse|14|12}}And Asa and the people that were with him pursued them unto Gerar; and there fell of the Ethiopians so that none remained alive; for they were shattered before the {{sc|Lord}}, and before His host; and they carried away very much booty. {{verse|14|13}}And they smote all the cities round about Gerar; for a terror from the {{sc|Lord}} came upon them; and they spoiled all the cities; for there was much spoil in them. {{verse|14|14}}They smote also the tents of cattle, and carried away sheep in abundance and camels, and returned to Jerusalem.

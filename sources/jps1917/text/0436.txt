<noinclude><pagequality level="3" user="Outlier59" />{{rh|{{larger|8}}.14|FIRST KINGS|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{verse|8|14}}And the king turned his face about, and blessed all the congregation of Israel; and all the congregation of Israel stood. {{verse|8|15}}And he said: 'Blessed be the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, who spoke with His mouth unto David my father, and hath with His hand fulfilled it, saying: {{verse|8|16}}Since the day that I brought forth My people Israel out of Egypt, I chose no city out of all the tribes of Israel to build a house, that My name might be there; but I chose David to be over My people Israel. {{verse|8|17}}Now it was in the heart of David my father to build a house for the name of the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel. {{verse|8|18}}But the {{sc|Lord}} said unto David my father: Whereas it was in thy heart to build a house for My name, thou didst well that it was in thy heart; {{verse|8|19}}nevertheless thou shalt not build the house; but thy son that shall come forth out of thy loins, he shall build the house for My name. {{verse|8|20}}And the {{sc|Lord}} hath established His word that He spoke; for I am risen up in the room of David my father, and sit on the throne of Israel, as the {{sc|Lord}} promised, and have built the house for the name of the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel. {{verse|8|21}}And there have I set a place for the ark, wherein is the covenant of the {{sc|Lord}}, which He made with our fathers, when He brought them out of the land of Egypt.'

{{verse|8|22}}And Solomon stood before the altar of the {{sc|Lord}} in the presence of all the congregation of Israel, and spread forth his hands toward heaven; {{verse|8|23}}and he said: 'O {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, there is no God like Thee, in heaven above, or on earth beneath; who keepest covenant and mercy with Thy servants, that walk before Thee with all their heart; {{verse|8|24}}who hast kept with Thy servant David my father that which Thou didst promise him; yea, Thou spokest with Thy mouth, and hast fulfilled it with Thy hand, as it is this day. {{verse|8|25}}Now therefore, O {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, keep with Thy servant David my father that which Thou hast promised him saying: There shall not fail thee a man in My sight to sit on the throne of Israel, if only thy children take heed to their way, to walk before Me as thou hast walked before Me. {{verse|8|26}}Now therefore, O God of Israel, let Thy word, I pray Thee, be verified, which Thou didst speak unto Thy servant David my father.

{{verse|8|27}}But will God in very truth dwell on the earth? behold, heaven and the heaven of heavens cannot contain Thee; how much less this house that I have builded! {{verse|8|28}}Yet have Thou respect unto the prayer of Thy servant, and to his supplication, O {{sc|Lord}} my God, to hearken unto the cry and to the prayer which Thy servant prayeth before Thee this day; {{verse|8|29}}that Thine eyes may be open toward this house night and day, even toward the place whereof Thou hast said: My name shall be there; to hearken unto the prayer which Thy servant shall pray toward this place. {{verse|8|30}}And hearken Thou to the supplication of Thy servant, and of Thy people Israel, when they shall pray toward this place; yea, hear Thou in heaven Thy dwelling-place; and when Thou hearest, forgive.

{{verse|8|31}}If a man sin against his neighbour, and an oath be exacted of him to cause him to swear, and he come and swear before Thine altar in this house; {{verse|8|32}}then hear Thou in heaven, and do, and judge Thy servants, condemning the wicked, to bring his way upon his own head; and justifying the righteous, to give him according to his righteousness.

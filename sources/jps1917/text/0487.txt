<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh||SECOND KINGS|{{larger|17}}.34}}{{rule}}</noinclude>wherewith He testified against them; and they went after things of nought, and became nought, and after the nations that were round about them, concerning whom the {{sc|Lord}} had charged them that they should not do like them; {{verse|17|16}}and they forsook all the commandments of the {{sc|Lord}} their God, and made them molten images, even two calves, and made an Asherah, and worshipped all the host of heaven, and served Baal; {{verse|17|17}}and they caused their sons and their daughters to pass through the fire, and used divination and enchantments, and gave themselves over to do that which was evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}}, to provoke Him; {{verse|17|18}}that the {{sc|Lord}} was very angry with Israel, and removed them out of His sight; there was none left but the tribe of Judah only. {{verse|17|19}}Also Judah kept not the commandments of the {{sc|Lord}} their God, but walked in the statutes of Israel which they practised. {{verse|17|20}}And the {{sc|Lord}} rejected all the seed of Israel, and afflicted them, and delivered them into the hand of spoilers, until He had cast them out of His sight. {{verse|17|21}}For He rent Israel from the house of David; and they made Jeroboam the son of Nebat king; and Jeroboam drew Israel away from following the {{sc|Lord}}, and made them sin a great sin. {{verse|17|22}}And the children of Israel walked in all the sins of Jeroboam which he did; they departed not from them; {{verse|17|23}}until the {{sc|Lord}} removed Israel out of His sight, as He spoke by the hand of all His servants the prophets. So Israel was carried away out of their own land to Assyria, unto this day.

{{verse|17|24}}And the king of Assyria brought men from Babylon, and from Cuthah, and from Avva, and from Hamath and Sepharvaim, and placed them in the cities of Samaria instead of the children of Israel; and they possessed Samaria, and dwelt in the cities thereof. {{verse|17|25}}And so it was, at the beginning of their dwelling there, that they feared not the {{sc|Lord}}; therefore the {{sc|Lord}} sent lions among them, which killed some of them. {{verse|17|26}}Wherefore they spoke to the king of Assyria, saying: 'The nations which thou hast carried away, and placed in the cities of Samaria, know not the manner of the God of the land; therefore He hath sent lions among them, and, behold, they slay them, because they know not the manner of the God of the land.'

{{verse|17|27}}Then the king of Assyria commanded, saying: 'Carry thither one of the priests whom ye brought from thence; and let them go and dwell there, and let him teach them the manner of the God of the land.' {{verse|17|28}}So one of the priests whom they had carried away from Samaria came and dwelt in Beth-el, and taught them how they should fear the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|17|29}}Howbeit every nation made gods of their own, and put them in the houses of the high places which the Samaritans had made, every nation in their cities wherein they dwelt. {{verse|17|30}}And the men of Babylon made Succoth-benoth, and the men of Cuth made Nergal, and the men of Hamath made Ashima, {{verse|17|31}}and the Avvites made Nibhaz and Tartak, and the Sepharvites burnt their children in the fire to Adrammelech and Anammelech, the gods of Sepharvaim. {{verse|17|32}}So they feared the {{sc|Lord}}, and made unto them from among themselves priests of the high places, who sacrificed for them in the houses of the high places. {{verse|17|33}}They feared the {{sc|Lord}}, and served their own gods, after the manner of the nations from among whom they had been carried away.

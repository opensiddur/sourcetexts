{{verse|6|12}}And it was told king David, saying: 'The {{sc|Lord}} hath blessed the house of Obed-edom, and all that pertaineth unto him, because of the ark of God.' And David went and brought up the ark of God from the house of Obed-edom into the city of David with joy. {{verse|6|13}}And it was so, that when they that bore the ark of the {{sc|Lord}} had gone six paces, he sacrificed an ox and a fatling. {{verse|6|14}}And David danced before the {{sc|Lord}} with all his might; and David was girded with a linen ephod. {{verse|6|15}}So David and all the house of Israel brought up the ark of the {{sc|Lord}} with shouting, and with the sound of the horn.

{{verse|6|16}}And it was so, as the ark of the {{sc|Lord}} came into the city of David, that Michal the daughter of Saul looked out at the window, and saw king David leaping and dancing before the {{sc|Lord}}; and she despised him in her heart. {{verse|6|17}}And they brought in the ark of the {{sc|Lord}}, and set it in its place, in the midst of the tent that David had pitched for it; and David offered burnt-offerings and peace-offerings before the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|6|18}}And when David had made an end of offering the burnt-offering and the peace-offerings, he blessed the people in the name of the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts. {{verse|6|19}}And he dealt among all the people, even among the whole multitude of Israel, both to men and women, to every one a cake of bread, and a cake made in a pan, and a sweet cake. So all the people departed every one to his house.

{{verse|6|20}}Then David returned to bless his household. And Michal the daughter of Saul came out to meet David, and said: 'How did the king of Israel get him honour to-day, who uncovered himself to-day in the eyes of the handmaids of his servants, as one of the vain fellows shamelessly uncovereth himself!' {{verse|6|21}}And David said unto Michal: 'Before the {{sc|Lord}}, who chose me above thy father, and above all his house, to appoint me prince over the people of the {{sc|Lord}}, over Israel, before the {{sc|Lord}} will I make merry. {{verse|6|22}}And I will be yet more vile than thus, and will be base in mine own sight; and with the handmaids whom thou hast spoken of, with them will I get me honour.' {{verse|6|23}}And Michal the daughter of Saul had no child unto the day of her death.

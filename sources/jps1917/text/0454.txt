And they danced in halting wise about the altar which was made. {{verse|18|27}}And it came to pass at noon, that Elijah mocked them, and said: 'Cry aloud; for he is a god; either he is musing, or he is gone aside, or he is in a journey, or peradventure he sleepeth, and must be awaked.' {{verse|18|28}}And they cried aloud, and cut themselves after their manner with swords and lances, till the blood gushed out upon them. {{verse|18|29}}And it was so, when midday was past, that they prophesied until the time of the offering of the evening offering; but their was neither voice, nor any to answer, nor any that regarded.

{{verse|18|30}}And Elijah said unto all the people: 'Come near unto me'; and all the people came near unto him. And he repaired the altar of the {{sc|Lord}} that was thrown down. {{verse|18|31}}And Elijah took twelve stones, according to the number of the tribes of the sons of Jacob, unto whom the word of the {{sc|Lord}} came, saying: 'Israel shall be thy name.' {{verse|18|32}}And with the stones he built an altar in the name of the {{sc|Lord}}; and he made a trench about the altar, as great as would contain two measures of seed. {{verse|18|33}}And he put the wood in order, and cut the bullock in pieces, and laid it on the wood. {{verse|18|34}}And he said: 'Fill four jars with water, and pour it on the burnt-offering, and on the wood.' And he said: 'Do it the second time'; and they did it the second time. And he said: 'Do it the third time'; and they did it the third time. {{verse|18|35}}And the water ran round about the altar; and he filled the trench also with water. {{verse|18|36}}And it came to pass at the time of the offering of the evening offering, that Elijah the prophet came near, and said: 'O {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Abraham, of Isaac, and of Israel, let it be known this day that Thou art God in Israel, and that I am Thy servant, and that I have done all these things at Thy word. {{verse|18|37}}Hear me, O {{sc|Lord}}, hear me, that this people may know that Thou, {{sc|Lord}}, art God, for Thou didst turn their heart backward.' {{verse|18|38}}Then the fire of the {{sc|Lord}} fell, and consumed the burnt-offering, and the wood, and the stones, and the dust, and licked up the water that was in the trench. {{verse|18|39}}And when all the people saw it, they fell on their faces; and they said: 'The {{sc|Lord}}, He is God; the {{sc|Lord}}, He is God.' {{verse|18|40}}And Elijah said unto them: 'Take the prophets of Baal; let not one of them escape.' And they took them; and Elijah brought them down to the brook Kishon, and slew them there.

{{verse|18|41}}And Elijah said unto Ahab: 'Get thee up, eat and drink; for there is the sound of abundance of rain.' {{verse|18|42}}So Ahab went up to eat and to drink. And Elijah went up to the top of Carmel; and he bowed himself down upon the earth, and put his face between his knees. {{verse|18|43}}And he said to his servant: 'Go up now, look toward the sea.' And he went up, and looked, and said: 'There is nothing.' And he said: 'Go again seven times.' {{verse|18|44}}And it came to pass at the seventh time, that he said: 'Behold, there ariseth a cloud out of the sea, as small as a man's hand.'

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|8:1}}8}}And it came to pass, when Samuel was old, that he made his sons judges over Israel. {{verse|8|2}}Now the name of his first-born was Joel; and the name of his second, Abijah; they were judges in Beer-sheba. {{verse|8|3}}And his sons walked not in his ways, but turned aside after lucre, and took bribes, and perverted justice.

{{verse|8|4}}Then all the elders of Israel gathered themselves together, and came to Samuel unto Ramah. {{verse|8|5}}And they said unto him: 'Behold, thou art old, and thy sons walk not in thy ways; now make us a king to judge us like all the nations.' {{verse|8|6}}But the thing displeased Samuel, when they said: 'Give us a king to judge us.' And Samuel prayed unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|8|7}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Samuel: 'Hearken unto the voice of the people in all that they say unto thee; for they have not rejected thee, but they have rejected Me, that I should not be king over them. {{verse|8|8}}According to all the works which they have done since the day that I brought them up out of Egypt even unto this day, in that they have forsaken Me, and served other gods, so do they also unto thee. {{verse|8|9}}Now therefore hearken unto their voice; howbeit thou shalt earnestly forewarn them, and shalt declare unto them the manner of the king that shall reign over them.'

{{verse|8|10}}And Samuel told all the words of the {{sc|Lord}} unto the people that asked of him a king. {{verse|8|11}}And he said: 'This will be the manner of the king that shall reign over you: he will take your sons, and appoint them unto him, for his chariots, and to be his horsemen; and they shall run before his chariots. {{verse|8|12}}And he will appoint them unto him for captains of thousands, and captains of fifties; and to plow his ground, and to reap his harvest, and to make his instruments of war, and the instruments of his chariots. {{verse|8|13}}And he will take your daughters to be perfumers, and to be cooks, and to be bakers. {{verse|8|14}}And he will take your fields, and your vineyards, and your oliveyards, even the best of them, and give them to his servants. {{verse|8|15}}And he will take the tenth of your seed, and of your vineyards, and give to his officers, and to his servants. {{verse|8|16}}And he will take your men-servants, and your maid-servants, and your goodliest young men, and your asses, and put them to his work. {{verse|8|17}}He will take the tenth of your flocks; and ye shall be his servants. {{verse|8|18}}And ye shall cry out in that day because of your king whom ye shall have chosen you; and the {{sc|Lord}} will not answer you in that day.' {{verse|8|19}}But the people refused to hearken unto the voice of Samuel; and they said: 'Nay; but there shall be a king over us; {{verse|8|20}}that we also may be like all the nations; and that our king may judge us, and go out before us, and fight our battles.' {{verse|8|21}}And Samuel heard all the words of the people, and he spoke them in the ears of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|8|22}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said to Samuel: 'Hearken unto their voice, and make them a king.' And Samuel said unto the men of Israel: 'Go ye every man unto his city.'

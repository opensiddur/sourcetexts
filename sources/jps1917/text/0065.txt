{{dropinitial|{{anchor|35:1}}35}}And God said unto Jacob: 'Arise, go up to Beth-el, and dwell there; and make there an altar unto God, who appeared unto thee when thou didst flee from the face of Esau thy brother.' {{verse|chapter=35|verse=2}}Then Jacob said unto his household, and to all that were with him: 'Put away the strange gods that are among you, and purify yourselves, and change your garments; {{verse|chapter=35|verse=3}}and let us arise, and go up to Beth-el; and I will make there an altar unto God, who answered me in the day of my distress, and was with me in the way which I went.' {{verse|chapter=35|verse=4}}And they gave unto Jacob all the foreign gods which were in their hand, and the rings which were in their ears; and Jacob hid them under the terebinth which was by Shechem. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=5}}And they journeyed; and a terror of God was upon the cities that were round about them, and they did not pursue after the sons of Jacob. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=6}}So Jacob came to Luz, which is in the land of Canaan—the same is Beth-el—he and all the people that were with him. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=7}}And he built there an altar, and called the place <ref>That is, ''The God of Beth-el.''</ref>El-beth-el, because there God was revealed unto him, when he fled from the face of his brother. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=8}}And Deborah Rebekah's nurse died, and she was buried below Beth-el under the oak; and the name of it was called <ref>That is, ''The oak of weeping.''</ref>Allon-bacuth.

Be fruitful and multiply; a nation and a company of nations shall be of thee, and kings shall come out of thy loins; {{verse|chapter=35|verse=12}}and the land which I gave unto Abraham and Isaac, to thee I will give it, and to thy seed after thee will I give the land.' {{verse|chapter=35|verse=13}}And God went up from him in the place where He spoke with him. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=14}}And Jacob set up a pillar in the place where He spoke with him, a pillar of stone, and he poured out a drink-offering thereon, and poured oil thereon. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=15}}And Jacob called the name of the place where God spoke with him, Beth-el. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=16}}And they journeyed from Beth-el; and there was still some way to come to Ephrath; and Rachel travailed, and she had hard labour. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=17}}And it came to pass, when she was in hard labour, that the midwife said unto her: 'Fear not; for this also is a son for thee.' {{verse|chapter=35|verse=18}}And it came to pass, as her soul was in departing—for she died—that she called his name <ref>That is, ''The son of my sorrow.''</ref>Ben-oni; but his father called him <ref>That is, ''The son of the right hand.''</ref>Benjamin. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=19}}And Rachel died, and was buried in the way to Ephrath—the same is Beth-lehem. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=20}}And Jacob set up a pillar upon her grave; the same is the pillar of Rachel's grave unto this day. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=21}}And Israel journeyed, and spread his tent beyond Migdal-eder. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=22}}And it came to pass, while Israel dwelt in that land, that Reuben went and lay with Bilhah his father's concubine; and Israel heard of it.

<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh||FIRST CHRONICLES|{{larger|17}}.27}}{{rule}}</noinclude>Why have ye not built Me a house of cedar? {{verse|17|7}}Now therefore thus shalt thou say unto My servant David: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts: I took thee from the sheepcote, from following the sheep, that thou shouldest be prince over My people Israel; {{verse|17|8}}and I have been with thee whithersoever thou wentest, and have cut off all thine enemies from before thee; and I will make thee a name, like unto the name of the great ones that are in the earth. {{verse|17|9}}And I will appoint a place for My people Israel, and will plant them, that they may dwell in their own place, and be disquieted no more; neither shall the children of wickedness waste them any more, as at the first, {{verse|17|10}}even from the day that I commanded judges to be over My people Israel; and I will subdue all thine enemies. Moreover I tell thee that the {{sc|Lord}} will build thee a house. {{verse|17|11}}And it shall come to pass, when thy days are fulfilled that thou must go to be with thy fathers, that I will set up thy seed after thee, who shall be of thy sons; and I will establish his kingdom. {{verse|17|12}}He shall build Me a house, and I will establish his throne for ever. {{verse|17|13}}I will be to him for a father, and he shall be to Me for a son; and I will not take My mercy away from him, as I took it from him that was before thee; {{verse|17|14}}but I will settle him in My house and in My kingdom for ever; and his throne shall be established for ever.' {{verse|17|15}}According to all these words, and according to all this vision, so did Nathan speak unto David.

{{verse|17|16}}Then David the king went in, and sat before the {{sc|Lord}}; and he said: 'Who am I, O {{sc|Lord}} God, and what is my house, that Thou hast brought me thus far? {{verse|17|17}}And this was a small thing in Thine eyes, O God; but Thou hast spoken of Thy servant's house for a great while to come, and hast regarded me after the manner of a man of high degree, O {{sc|Lord}} God. {{verse|17|18}}What can David say yet more unto Thee concerning the honour which is done to Thy servant? for Thou knowest Thy servant. {{verse|17|19}}O {{sc|Lord}}, for Thy servant's sake, and according to Thine own heart, hast Thou wrought all this greatness, to make known all these great things. {{verse|17|20}}O {{sc|Lord}}, there is none like Thee, neither is there any God beside Thee, according to all that we have heard with our ears. {{verse|17|21}}And who is like Thy people Israel, a nation one in the earth, whom God went to redeem unto Himself for a people, to make Thee a name by great and tremendous things, in driving out nations from before Thy people, whom Thou didst redeem out of Egypt. {{verse|17|22}}For Thy people Israel didst Thou make Thine own people for ever; and Thou, {{sc|Lord}}, becamest their God. {{verse|17|23}}And now, O {{sc|Lord}}, let the word that Thou hast spoken concerning Thy servant, and concerning his house, be established for ever, and do as Thou hast spoken. {{verse|17|24}}Yea, let it be established, and let Thy name be magnified for ever, that it may be said: The {{sc|Lord}} of hosts is the God of Israel, even a God to Israel; and the house of David Thy servant shall be established before Thee. {{verse|17|25}}For Thou, O my God, hast revealed to Thy servant that Thou wilt build him a house; therefore hath Thy servant taken heart to pray before Thee. {{verse|17|26}}And now, O {{sc|Lord}}, Thou alone art God, and hast promised this good thing unto Thy servant; {{verse|17|27}}and now it hath pleased Thee to bless the house of Thy servant, that it may continue for ever before Thee; for Thou, O {{sc|Lord}}, hast blessed, and so let [Thy servant] be blessed for ever.'

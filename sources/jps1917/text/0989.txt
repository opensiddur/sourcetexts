:So is my beloved among the sons.
:Under its shadow I delighted to sit,
:And its fruit was sweet to my taste.
:{{verse|2|4|align=float}}He hath brought me to the banqueting-house,
:And his banner over me is love.
:{{verse|2|5|align=float}}'Stay ye me with dainties, refresh me with apples;
:For I am love-sick.'
:{{verse|2|6|align=float}}Let his left hand be under my head,
:And his right hand embrace me.
:{{verse|2|7|align=float}}'I adjure you, O daughters of Jerusalem,
:By the gazelles, and by the hinds of the field,
:That ye awaken not, nor stir up love, until it please.'
:{{verse|2|8|align=float}}Hark! my beloved! behold, he cometh,
:Leaping upon the mountains, skipping upon the hills.
:{{verse|2|9|align=float}}My beloved is like a gazelle or a young hart;
:Behold, he standeth behind our wall,
:He looketh in through the windows,
:He peereth through the lattice.
:{{verse|2|10|align=float}}My beloved spoke, and said unto me:
:'Rise up, my love, my fair one, and come away.
:{{verse|2|11|align=float}}For, lo, the winter is past,
:The rain is over and gone;
:{{verse|2|12|align=float}}The flowers appear on the earth;
:The time of singing is come,
:And the voice of the turtle is heard in our land;
:{{verse|2|13|align=float}}The fig-tree putteth forth her green figs,
:And the vines in blossom give forth their fragrance.
:Arise, my love, my fair one, and come away.
:{{verse|2|14|align=float}}O my dove, that art in the clefts of the rock, in the covert of the cliff,
:Let me see thy countenance, let me hear thy voice;
:For sweet is thy voice, and thy countenance is comely.'
:{{verse|2|15|align=float}}'Take us the foxes, the little foxes, that spoil the vineyards;
:For our vineyards are in blossom.'
:{{verse|2|16|align=float}}My beloved is mine, and I am his,
:That feedeth among the lilies.
:{{verse|2|17|align=float}}Until the day breathe, and the shadows flee away,
:Turn, my beloved, and be thou like a gazelle or a young hart
:Upon the mountains of spices.

<noinclude><pagequality level="3" user="Outlier59" />{{rh||LEVITICUS|{{larger|27}}.26}}{{rule}}</noinclude>from twenty years old even unto sixty years old, even thy valuation shall be fifty shekels of silver, after the shekel of the sanctuary. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=4}}And if it be a female, then thy valuation shall be thirty shekels. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=5}}And if it be from five years old even unto twenty years old, then thy valuation shall be for the male twenty shekels, and for the female ten shekels. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=6}}And if it be from a month old even unto five years old, then thy valuation shall be for the male five shekels of silver, and for the female thy valuation shall be three shekels of silver. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=7}}And if it be from sixty years old and upward: if it be a male, then thy valuation shall be fifteen shekels, and for the female ten shekels. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=8}}But if he be too poor for thy valuation, then he shall be set before the priest, and the priest shall value him; according to the means of him that vowed shall the priest value him.

{{verse|chapter=27|verse=9}}And if it be a beast, whereof men bring an offering unto the {{sc|Lord}}, all that any man giveth of such unto the {{sc|Lord}} shall be holy. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=10}}He shall not alter it, nor change it, a good for a bad, or a bad for a good; and if he shall at all change beast for beast, then both it and that for which it is changed shall be holy. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=11}}And if it be any unclean beast, of which they may not bring an offering unto the {{sc|Lord}}, then he shall set the beast before the priest. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=12}}And the priest shall value it, whether it be good or bad; as thou the priest valuest it, so shall it be. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=13}}But if he will indeed redeem it, then he shall add the fifth part thereof unto thy valuation.

{{verse|chapter=27|verse=14}}And when a man shall sanctify his house to be holy unto the {{sc|Lord}}, then the priest shall value it, whether it be good or bad; as the priest shall value it, so shall it stand. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=15}}And if he that sanctified it will redeem his house, then he shall add the fifth part of the money of thy valuation unto it, and it shall be his.

{{verse|chapter=27|verse=16}}And if a man shall sanctify unto the {{sc|Lord}} part of the field of his possession, then thy valuation shall be according to the sowing thereof; the sowing of a homer of barley shall be valued at fifty shekels of silver. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=17}}If he sanctify his field from the year of jubilee, according to thy valuation it shall stand. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=18}}But if he sanctify his field after the jubilee, then the priest shall reckon unto him the money according to the years that remain unto the year of jubilee, and an abatement shall be made from thy valuation. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=19}}And if he that sanctified the field will indeed redeem it, then he shall add the fifth part of the money of thy valuation unto it, and it shall be assured to him. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=20}}And if he will not redeem the field, or if he have sold the field to another man, it shall not be redeemed any more. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=21}}But the field, when it goeth out in the jubilee, shall be holy unto the {{sc|Lord}}, as a field devoted; the possession thereof shall be the priest's. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=22}}And if he sanctify unto the {{sc|Lord}} a field which he hath bought, which is not of the field of his possession; {{verse|chapter=27|verse=23}}then the priest shall reckon unto him the worth of thy valuation unto the year of jubilee; and he shall give thy valuation in that day, as a holy thing unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=24}}In the year of jubilee the field shall return unto him of whom it was bought, even to him to whom the possession of the land belongeth. {{verse|chapter=27|verse=25}}And all thy valuations shall be according to the shekel of the sanctuary; twenty gerahs shall be the shekel.

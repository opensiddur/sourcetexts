<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh|{{larger|9}}.20|SECOND CHRONICLES|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>of the house of the forest of Lebanon were of pure gold; silver was nothing accounted of in the days of Solomon. {{verse|9|21}}For the king had ships that went to Tarshish with the servants of Huram; once every three years came the ships of Tarshish, bringing gold, and silver, ivory, and apes, and peacocks.

{{verse|9|22}}So king Solomon exceeded all the kings of the earth in riches and wisdom. {{verse|9|23}}And all the kings of the earth sought the presence of Solomon, to hear his wisdom, which God had put in his heart. {{verse|9|24}}And they brought every man his present, vessels of silver, and vessels of gold, and raiment, armour, and spices, horses, and mules, a rate year by year.

{{verse|9|25}}And Solomon had four thousand stalls for horses and chariots, and twelve thousand horsemen, that he bestowed in the chariot cities, and with the king at Jerusalem. {{verse|9|26}}And he ruled over all the kings from the River even unto the land of the Philistines, and to the border of Egypt. {{verse|9|27}}And the king made silver to be in Jerusalem as stones, and cedars made he to be as the sycamore-trees that are in the Lowland, for abundance. {{verse|9|28}}And they brought horses for Solomon out of Egypt, and out of all lands.

{{verse|9|29}}Now the rest of the acts of Solomon, first and last, are they not written in the words of Nathan the prophet, and in the prophecy of Ahijah the Shilonite, and in the visions of Jedo the seer concerning Jeroboam the son of Nebat? {{verse|9|30}}And Solomon reigned in Jerusalem over all Israel forty years. {{verse|9|31}}And Solomon slept with his fathers; they buried him in the city of David his father; and Rehoboam his son reigned in his stead.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|10:1}}10}} And Rehoboam went to Shechem; for all Israel were come to Shechem to make him king. {{verse|10|2}}And it came to pass, when Jeroboam the son of Nebat heard of it—for he was in Egypt, whither he had fled from the presence of king Solomon—that Jeroboam returned out of Egypt. {{verse|10|3}}And they sent and called him; and Jeroboam and all Israel came, and they spoke to Rehoboam, saying: {{verse|10|4}}'Thy father made our yoke grievous; now therefore make thou the grievous service of thy father, and his heavy yoke which he put upon us, lighter, and we will serve thee.' {{verse|10|5}}And he said unto them: 'Come again unto me after three days.' And the people departed.

{{verse|10|6}}And king Rehoboam took counsel with the old men, that had stood before Solomon his father while he yet lived, saying: 'What counsel give ye me to return answer to this people?' {{verse|10|7}}And they spoke unto him, saying: 'If thou be kind to this people, and please them, and speak good words to them, then they will be thy servants for ever.' {{verse|10|8}}But he forsook the counsel of the old men which they had given him, and took counsel with the young men that were grown up with him, that stood before him. {{verse|10|9}}And he said unto them: 'What counsel give ye, that we may return answer to this people, who have spoken to me, saying: Make the yoke that thy father did put upon us lighter?' {{verse|10|10}}And the young men that were grown up with him spoke unto him, saying: 'Thus shalt thou say unto the people that spoke unto thee, saying: Thy father made our yoke heavy, but make thou it lighter unto us; thus shalt thou say unto them: My little finger is thicker than my father's loins. {{verse|10|11}}And now whereas my father did lade you with a heavy yoke, I will add to your yoke; my father chastised you with whips, but I will chastise you with scorpions.'

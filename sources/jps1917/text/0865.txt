{{dropinitial|{{anchor|92:1}}92}} A Psalm, a Song. For the sabbath day.


:{{verse|92|2|align=float}}It is a good thing to give thanks unto the {{sc|Lord}},
:And to sing praises unto Thy name, O Most High;
:{{verse|92|3|align=float}}To declare Thy lovingkindness in the morning,
:And Thy faithfulness in the night seasons,
:{{verse|92|4|align=float}}With an instrument of ten strings, and with the psaltery;
:With a solemn sound upon the harp.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|92|5|align=float}}For Thou, {{sc|Lord}}, hast made me glad through Thy work;
:I will exult in the works of Thy hands.
:{{verse|92|6|align=float}}How great are Thy works, O {{sc|Lord}}!
:Thy thoughts are very deep.
:{{verse|92|7|align=float}}A brutish man knoweth not,
:Neither doth a fool understand this.
:{{verse|92|8|align=float}}When the wicked spring up as the grass,
:And when all the workers of iniquity do flourish;
:It is that they may be destroyed for ever.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|92|9|align=float}}But Thou, O {{sc|Lord}}, art on high for evermore.
:{{verse|92|10|align=float}}For, lo, Thine enemies, O {{sc|Lord}},
:For, lo, Thine enemies shall perish:
:All the workers of iniquity shall be scattered.
:{{verse|92|11|align=float}}But my horn hast Thou exalted like the horn of the wild-ox;
:I am anointed with rich oil.
:{{verse|92|12|align=float}}Mine eye also hath gazed on them that lie in wait for me,
:Mine ears have heard my desire of the evil-doers that rise up against me.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|92|13|align=float}}The righteous shall flourish like the palm-tree;
:He shall grow like a cedar in Lebanon.
:{{verse|92|14|align=float}}Planted in the house of the {{sc|Lord}},
:They shall flourish in the courts of our God.
:{{verse|92|15|align=float}}They shall still bring forth fruit in old age;
:They shall be full of sap and richness;
:{{verse|92|16|align=float}}To declare that the {{sc|Lord}} is upright,
:My Rock, in whom there is no unrighteousness.

{{verse|chapter=35|verse=30}}And Moses said unto the children of Israel: 'See, the {{sc|Lord}} hath called by name Bezalel the son of Uri, the son of Hur, of the tribe of Judah. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=31}}And He hath filled him with the spirit of God, in wisdom, in understanding, and in knowledge, and in all manner of workmanship. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=32}}And to devise skilful works, to work in gold, and in silver, and in brass, {{verse|chapter=35|verse=33}}and in cutting of stones for setting, and in carving of wood, to work in all manner of skilful workmanship. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=34}}And He hath put in his heart that he may teach, both he, and Oholiab, the son of Ahisamach, of the tribe of Dan. {{verse|chapter=35|verse=35}}Them hath He filled with wisdom of heart, to work all manner of workmanship, of the craftsman, and of the skilful workman, and of the weaver in colours, in blue, and in purple, in scarlet, and in fine linen, and of the weaver, even of them that do any workmanship, and of those that devise skilful works. {{dropinitial|{{anchor|36:1}}36}}And Bezalel and Oholiab shall work, and every wise-hearted man, in whom the {{sc|Lord}} hath put wisdom and understanding to know how to work all the work for the service of the sanctuary, according to all that the {{sc|Lord}} hath commanded.'

{{verse|chapter=36|verse=2}}And Moses called Bezalel and Oholiab, and every wise-hearted man, in whose heart the {{sc|Lord}} had put wisdom, even every one whose heart stirred him up to come unto the work to do it. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=3}}And they received of Moses all the offering, which the children of Israel had brought for the work of the service of the sanctuary, wherewith to make it. And they brought yet unto him freewill-offerings every morning. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=4}}And all the wise men, that wrought all the work of the sanctuary, came every man from his work which they wrought. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=5}}And they spoke unto Moses, saying: 'The people bring much more than enough for the service of the work, which the {{sc|Lord}} commanded to make.' {{verse|chapter=36|verse=6}}And Moses gave commandment, and they caused it to be proclaimed throughout the camp, saying: 'Let neither man nor woman make any more work for the offering of the sanctuary.' So the people were restrained from bringing. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=7}}For the stuff they had was sufficient for all the work to make it, and too much.

{{verse|chapter=36|verse=8}}And every wise-hearted man among them that wrought the work made the tabernacle with ten curtains: of fine twined linen, and blue, and purple, and scarlet, with cherubim the work of the skilful workman made he them. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=9}}The length of each curtain was eight and twenty cubits, and the breadth of each curtain four cubits; all the curtains had one measure. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=10}}And he coupled five curtains one to another; and the other five curtains he coupled one to another. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=11}}And he made loops of blue upon the edge of the one curtain that was outmost in the first set; likewise he made in the edge of the curtain that was outmost in the second set. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=12}}Fifty loops made he in the one curtain, and fifty loops made he in the edge of the curtain that was in the second set; the loops were opposite one to another. {{verse|chapter=36|verse=13}}And he made fifty clasps of gold, and coupled the curtains one to another with the clasps; so the tabernacle was one.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|22:1}}22}}And they continued three years without war between Aram and Israel. {{verse|22|2}}And it came to pass in the third year, that Jehoshaphat the king of Judah came down to the king of Israel. {{verse|22|3}}And the king of Israel said unto his servants: 'Know ye that Ramoth-gilead is ours, and we are still, and take it not out of the hand of the king of Aram?' {{verse|22|4}}And he said unto Jehoshaphat: 'Wilt thou go with me to battle to Ramoth-gilead?' And Jehoshaphat said to the king of Israel: 'I am as thou art, my people as thy people, my horses as thy horses.'

{{verse|22|5}}And Jehoshaphat said unto the king of Israel: 'Inquire, I pray thee, at the word of the {{sc|Lord}} to-day.' {{verse|22|6}}Then the king of Israel gathered the prophets together, about four hundred men, and said unto them: 'Shall I go against Ramoth-gilead to battle, or shall I forbear?' And they said: 'Go up; for the {{sc|Lord}} will deliver it into the hand of the king.' {{verse|22|7}}But Jehoshaphat said: 'Is there not here besides a prophet of the {{sc|Lord}}, that we might inquire of him?' {{verse|22|8}}And the king of Israel said unto Jehoshaphat: 'There is yet one man by whom we may inquire of the {{sc|Lord}}, Micaiah the son of Imlah; but I hate him; for he doth not prophesy good concerning me, but evil.' And Jehoshaphat said: 'Let not the king say so.' {{verse|22|9}}Then the king of Israel called an officer, and said: 'Fetch quickly Micaiah the son of Imlah.' {{verse|22|10}}Now the king of Israel and Jehoshaphat the king of Judah sat each on his throne, arrayed in their robes, in a threshing-floor, at the entrance of the gate of Samaria; and all the prophets prophesied before them. {{verse|22|11}}And Zedekiah the son of Chenaanah made him horns of iron, and said: 'Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: With these shalt thou gore the Arameans, until they be consumed.' {{verse|22|12}}And all the prophets prophesied so, saying: 'Go up to Ramoth-gilead, and prosper; for the {{sc|Lord}} will deliver it into the hand of the king.'

{{verse|22|13}}And the messenger that went to call Micaiah spoke unto him, saying: 'Behold now, the words of the prophets declare good unto the king with one mouth, let thy word, I pray thee, be like the word of one of them, and speak thou good.' {{verse|22|14}}And Micaiah said: 'As the {{sc|Lord}} liveth, what the {{sc|Lord}} saith unto me, that will I speak.' {{verse|22|15}}And when he was come to the king, the king said unto him: 'Micaiah, shall we go to Ramoth-gilead to battle, or shall we forbear?' And he answered him: 'Go up, and prosper; and the {{sc|Lord}} will deliver it into the hand of the king.' {{verse|22|16}}And the king said unto him: 'How many times shall I adjure thee that thou speak unto me nothing but the truth in the name of the {{sc|Lord}}?' {{verse|22|17}}And he said: 'I saw all Israel scattered upon the mountains, as sheep that have no shepherd; and the {{sc|Lord}} said: These have no master; let them return every man to his house in peace.' {{verse|22|18}}And the king of Israel said to Jehoshaphat: 'Did I not tell thee that he would not prophesy good concerning me, but evil?' {{verse|22|19}}And he said: 'Therefore hear thou the word of the {{sc|Lord}}.

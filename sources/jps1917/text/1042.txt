{{verse|8|15}}And it came to pass, when I, even I Daniel, had seen the vision, that I sought to understand it; and, behold, there stood before me as the appearance of a man. {{verse|8|16}}And I heard the voice of a man between the banks of Ulai, who called, and said: 'Gabriel, make this man to understand the vision.' {{verse|8|17}}So he came near where I stood; and when he came, I was terrified, and fell upon my face; but he said unto me: 'Understand, O son of man; for the vision belongeth to the time of the end.' {{verse|8|18}}Now as he was speaking with me, I fell into a deep sleep with my face toward the ground; but he touched me, and set me upright. {{verse|8|19}}And he said: 'Behold, I will make thee know what shall be in the latter time of the indignation; for it belongeth to the appointed time of the end. {{verse|8|20}}The ram which thou sawest having the two horns, they are the kings of Media and Persia. {{verse|8|21}}And the rough he-goat is the king of Greece; and the great horn that is between his eyes is the first king. {{verse|8|22}}And as for that which was broken, in the place whereof four stood up, four kingdoms shall stand up out of the nation, but not with his power. {{verse|8|23}}And in the latter time of their kingdom, when the transgressors have completed their transgression, there shall stand up a king of fierce countenance, and understanding stratagems. {{verse|8|24}}And his power shall be mighty, but not by his own power; and he shall destroy wonderfully, and shall prosper and do; and he shall destroy them that are mighty and the people of the saints. {{verse|8|25}}And through his cunning he shall cause craft to prosper in his hand; and he shall magnify himself in his heart, and in time of security shall he destroy many; he shall also stand up against the prince of princes; but he shall be broken without hand. {{verse|8|26}}And the vision of the evenings and mornings which hath been told is true; but thou, shut thou up the vision; for it belongeth to many days to come.' {{verse|8|27}}And I Daniel fainted, and was sick certain days; then I rose up, and did the king's business; and I was appalled at the vision, but understood it not.

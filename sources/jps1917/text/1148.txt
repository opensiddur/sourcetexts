And they sang praises with gladness, and they bowed their heads and prostrated themselves.

{{verse|29|31}}Then Hezekiah answered and said: 'Now ye have consecrated yourselves unto the {{sc|Lord}}, come near and bring sacrifices and thank-offerings into the house of the {{sc|Lord}}.' And the congregation brought in sacrifices and thank-offerings; and as many as were of a willing heart brought burnt-offerings. {{verse|29|32}}And the number of the burnt-offerings, which the congregation brought, was threescore and ten bullocks, a hundred rams, and two hundred lambs; all these were for a burnt-offering to the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|29|33}}And the consecrated things were six hundred oxen and three thousand sheep. {{verse|29|34}}But the priests were too few, so that they could not flay all the burnt-offerings; wherefore their brethren the Levites did help them, till the work was ended, and until the priests had sanctified themselves; for the Levites were more upright in heart to sanctify themselves than the priests. {{verse|29|35}}And also the burnt-offerings were in abundance, with the fat of the peace-offerings, and the drink-offerings for every burnt-offering. So the service of the house of the {{sc|Lord}} was firmly established. {{verse|29|36}}And Hezekiah rejoiced, and all the people, because of that which God had prepared for the people; for the thing was done suddenly.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|30:1}}30}} And Hezekiah sent to all Israel and Judah, and wrote letters also to Ephraim and Manasseh, that they should come to the house of the {{sc|Lord}} at Jerusalem, to keep the passover unto the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel. {{verse|30|2}}For the king had taken counsel, and his princes, and all the congregation in Jerusalem, to keep the passover in the second month. {{verse|30|3}}For they could not keep it at that time, because the priests had not sanctified themselves in sufficient number, neither had the people gathered themselves together to Jerusalem. {{verse|30|4}}And the thing was right in the eyes of the king and of all the congregation. {{verse|30|5}}So they established a decree to make proclamation throughout all Israel, from Beer-sheba even to Dan, that they should come to keep the passover unto the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, at Jerusalem; for they had not kept it in great numbers according as it is written.

{{verse|30|6}}So the posts went with the letters from the king and his princes throughout all Israel and Judah, and according to the commandment of the king, saying: 'Ye children of Israel, turn back unto the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Israel, that He may return to the remnant that are escaped of you out of the hand of the kings of Assyria. {{verse|30|7}}And be not ye like your fathers, and like your brethren, who acted treacherously against the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers, so that He delivered them to be an astonishment, as ye see. {{verse|30|8}}Now be ye not stiffnecked, as your fathers were; but yield yourselves unto the {{sc|Lord}}, and enter into His sanctuary, which He hath sanctified for ever, and serve the {{sc|Lord}} your God, that His fierce anger may turn away from you. {{verse|30|9}}For if ye turn back unto the {{sc|Lord}}, your brethren and your children shall find compassion before them that led them captive, and shall come back into this land; for the {{sc|Lord}} your God is gracious and merciful, and will not turn away His face from you, if ye return unto Him.'

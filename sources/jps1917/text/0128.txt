{{dropinitial|{{anchor|33:1}}33}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses: 'Depart, go up hence, thou and the people that thou hast brought up out of the land of Egypt, unto the land of which I swore unto Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, saying: Unto thy seed will I give it— {{verse|chapter=33|verse=2}}and I will send an angel before thee; and I will drive out the Canaanite, the Amorite, and the Hittite, and the Perizzite, the Hivite, and the Jebusite— {{verse|chapter=33|verse=3}}unto a land flowing with milk and honey; for I will not go up in the midst of thee; for thou art a stiffnecked people; lest I consume thee in the way.' {{verse|chapter=33|verse=4}}And when the people heard these evil tidings, they mourned; and no man did put on him his ornaments. {{verse|chapter=33|verse=5}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'Say unto the children of Israel: Ye are a stiffnecked people; if I go up into the midst of thee for one moment, I shall consume thee; therefore now put off thy ornaments from thee, that I may know what to do unto thee.' {{verse|chapter=33|verse=6}}And the children of Israel stripped themselves of their ornaments from mount Horeb onward.

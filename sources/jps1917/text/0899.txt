{{dropinitial|{{anchor|141:1}}141}} A Psalm of David.

:{{sc|Lord}}, I have called Thee; make haste unto me; 
:Give ear unto my voice, when I call unto Thee.
:{{verse|141|2|align=float}}Let my prayer be set forth as incense before Thee,
:The lifting up of my hands as the evening sacrifice.
:{{verse|141|3|align=float}}Set a guard, O {{sc|Lord}}, to my mouth;
:Keep watch at the door of my lips.
:{{verse|141|4|align=float}}Incline not my heart to any evil thing,
:To be occupied in deeds of wickedness
:With men that work iniquity;
:And let me not eat of their dainties.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|141|5|align=float}}Let the righteous smite me in kindness, and correct me;
:Oil so choice let not my head refuse;
:For still is my prayer because of their wickedness.
:{{verse|141|6|align=float}}Their judges are thrown down by the sides of the rock;
:And they shall hear my words, that they are sweet.
:{{verse|141|7|align=float}}As when one cleaveth and breaketh up the earth,
:Our bones are scattered at the grave's mouth.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|141|8|align=float}}For mine eyes are unto Thee, O {{sc|God}} the Lord;
:In Thee have I taken refuge, O pour not out my soul.
:{{verse|141|9|align=float}}Keep me from the snare which they have laid for me,
:And from the gins of the workers of iniquity.
:{{verse|141|10|align=float}}Let the wicked fall into their own nets,
:Whilst I withal escape.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=24}}If thou lend money to any of My people, even to the poor with thee, thou shalt not be to him as a creditor; neither shall ye lay upon him interest. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=25}}If thou at all take thy neighbour's garment to pledge, thou shalt restore it unto him by that the sun goeth down; {{verse|chapter=22|verse=26}}for that is his only covering, it is his garment for his skin; wherein shall he sleep? and it shall come to pass, when he crieth unto Me, that I will hear; for I am gracious.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=27}}Thou shalt not revile <ref name="judges">That is, the judges.</ref>God, nor curse a ruler of thy people. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=28}}Thou shalt not delay to offer of the fulness of thy harvest, and of the outflow of thy presses. The first-born of thy sons shalt thou give unto Me. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=29}}Likewise shalt thou do with thine oxen, and with thy sheep; seven days it shall be with its dam; on the eighth day thou shalt give it Me. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=30}}And ye shall be holy men unto Me; therefore ye shall not eat any flesh that is torn of beasts in the field; ye shall cast it to the dogs.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|23:1}}23}}Thou shalt not utter a false report; put not thy hand with the wicked to be an unrighteous witness. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=2}}Thou shalt not follow a multitude to do evil; neither shalt thou bear witness in a cause to turn aside after a multitude to pervert justice; {{verse|chapter=23|verse=3}}neither shalt thou favour a poor man in his cause.

{{verse|chapter=23|verse=4}}If thou meet thine enemy's ox or his ass going astray, thou shalt surely bring it back to him again.

{{verse|chapter=23|verse=5}}If thou see the ass of him that hateth thee lying under its burden, thou shalt forbear to pass by him; thou shalt surely release it with him.

{{verse|chapter=23|verse=6}}Thou shalt not wrest the judgment of thy poor in his cause. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=7}}Keep thee far from a false matter; and the innocent and righteous slay thou not; for I will not justify the wicked. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=8}}And thou shalt take no gift; for a gift blindeth them that have sight, and perverteth the words of the righteous. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=9}}And a stranger shalt thou not oppress; for ye know the heart of a stranger, seeing ye were strangers in the land of Egypt.

{{verse|chapter=23|verse=10}}And six years thou shalt sow thy land, and gather in the increase thereof; {{verse|chapter=23|verse=11}}but the seventh year thou shalt let it rest and lie fallow, that the poor of thy people may eat; and what they leave the beast of the field shall eat. In like manner thou shalt deal with thy vineyard, and with thy oliveyard. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=12}}Six days thou shalt do thy work, but on the seventh day thou shalt rest; that thine ox and thine ass may have rest, and the son of thy handmaid, and the stranger, may be refreshed. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=13}}And in all things that I have said unto you take ye heed; and make no mention of the name of other gods, neither let it be heard out of thy mouth. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=14}}Three times thou shalt keep a feast unto Me in the year. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=15}}The feast of unleavened bread shalt thou keep; seven days thou shalt eat unleavened bread, as I commanded thee, at the time appointed in the month Abib—for in it thou camest out from Egypt; and none shall appear before Me empty; {{verse|chapter=23|verse=16}}and the feast of harvest, the first-fruits of thy labours, which thou sowest in the field; and the feast of ingathering, at the end of the year, when thou gatherest in thy labours out of the field. {{verse|chapter=23|verse=17}}Three times in the year all thy males shall appear before the Lord {{sc|God}}.

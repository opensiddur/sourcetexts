{{verse|14|6}}Then the children of Judah drew nigh unto Joshua in Gilgal; and Caleb the son of Jephunneh the Kenizzite said unto him: 'Thou knowest the thing that the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses the man of God concerning me and concerning thee in Kadesh-barnea. {{verse|14|7}}Forty years old was I when Moses the servant of the {{sc|Lord}} sent me from Kadesh-barnea to spy out the land; and I brought him back word as it was in my heart. {{verse|14|8}}Nevertheless my brethren that went up with me made the heart of the people melt; but I wholly followed the {{sc|Lord}} my God. {{verse|14|9}}And Moses swore on that day, saying: Surely the land whereon thy foot hath trodden shall be an inheritance to thee and to thy children for ever, because thou hast wholly followed the {{sc|Lord}} my God. {{verse|14|10}}And now, behold, the {{sc|Lord}} hath kept me alive, as He spoke, these forty and five years, from the time that the {{sc|Lord}} spoke this word unto Moses, while Israel walked in the wilderness; and now, lo, I am this day fourscore and five years old. {{verse|14|11}}As yet I am as strong this day as I was in the day that Moses sent me; as my strength was then, even so is my strength now, for war, and to go out and to come in. {{verse|14|12}}Now therefore give me this mountain, whereof the {{sc|Lord}} spoke in that day; for thou heardest in that day how the Anakim were there, and cities great and fortified; it may be that the {{sc|Lord}} will be with me, and I shall drive them out, as the {{sc|Lord}} spoke.' {{verse|14|13}}And Joshua blessed him; and he gave Hebron unto Caleb the son of Jephunneh for an inheritance. {{verse|14|14}}Therefore Hebron became the inheritance of Caleb the son of Jephunneh the Kenizzite, unto this day; because that he wholly followed the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel. {{verse|14|15}}Now the name of Hebron beforetime was <ref>That is, ''The city of Arba.''</ref>Kiriath-arba, which Arba was the greatest man among the Anakim. And the land had rest from war.

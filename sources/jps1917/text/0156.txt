<noinclude><pagequality level="4" user="Kathleen.wright5" />{{rh|{{larger|13}}.41|LEVITICUS|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{hwe|bald|forehead-bald}}; yet is he clean. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=42}}But if there be in the bald head, or the bald forehead, a reddish-white plague, it is leprosy breaking out in his bald head, or his bald forehead. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=43}}Then the priest shall look upon him; and, behold, if the rising of the plague be reddish-white in his bald head, or in his bald forehead, as the appearance of leprosy in the skin of the flesh, {{verse|chapter=13|verse=44}}he is a leprous man, he is unclean; the priest shall surely pronounce him unclean: his plague is in his head.

{{verse|chapter=13|verse=45}}And the leper in whom the plague is, his clothes shall be rent, and the hair of his head shall go loose, and he shall cover his upper lip, and shall cry: 'Unclean, unclean.' {{verse|chapter=13|verse=46}}All the days wherein the plague is in him he shall be unclean; he is unclean; he shall dwell alone; without the camp shall his dwelling be.

{{verse|chapter=13|verse=47}}And when the plague of leprosy is in a garment, whether it be a woolen garment, or a linen garment; {{verse|chapter=13|verse=48}}or in the warp, or in the woof, whether they be of linen, or of wool; or in a skin, or in any thing made of skin. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=49}}If the plague be greenish or reddish in the garment, or in the skin, or in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of skin, it is the plague of leprosy, and shall be shown unto the priest. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=50}}And the priest shall look upon the plague, and shut up that which hath the plague seven days. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=51}}And he shall look on the plague on the seventh day: if the plague be spread in the garment, or in the warp, or in the woof, or in the skin, whatever service skin is used for, the plague is a malignant leprosy: it is unclean. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=52}}And he shall burn the garment, or the warp, or the woof, whether it be of wool or of linen, or any thing of skin, wherein the plague is; for it is a malignant leprosy; it shall be burnt in the fire. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=53}}And if the priest shall look, and, behold, the plague be not spread in the garment, or in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of skin; {{verse|chapter=13|verse=54}}then the priest shall command that they wash the thing wherein the plague is, and he shall shut it up seven days more. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=55}}And the priest shall look, after that the plague is washed; and, behold, if the plague have not changed its colour, and the plague be not spread, it is unclean; thou shalt burn it in the fire; it is a fret, whether the bareness be within or without. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=56}}And if the priest look, and, behold, the plague be dim after the washing thereof, then he shall rend it out of the garment, or out of the skin, or out of the warp, or out of the woof. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=57}}And if it appear still in the garment, or in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of skin, it is breaking out, thou shalt burn that wherein the plague is with fire. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=58}}And the garment, or the warp, or the woof, or whatsoever thing of skin it be, which thou shalt wash, if the plague be departed from them, then it shall be washed the second time, and shall be clean. {{verse|chapter=13|verse=59}}This is the law of the plague of leprosy in a garment of wool or linen, or in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of skin, to pronounce it clean, or to pronounce it unclean.

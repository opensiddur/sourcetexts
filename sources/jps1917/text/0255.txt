{{dropinitial|{{anchor|16:1}}16}}Observe the month of Abib, and keep the passover unto the {{sc|Lord}} thy God; for in the month of Abib the {{sc|Lord}} thy God brought thee forth out of Egypt by night. {{verse|16|2}}And thou shalt sacrifice the passover-offering unto the {{sc|Lord}} thy God, of the flock and the herd, in the place which the {{sc|Lord}} shall choose to cause His name to dwell there. {{verse|16|3}}Thou shalt eat no leavened bread with it; seven days shalt thou eat unleavened bread therewith, even the bread of affliction; for in haste didst thou come forth out of the land of Egypt; that thou mayest remember the day when thou camest forth out of the land of Egypt all the days of thy life. {{verse|16|4}}And there shall be no leaven seen with thee in all thy borders seven days; neither shall any of the flesh, which thou sacrificest the first day at even, remain all night until the morning. {{verse|16|5}}Thou mayest not sacrifice the passover-offering within any of thy gates, which the {{sc|Lord}} thy God giveth thee; {{verse|16|6}}but at the place which the {{sc|Lord}} thy God shall choose to cause His name to dwell in, there thou shalt sacrifice the passover-offering at even, at the going down of the sun, at the season that thou camest forth out of Egypt. {{verse|16|7}}And thou shalt roast and eat it in the place which the {{sc|Lord}} thy God shall choose; and thou shalt turn in the morning, and go unto thy tents. {{verse|16|8}}Six days thou shalt eat unleavened bread; and on the seventh day shall be a solemn assembly to the {{sc|Lord}} thy God; thou shalt do no work therein.

{{verse|16|9}}Seven weeks shalt thou number unto thee; from the time the sickle is first put to the standing corn shalt thou begin to number seven weeks. {{verse|16|10}}And thou shalt keep the feast of weeks unto the {{sc|Lord}} thy God after the measure of the freewill-offering of thy hand, which thou shalt give, according as the {{sc|Lord}} thy God blesseth thee. {{verse|16|11}}And thou shalt rejoice before the {{sc|Lord}} thy God, thou, and thy son, and thy daughter, and thy man-servant, and thy maid-servant, and the Levite that is within thy gates, and the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow, that are in the midst of thee, in the place which the {{sc|Lord}} thy God shall choose to cause His name to dwell there. {{verse|16|12}}And thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in Egypt; and thou shalt observe and do these statutes.

{{verse|16|13}}Thou shalt keep the feast of tabernacles seven days, after that thou hast gathered in from thy threshing-floor and from thy winepress. {{verse|16|14}}And thou shalt rejoice in thy feast, thou, and thy son, and thy daughter, and thy man-servant, and thy maid-servant, and the Levite, and the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow, that are within thy gates. {{verse|16|15}}Seven days shalt thou keep a feast unto the {{sc|Lord}} thy God in the place which the {{sc|Lord}} shall choose; because the {{sc|Lord}} thy God shall bless thee in all thine increase, and in all the work of thy hands, and thou shalt be altogether joyful. {{verse|16|16}}Three times in a year shall all thy males appear before the {{sc|Lord}} thy God in the place which He shall choose. on the feast of unleavened bread, and on the feast of weeks, and on the feast of tabernacles; and they shall not appear before the {{sc|Lord}} empty; {{verse|16|17}}every man shall give as he is able, according to the blessing of the {{sc|Lord}} thy God which He hath given thee.

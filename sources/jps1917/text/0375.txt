{{verse|21|11}}And David arose, and fled that day for fear of Saul, and went to Achish the king of Gath. {{verse|21|12}}And the servants of Achish said unto him: 'Is not this David the king of the land? Did they not sing one to another of him in dances, saying:

:Saul hath slain his thousands,
:And David his ten thousands?'

{{verse|21|13}}And David laid up these words in his heart, and was sore afraid of Achish the king of Gath. {{verse|21|14}}And he changed his demeanour before them, and feigned himself mad in their hands, and scrabbled on the doors of the gate, and let his spittle fall down upon his beard. {{verse|21|15}}Then said Achish unto his servants: 'Lo, when ye see a man that is mad, wherefore do ye bring him to me? {{verse|21|16}}Do I lack madmen, that ye have brought this fellow to play the madman in my presence? shall this fellow come into my house?'


{{dropinitial|{{anchor|22:1}}22}}David therefore departed thence, and escaped to the cave of Adullam; and when his brethren and all his father's house heard it, they went down thither to him. {{verse|22|2}}And every one that was in distress, and every one that was in debt, and every one that was discontented, gathered themselves unto him; and he became captain over them; and there were with him about four hundred men.

{{verse|22|3}}And David went thence to Mizpeh of Moab; and he said unto the king of Moab: 'Let my father and my mother, I pray thee, come forth, and be with you, till I know what God will do for me.' {{verse|22|4}}And he brought them before the king of Moab; and they dwelt with him all the while that David was in the stronghold. {{verse|22|5}}And the prophet Gad said unto David: 'Abide not in the stronghold; depart, and get thee into the land of Judah.' Then David departed, and came into the forest of Hereth.

{{verse|22|6}}And Saul heard that David was discovered, and the men that were with him; now Saul was sitting in Gibeah, under the tamarisk-tree in Ramah, with his spear in his hand, and all his servants were standing about him. {{verse|22|7}}And Saul said unto his servants that stood about him: 'Hear now, ye Benjamites; will the son of Jesse give every one of you fields and vineyards, will he make you all captains of thousands and captains of hundreds; {{verse|22|8}}that all of you have conspired against me, and there was none that disclosed it to me when my son made a league with the son of Jesse, and there is none of you that is sorry for me, or discloseth unto me that my son hath stirred up my servant against me, to lie in wait, as at this day?' {{verse|22|9}}Then answered Doeg the Edomite, who was set over the servants of Saul, and said: 'I saw the son of Jesse coming to Nob, to Ahimelech the son of Ahitub. {{verse|22|10}}And he inquired of the {{sc|Lord}} for him, and gave him victuals, and gave him the sword of Goliath the Philistine.'

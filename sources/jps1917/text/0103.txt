And they did so. {{verse|chapter=14|verse=5}}And it was told the king of Egypt that the people were fled; and the heart of Pharaoh and of his servants was turned towards the people, and they said: 'What is this we have done, that we have let Israel go from serving us? {{verse|chapter=14|verse=6}}And he made ready his chariots, and took his people with him. {{verse|chapter=14|verse=7}}And he took six hundred chosen chariots, and all the chariots of Egypt, and captains over all of them. {{verse|chapter=14|verse=8}}And the {{sc|Lord}} hardened the heart of Pharaoh king of Egypt, and he pursued after the children of Israel; for the children of Israel went out with a high hand. {{verse|chapter=14|verse=9}}And the Egyptians pursued after them, all the horses and chariots of Pharaoh, and his horsemen, and his army, and overtook them encamping by the sea, beside Pi-hahiroth, in front of Baal-zephon. {{verse|chapter=14|verse=10}}And when Pharaoh drew nigh, the children of Israel lifted up their eyes, and, behold, the Egyptians were marching after them; and they were sore afraid; and the children of Israel cried out unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=14|verse=11}}And they said unto Moses: 'Because there were no graves in Egypt, hast thou taken us away to die in the wilderness? wherefore hast thou dealt thus with us, to bring us forth out of Egypt? {{verse|chapter=14|verse=12}}Is not this the word that we spoke unto thee in Egypt, saying: Let us alone, that we may serve the Egyptians? For it were better for us to serve the Egyptians, than that we should die in the wilderness.' {{verse|chapter=14|verse=13}}And Moses said unto the people: 'Fear ye not, stand still, and see the salvation of the {{sc|Lord}}, which He will work for you to-day; for whereas ye have seen the Egyptians to-day, ye shall see them again no more for ever. {{verse|chapter=14|verse=14}}The {{sc|Lord}} will fight for you, and ye shall hold your peace.'

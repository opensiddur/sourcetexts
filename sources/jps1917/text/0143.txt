{{verse|chapter=4|verse=13}}And if the whole congregation of Israel shall err, the thing being hid from the eyes of the assembly, and do any of the things which the {{sc|Lord}} hath commanded not to be done, and are guilty: {{verse|chapter=4|verse=14}}when the sin wherein they have sinned is known, then the assembly shall offer a young bullock for a sin-offering, and bring it before the tent of meeting. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=15}}And the elders of the congregation shall lay their hands upon the head of the bullock before the {{sc|Lord}}; and the bullock shall be killed before the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=16}}And the anointed priest shall bring of the blood of the bullock to the tent of meeting. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=17}}And the priest shall dip his finger in the blood, and sprinkle it seven times before the {{sc|Lord}}, in front of the veil. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=18}}And he shall put of the blood upon the horns of the altar which is before the {{sc|Lord}}, that is in the tent of meeting, and all the remaining blood shall he pour out at the base of the altar of burnt-offering, which is at the door of the tent of meeting. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=19}}And all the fat thereof shall he take off from it, and make it smoke upon the altar. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=20}}Thus shall he do with the bullock; as he did with the bullock of the sin-offering, so shall he do with this; and the priest shall make atonement for them, and they shall be forgiven. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=21}}And he shall carry forth the bullock without the camp, and burn it as he burned the first bullock; it is the sin-offering for the assembly.

{{verse|chapter=4|verse=22}}When a ruler sinneth, and doeth through error any one of all the things which the {{sc|Lord}} his God hath commanded not to be done, and is guilty: {{verse|chapter=4|verse=23}}if his sin, wherein he hath sinned, be known to him, he shall bring for his offering a goat, a male without blemish. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=24}}And he shall lay his hand upon the head of the goat, and kill it in the place where they kill the burnt-offering before the {{sc|Lord}}; it is a sin-offering. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=25}}And the priest shall take of the blood of the sin-offering with his finger, and put it upon the horns of the altar of burnt-offering, and the remaining blood thereof shall he pour out at the base of the altar of burnt-offering. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=26}}And all the fat thereof shall he make smoke upon the altar, as the fat of the sacrifice of peace-offerings; and the priest shall make atonement for him as concerning his sin, and he shall be forgiven.

{{verse|chapter=4|verse=27}}And if any one of the common people sin through error, in doing any of the things which the {{sc|Lord}} hath commanded not to be done, and be guilty: {{verse|chapter=4|verse=28}}if his sin, which he hath sinned, be known to him, then he shall bring for his offering a goat, a female without blemish, for his sin which he hath sinned. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=29}}And he shall lay his hand upon the head of the sin-offering, and kill the sin-offering in the place of burnt-offering. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=30}}And the priest shall take of the blood thereof with his finger, and put it upon the horns of the altar of burnt-offering, and all the remaining blood thereof shall he pour out at the base of the altar. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=31}}And all the fat thereof shall he take away, as the fat is taken away from off the sacrifice of peace-offerings; and the priest shall make it smoke upon the altar for a sweet savour unto the {{sc|Lord}}; and the priest shall make atonement for him, and he shall be forgiven.

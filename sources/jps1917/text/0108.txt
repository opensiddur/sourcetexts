{{verse|chapter=17|verse=8}}Then came Amalek, and fought with Israel in Rephidim. {{verse|chapter=17|verse=9}}And Moses said unto Joshua: 'Choose us out men, and go out, fight with Amalek; to-morrow I will stand on the top of the hill with the rod of God in my hand.' {{verse|chapter=17|verse=10}}So Joshua did as Moses had said to him, and fought with Amalek; and Moses, Aaron, and Hur went up to the top of the hill. {{verse|chapter=17|verse=11}}And it came to pass, when Moses held up his hand, that Israel prevailed; and when he let down his hand, Amalek prevailed. {{verse|chapter=17|verse=12}}But Moses' hands were heavy; and they took a stone, and put it under him, and he sat thereon; and Aaron and Hur stayed up his hands, the one on the one side, and the other on the other side; and his hands were steady until the going down of the sun. {{verse|chapter=17|verse=13}}And Joshua discomfited Amalek and his people with the edge of the sword.

{{verse|chapter=17|verse=14}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'Write this for a memorial in the book, and rehearse it in the ears of Joshua: for I will utterly blot out the remembrance of Amalek from under heaven.' {{verse|chapter=17|verse=15}}And Moses built an altar, and called the name of it <ref>That is, ''The {{sc|Lord}} is my banner.''</ref>Adonai-nissi. {{verse|chapter=17|verse=16}}And he said: 'The hand upon the throne of the {{sc|Lord}}: the {{sc|Lord}} will have war with Amalek from generation to generation.'

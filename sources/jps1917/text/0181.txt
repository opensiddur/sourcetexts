{{verse|chapter=1|verse=48}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses, saying: {{verse|chapter=1|verse=49}}'Howbeit the tribe of Levi thou shalt not number, neither shalt thou take the sum of them among the children of Israel; {{verse|chapter=1|verse=50}}but appoint thou the Levites over the tabernacle of the testimony, and over all the furniture thereof, and over all that belongeth to it; they shall bear the tabernacle, and all the furniture thereof; and they shall minister unto it, and shall encamp round about the tabernacle. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=51}}And when the tabernacle setteth forward, the Levites shall take it down; and when the tabernacle is to be pitched, the Levites shall set it up; and the common man that draweth nigh shall be put to death. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=52}}And the children of Israel shall pitch their tents, every man with his own camp, and every man with his own standard, according to their hosts. {{verse|chapter=1|verse=53}}But the Levites shall pitch round about the tabernacle of the testimony, that there be no wrath upon the congregation of the children of Israel; and the Levites shall keep the charge of the tabernacle of the testimony.' {{verse|chapter=1|verse=54}}Thus did the children of Israel; according to all that the {{sc|Lord}} commanded Moses, so did they.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|2:1}}2}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses and unto Aaron, saying: {{verse|chapter=2|verse=2}}'The children of Israel shall pitch by their fathers' houses; every man with his own standard, according to the ensigns; a good way off shall they pitch round about the tent of meeting. {{verse|chapter=2|verse=3}}Now those that pitch on the east side toward the sunrising shall be they of the standard of the camp of Judah, according to their hosts; the prince of the children of Judah being Nahshon the son of Amminadab, {{verse|chapter=2|verse=4}}and his host, and those that were numbered of them, threescore and fourteen thousand and six hundred; {{verse|chapter=2|verse=5}}and those that pitch next unto him shall be the tribe of Issachar; the prince of the children of Issachar being Nethanel the son of Zuar, {{verse|chapter=2|verse=6}}and his host, even those that were numbered thereof, fifty and four thousand and four hundred; {{verse|chapter=2|verse=7}}and the tribe of Zebulun; the prince of the children of Zebulun being Eliab the son of Helon, {{verse|chapter=2|verse=8}}and his host, and those that were numbered thereof, fifty and seven thousand and four hundred; {{verse|chapter=2|verse=9}}all that were numbered of the camp of Judah being a hundred thousand and fourscore thousand and six thousand and four hundred, according to their hosts; they shall set forth first.

{{verse|chapter=2|verse=10}}On the south side shall be the standard of the camp of Reuben according to their hosts; the prince of the children of Reuben being Elizur the son of Shedeur, {{verse|chapter=2|verse=11}}and his host, and those that were numbered thereof, forty and six thousand and five hundred; {{verse|chapter=2|verse=12}}and those that pitch next unto him shall be the tribe of Simeon; the prince of the children of Simeon being Shelumiel the son of Zurishaddai, {{verse|chapter=2|verse=13}}and his host, and those that were numbered of them, fifty and nine thousand and three hundred; {{verse|chapter=2|verse=14}}and the tribe of Gad; the prince of the children of Gad being Eliasaph the son of Reuel, {{verse|chapter=2|verse=15}}and his host, even those that were numbered of them, forty and five thousand and six hundred and fifty; {{verse|chapter=2|verse=16}}all that were numbered of the camp of Reuben being a hundred thousand and fifty and one thousand and four hundred and fifty, according to their hosts; and they shall set forth second.

<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh|{{larger|32}}.16|SECOND CHRONICLES|}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{verse|32|16}}And his servants spoke yet more against the {{sc|Lord}} God, and against His servant Hezekiah. {{verse|32|17}}He wrote also a letter, to taunt the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, and to speak against Him, saying: 'As the gods of the nations of the lands, which have not delivered their people out of my hand, so shall not the God of Hezekiah deliver His people out of my hand.' {{verse|32|18}}And they cried with a loud voice in the Jews' language unto the people of Jerusalem that were on the wall, to terrify them, and to affright them; that they might take the city. {{verse|32|19}}And they spoke of the God of Jerusalem, as of the gods of the peoples of the earth, which are the work of men's hands.

{{verse|32|20}}And Hezekiah the king, and Isaiah the prophet the son of Amoz, prayed because of this, and cried to heaven. {{verse|32|21}}And the {{sc|Lord}} sent an angel, who cut off all the mighty men of valour, and the leaders and captains, in the camp of the king of Assyria. So he returned with shame of face to his own land. And when he was come into the house of his god, they that came forth of his own bowels slew him there with the sword. {{verse|32|22}}Thus the {{sc|Lord}} saved Hezekiah and the inhabitants of Jerusalem from the hand of Sennacherib the king of Assyria, and from the hand of all, and guided them on every side. {{verse|32|23}}And many brought gifts unto the {{sc|Lord}} to Jerusalem, and precious things to Hezekiah king of Judah; so that he was exalted in the sight of all nations from thenceforth.

{{verse|32|24}}In those days Hezekiah was sick even unto death; and he prayed unto the {{sc|Lord}}; and He spoke unto him, and gave him a sign. {{verse|32|25}}But Hezekiah rendered not according to the benefit done unto him; for his heart was lifted up; therefore there was wrath upon him, and upon Judah and Jerusalem. {{verse|32|26}}Notwithstanding Hezekiah humbled himself for the pride of his heart, both he and the inhabitants of Jerusalem, so that the wrath of the {{sc|Lord}} came not upon them in the days of Hezekiah.

{{verse|32|27}}And Hezekiah had exceeding much riches and honour; and he provided him treasuries for silver, and for gold, and for precious stones, and for spices, and for shields, and for all manner of goodly vessels; {{verse|32|28}}store-houses also for the increase of corn, and wine, and oil; and stalls for all manner of beasts, and flocks in folds. {{verse|32|29}}Moreover he provided him cities, and possessions of flocks and herds in abundance; for God had given him very much substance. {{verse|32|30}}This same Hezekiah also stopped the upper spring of the waters of Gihon, and brought them straight down on the west side of the city of David. And Hezekiah prospered in all his works. {{verse|32|31}}Howbeit in the business of the ambassadors of the princes of Babylon, who sent unto him to inquire of the wonder that was done in the land, God left him, to try him, that He might know all that was in his heart.

{{verse|32|32}}Now the rest of the acts of Hezekiah, and his good deeds, behold, they are written in the vision of Isaiah the prophet the son of Amoz, and in the book of the kings of Judah and Israel. {{verse|32|33}}And Hezekiah slept with his fathers, and they buried him in the ascent of the sepulchres of the sons of David; and all Judah and the inhabitants of Jerusalem did him honour at his death. And Manasseh his son reigned in his stead.

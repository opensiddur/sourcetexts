{{verse|chapter=7|verse=8}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses and unto Aaron, saying: {{verse|chapter=7|verse=9}}'When Pharaoh shall speak unto you, saying: Show a wonder for you; then thou shalt say unto Aaron: Take thy rod, and cast it down before Pharaoh, that it become a serpent.' {{verse|chapter=7|verse=10}}And Moses and Aaron went in unto Pharaoh, and they did so, as the {{sc|Lord}} had commanded; and Aaron cast down his rod before Pharaoh and before his servants, and it became a serpent. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=11}}Then Pharaoh also called for the wise men and the sorcerers; and they also, the magicians of Egypt, did in like manner with their secret arts. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=12}}For they cast down every man his rod, and they became serpents; but Aaron's rod swallowed up their rods. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=13}}And Pharaoh's heart was hardened, and he hearkened not unto them; as the {{sc|Lord}} had spoken.

{{verse|chapter=7|verse=14}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'Pharaoh's heart is stubborn, he refuseth to let the people go. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=15}}Get thee unto Pharaoh in the morning; lo, he goeth out unto the water; and thou shalt stand by the river's brink to meet him; and the rod which was turned to a serpent shalt thou take in thy hand. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=16}}And thou shalt say unto him: The {{sc|Lord}}, the God of the Hebrews, hath sent me unto thee, saying: Let My people go, that they may serve Me in the wilderness; and, behold, hitherto thou hast not hearkened; {{verse|chapter=7|verse=17}}thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: In this thou shalt know that I am the {{sc|Lord}}—behold, I will smite with the rod that is in my hand upon the waters which are in the river, and they shall be turned to blood. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=18}}And the fish that are in the river shall die, and the river shall become foul; and the Egyptians shall loathe to drink water from the river.'

{{verse|chapter=7|verse=19}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'Say unto Aaron: Take thy rod, and stretch out thy hand over the waters of Egypt, over their rivers, over their streams, and over their pools, and over all their ponds of water, that they may become blood; and there shall be blood throughout all the land of Egypt, both in vessels of wood and in vessels of stone.' {{verse|chapter=7|verse=20}}And Moses and Aaron did so, as the {{sc|Lord}} commanded; and he lifted up the rod, and smote the waters that were in the river, in the sight of Pharaoh, and in the sight of his servants; and all the waters that were in the river were turned to blood. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=21}}And the fish that were in the river died; and the river became foul, and the Egyptians could not drink water from the river; and the blood was throughout all the land of Egypt. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=22}}And the magicians of Egypt did in like manner with their secret arts; and Pharaoh's heart was hardened, and he hearkened not unto them; as the {{sc|Lord}} had spoken. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=23}}And Pharaoh turned and went into his house, neither did he lay even this to heart. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=24}}And all the Egyptians digged round about the river for water to drink; for they could not drink of the water of the river. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=25}}And seven days were fulfilled, after that the {{sc|Lord}} had smitten the river. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=26}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses: 'Go in unto Pharaoh, and say unto him: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: Let My people go, that they may serve Me. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=27}}And if thou refuse to let them go, behold, I will smite all thy borders with frogs. {{verse|chapter=7|verse=28}}And the river shall swarm with frogs, which shall go up and come into thy house, and into thy bed-chamber, and upon thy bed, and into the house of thy servants,<noinclude>{{smallrefs}}

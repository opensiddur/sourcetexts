{{x-larger|SECOND SAMUEL}}}}

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|1:1}}1}}{{sc|And}} it came to pass after the death of Saul, when David was returned from the slaughter of the Amalekites, and David had abode two days in Ziklag; {{verse|1|2}}it came even to pass on the third day, that, behold, a man came out of the camp from Saul with his clothes rent, and earth upon his head; and so it was, when he came to David, that he fell to the earth, and prostrated himself. {{verse|1|3}}And David said unto him: 'From whence comest thou?' And he said unto him: 'Out of the camp of Israel am I escaped.' {{verse|1|4}}And David said unto him: 'How went the matter? I pray thee, tell me.' And he answered: 'The people are fled from the battle, and many of the people also are fallen and dead; and Saul and Jonathan his son are dead also.' {{verse|1|5}}And David said unto the young man that told him: 'How knowest thou that Saul and Jonathan his son are dead?' {{verse|1|6}}And the young man that told him said: 'As I happened by chance upon mount Gilboa, behold, Saul leaned upon his spear; and, lo, the chariots and the horsemen pressed hard upon him. {{verse|1|7}}And when he looked behind him, he saw me, and called unto me. And I answered: Here am I. {{verse|1|8}}And he said unto me: Who art thou? And I answered him: I am an Amalekite. {{verse|1|9}}And he said unto me: Stand, I pray thee, beside me, and slay me, for the agony hath taken hold of me; because my life is just yet in me. {{verse|1|10}}So I stood beside him, and slew him, because I was sure that he could not live after that he was fallen; and I took the crown that was upon his head, and the bracelet that was on his arm, and have brought them hither unto my lord.'

{{verse|1|11}}Then David took hold on his clothes, and rent them; and likewise all the men that were with him. {{verse|1|12}}And they wailed, and wept, and fasted until even, for Saul, and for Jonathan his son, and for the people of the {{sc|Lord}}, and for the house of Israel; because they were fallen by the sword. {{verse|1|13}}And David said unto the young man that told him: 'Whence art thou?' And he answered: 'I am the son of an Amalekite stranger.' {{verse|1|14}}And David said unto him: 'How wast thou not afraid to put forth thy hand to destroy the {{sc|Lord's}} anointed?' {{verse|1|15}}And David called one of the young men, and said: 'Go near, and fall upon him.' And he smote him that he died. {{verse|1|16}}And David said unto him: 'Thy blood be upon thy head; for thy mouth hath testified against thee, saying: I have slain the {{sc|Lord's}} anointed.'

{{verse|1|17}}And David lamented with this lamentation over Saul and over Jonathan his son, {{verse|1|18}}and said—To teach the sons of Judah the bow. Behold, it is written in the book of Jashar:

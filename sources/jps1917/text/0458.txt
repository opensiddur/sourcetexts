And as soon as he was departed from him, a lion found him; and slew him. {{verse|20|37}}Then he found another man, and said: 'Smite me, I pray thee.' And the man smote him, smiting and wounding him. {{verse|20|38}}So the prophet departed, and waited for the king by the way, and disguised himself with his headband over his eyes. {{verse|20|39}}And as the king passed by, he cried unto the king; and he said: 'Thy servant went out into the midst of the battle; and, behold, a man turned aside, and brought a man unto me, and said: Keep this man; if by any means he be missing, then shall thy life be for his life, or else thou shalt pay a talent of silver. {{verse|20|40}}And as thy servant was busy here and there, he was gone.' And the king of Israel said unto him: 'So shall thy judgment be; thyself hast decided it.' {{verse|20|41}}And he hastened, and took the headband away from his eyes; and the king of Israel discerned him that he was of the prophets. {{verse|20|42}}And he said unto him: 'Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: Because thou hast let go out of thy hand the man whom I had devoted to destruction, therefore thy life shall go for his life, and thy people for his people.' {{verse|20|43}}And the king of Israel went to his house sullen and displeased, and came to Samaria.

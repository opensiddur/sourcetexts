{{verse|12|5}}Then I Daniel looked, and, behold, there stood other two, the one on the bank of the river on this side, and the other on the bank of the river on that side. {{verse|12|6}}And one said to the man clothed in linen, who was above the waters of the river: 'How long shall it be to the end of the wonders?' {{verse|12|7}}And I heard the man clothed in linen, who was above the waters of the river, when he lifted up his right hand and his left hand unto heaven, and swore by Him that liveth for ever that it shall be for a time, times, and a half; and when they have made an end of breaking in pieces the power of the holy people, all these things shall be finished.

{{verse|12|8}}And I heard, but I understood not; then said I: 'O my Lord, what shall be the latter end of these things?' {{verse|12|9}}And he said: 'Go thy way, Daniel; for the words are shut up and sealed till the time of the end. {{verse|12|10}}Many shall purify themselves, and make themselves white, and be refined; but the wicked shall do wickedly; and none of the wicked shall understand; but they that are wise shall understand. {{verse|12|11}}And from the time that the continual burnt-offering shall be taken away, and the detestable thing that causes appalment set up, there shall be a thousand two hundred and ninety days. {{verse|12|12}}Happy is he that waiteth, and cometh to the thousand three hundred and five and thirty days. {{verse|12|13}}But go thou thy way till the end be; and thou shalt rest, and shalt stand up to thy lot, at the end of the days.'<noinclude>{{smallrefs}}

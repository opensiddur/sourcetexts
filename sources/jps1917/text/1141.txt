<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh||SECOND CHRONICLES|{{larger|25}}.3}}{{rule}}</noinclude>such as wrought iron and brass to repair the house of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|24|13}}So the workmen wrought, and the work was perfected by them, and they set up the house of God in its state, and strengthened it. {{verse|24|14}}And when they had made an end, they brought the rest of the money before the king and Jehoiada, whereof were made vessels for the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, even vessels wherewith to minister, and buckets, and pans, and vessels of gold and silver. And they offered burnt-offerings in the house of the {{sc|Lord}} continually all the days of Jehoiada.

{{verse|24|15}}But Jehoiada waxed old and was full of days, and he died; a hundred and thirty years old was he when he died. {{verse|24|16}}And they buried him in the city of David among the kings, because he had done good in Israel, and toward God and His house. {{verse|24|17}}Now after the death of Jehoiada came the princes of Judah, and prostrated themselves before the king. Then the king hearkened unto them. {{verse|24|18}}And they forsook the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers, and served the Asherim and the idols; and wrath came upon Judah and Jerusalem for this their guiltiness. {{verse|24|19}}Yet He sent prophets to them, to bring them back unto the {{sc|Lord}}; and they admonished them, but they would not give ear.

{{verse|24|20}}And the spirit of God clothed Zechariah the son of Jehoiada the priest; and he stood above the people, and said unto them: 'Thus saith God: Why transgress ye the commandments of the {{sc|Lord}}, that ye cannot prosper? because ye have forsaken the {{sc|Lord}}, He hath also forsaken you.' {{verse|24|21}}And they conspired against him, and stoned him with stones at the commandment of the king in the court of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|24|22}}Thus Joash the king remembered not the kindness which Jehoiada his father had done to him, but slew his son. And when he died, he said: 'The {{sc|Lord}} look upon it, and require it.'

{{verse|24|23}}And it came to pass, when the year was come about, that the army of the Arameans came up against him; and they came to Judah and Jerusalem, and destroyed all the princes of the people from among the people, and sent all the spoil of them unto the king of Damascus. {{verse|24|24}}For the army of the Arameans came with a small company of men; and the {{sc|Lord}} delivered a very great host into their hand, because they had forsaken the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers. So they executed judgment upon Joash. {{verse|24|25}}And when they were departed from him—for they left him in great diseases—his own servants conspired against him for the blood of the sons of Jehoiada the priest, and slew him on his bed, and he died; and they buried him in the city of David, but they buried him not in the sepulchres of the kings. {{verse|24|26}}And these are they that conspired against him: Zabad the son of Shimeath the Ammonitess, and Jehozabad the son of Shimrith the Moabitess. {{verse|24|27}}Now concerning his sons, and the multitude of the burdens against him, and the rebuilding of the house of God, behold, they are written in the commentary of the book of the kings. And Amaziah his son reigned in his stead.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|25:1}}25}} Amaziah was twenty and five years old when he began to reign; and he reigned twenty and nine years in Jerusalem; and his mother's name was Jehoaddan of Jerusalem. {{verse|25|2}}And he did that which was right in the eyes of the {{sc|Lord}}, but not with a whole heart. {{verse|25|3}}Now it came to pass, when the kingdom was established unto him, that he slew his servants who had killed the king his father.<noinclude>{{smallrefs}}

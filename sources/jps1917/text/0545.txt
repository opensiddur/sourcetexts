:{{verse|38|10|align=float}}I said: In the noontide of my days I shall go,
:Even to the gates of the nether-world;
:I am deprived of the residue of my years.
:{{verse|38|11|align=float}}I said: I shall not see the {{sc|Lord}},
:Even the {{sc|Lord}} in the land of the living;
:I shall behold man no more with the inhabitants of the world.
:{{verse|38|12|align=float}}My habitation is plucked up and carried away from me
:As a shepherd's tent;
:I have rolled up like a weaver my life;
:He will cut me off from the thrum;
:From day even to night wilt Thou make an end of me.
:{{verse|38|13|align=float}}The more I make myself like unto a lion until morning,
:The more it breaketh all my bones;
:From day even to night wilt Thou make an end of me.
:{{verse|38|14|align=float}}Like a swallow or a crane, so do I chatter,
:I do moan as a dove;
:Mine eyes fail with looking upward.
:O {{sc|Lord}}, I am oppressed, be Thou my surety.

:{{verse|38|15|align=float}}What shall I say? He hath both spoken unto me,

:{{verse|38|16|align=float}}O Lord, by these things men live,
:And altogether therein is the life of my spirit;
:Wherefore recover Thou me, and make me to live.
:{{verse|38|17|align=float}}Behold, for my peace I had great bitterness;
:But Thou hast in love to my soul delivered it

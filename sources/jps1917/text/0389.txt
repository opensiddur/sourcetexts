{{dropinitial|{{anchor|2:1}}2}}And it came to pass after this, that David inquired of the {{sc|Lord}}, saying: 'Shall I go up into any of the cities of Judah?' And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto him: 'Go up.' And David said: 'Whither shall I go up?' And He said: 'Unto Hebron.' {{verse|2|2}}So David went up thither, and his two wives also, Ahinoam the Jezreelitess, and Abigail the wife of Nabal the Carmelite. {{verse|2|3}}And his men that were with him did David bring up, every man with his household; and they dwelt in the cities of Hebron. {{verse|2|4}}And the men of Judah came, and they there anointed David king over the house of Judah.

And they told David, saying: 'The men of Jabesh-gilead were they that buried Saul.' {{verse|2|5}}And David sent messengers unto the men of Jabesh-gilead, and said unto them: 'Blessed be ye of the {{sc|Lord}}, that ye have shown this kindness unto your lord, even unto Saul, and have buried him. {{verse|2|6}}And now the {{sc|Lord}} show kindness and truth unto you; and I also will requite you this kindness, because ye have done this thing. {{verse|2|7}}Now therefore let your hands be strong, and be ye valiant; for Saul your lord is dead, and also the house of Judah have anointed me king over them.'

{{verse|2|8}}Now Abner the son of Ner, captain of Saul's host, had taken Ish-bosheth the son of Saul, and brought him over to Mahanaim; {{verse|2|9}}and he made him king over Gilead, and over the Ashurites, and over Jezreel, and over Ephraim, and over Benjamin, and over all Israel. {{verse|2|10}}Ish-bosheth Saul's son was forty years old when he began to reign over Israel, and he reigned two years. But the house of Judah followed David. {{verse|2|11}}And the time that David was king in Hebron over the house of Judah was seven years and six months.

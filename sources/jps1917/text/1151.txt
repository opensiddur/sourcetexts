{{verse|31|20}}And thus did Hezekiah throughout all Judah; and he wrought that which was good and right and faithful before the {{sc|Lord}} his God. {{verse|31|21}}And in every work that he began in the service of the house of God, and in the law, and in the commandments, to seek his God, he did it with all his heart, and prospered.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|32:1}}32}} After these things, and this faithfulness, Sennacherib king of Assyria came, and entered into Judah, and encamped against the fortified cities, and thought to make a breach therein for himself. {{verse|32|2}}And when Hezekiah saw that Sennacherib was come, and that he was purposed to fight against Jerusalem, {{verse|32|3}}he took counsel with his princes and his mighty men to stop the waters of the fountains which were without the city; and they helped him. {{verse|32|4}}So there was gathered much people together, and they stopped all the fountains, and the brook that flowed through the midst of the land, saying: 'Why should the kings of Assyria come, and find much water?' {{verse|32|5}}And he took courage, and built up all the wall that was broken down, and raised it up to the towers, and another wall without, and strengthened Millo in the city of David, and made weapons and shields in abundance. {{verse|32|6}}And he set captains of war over the people, and gathered them together to him in the broad place at the gate of the city, and spoke encouragingly to them, saying: {{verse|32|7}}'Be strong and of good courage, be not afraid nor dismayed for the king of Assyria, nor for all the multitude that is with him; for there is a Greater with us than with him: {{verse|32|8}}with him is an arm of flesh; but with us is the {{sc|Lord}} our God to help us, and to fight our battles.' And the people rested themselves upon the words of Hezekiah king of Judah.

{{verse|32|9}}After this did Sennacherib king of Assyria send his servants to Jerusalem—now he was before Lachish, and all his power with him—unto Hezekiah king of Judah, and unto all Judah that were at Jerusalem, saying: {{verse|32|10}}'Thus saith Sennacherib king of Assyria: Whereon do ye trust, that ye abide the siege in Jerusalem? {{verse|32|11}}Doth not Hezekiah persuade you, to give you over to die by famine and by thirst, saying: The {{sc|Lord}} our God will deliver us out of the hand of the king of Assyria? {{verse|32|12}}Hath not the same Hezekiah taken away His high places and His altars, and commanded Judah and Jerusalem, saying: Ye shall worship before one altar, and upon it shall ye offer? {{verse|32|13}}Know ye not what I and my fathers have done unto all the peoples of the lands? Were the gods of the nations of the lands in any wise able to deliver their land out of my hand? {{verse|32|14}}Who was there among all the gods of those nations which my fathers utterly destroyed, that could deliver his people out of my hand, that your God should be able to deliver you out of my hand? {{verse|32|15}}Now therefore let not Hezekiah beguile you, nor persuade you after this manner, neither believe ye him; for no god of any nation or kingdom was able to deliver his people out of my hand, and out of the hand of my fathers; how much less shall your God deliver you out of my hand?'

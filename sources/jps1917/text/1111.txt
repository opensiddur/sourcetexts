<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh||FIRST CHRONICLES|{{larger|27}}.25}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{dropinitial|{{anchor|27:1}}27}}Now the children of Israel after their number, to wit, the heads of fathers' houses and the captains of thousands and of hundreds, and their officers that served the king, in any matter of the courses which came in and went out month by month throughout all the months of the year, of every course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|2}}Over the first course for the first month was Jashobeam the son of Zabdiel; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|3}}Of the children of Perez was he, and the chief of all the captains of the host for the first month. {{verse|27|4}}And over the course of the second month was Dodai the Ahohite, and his course, and Mikloth the ruler; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|5}}The third captain of the host for the third month was Benaiah the son of Jehoiada, the priest, chief; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|6}}This is that Benaiah, who was the mighty man of the thirty, and over the thirty; and of his course was Ammizabad his son. {{verse|27|7}}The fourth captain for the fourth month was Asahel the brother of Joab, and Zebadiah his son after him; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|8}}The fifth captain for the fifth month was Shamhuth the Izrahite; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|9}}The sixth captain for the sixth month was Ira the son of Ikkesh the Tekoite; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|10}}The seventh captain for the seventh month was Helez the Pelonite, of the children of Ephraim; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|11}}The eighth captain for the eighth month was Sibbecai the Hushathite, of the Zerahites; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|12}}The ninth captain for the ninth month was Abiezer the Anathothite, of the Benjamites; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|13}}The tenth captain for the tenth month was Mahrai, the Netophathite, of the Zerahites; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|14}}The eleventh captain for the eleventh month was Benaiah the Pirathonite, of the children of Ephraim; and in his course were twenty and four thousand. {{verse|27|15}}The twelfth captain for the twelfth month was Heldai the Netophathite, of Othniel; and in his course were twenty and four thousand.

{{verse|27|16}}Furthermore over the tribes of Israel: of the Reubenites was Eliezer the son of Zichri the ruler; of the Simeonites, Shephatiah the son of Maacah; {{verse|27|17}}of Levi, Hashabiah the son of Kemuel; of Aaron, Zadok; {{verse|27|18}}of Judah, Elihu, one of the brethren of David; of Issachar, Omri the son of Michael; {{verse|27|19}}of Zebulun, Ishmaiah the son of Obadiah; of Naphtali, Jerimoth the son of Azriel; {{verse|27|20}}of the children of Ephraim, Hoshea the son of Azaziah; of the half-tribe of Manasseh, Joel the son of Pedaiah; {{verse|27|21}}of the half-tribe of Manasseh in Gilead, Iddo the son of Zechariah; of Benjamin, Jaasiel the son of Abner; {{verse|27|22}}of Dan, Azarel the son of Jeroham. These were the captains of the tribes of Israel. {{verse|27|23}}But David took not the number of them from twenty years old and under; because the {{sc|Lord}} had said He would increase Israel like to the stars of heaven. {{verse|27|24}}Joab the son of Zeruiah began to number, but finished not; and there came wrath for this upon Israel; neither was the number put into the account in the chronicles of king David.

And Jehudi read it in the ears of the king, and in the ears of all the princes that stood beside the king. {{verse|36|22}}Now the king was sitting in the winter-house in the ninth month; and the brazier was burning before him. {{verse|36|23}}And it came to pass, when Jehudi had read three or four columns, that he cut it with the penknife, and cast it into the fire that was in the brazier, until all the roll was consumed in the fire that was in the brazier. {{verse|36|24}}Yet they were not afraid, nor rent their garments, neither the king, nor any of his servants that heard all these words. {{verse|36|25}}Moreover Elnathan and Delaiah and Gemariah had entreated the king not to burn the roll; but he would not hear them. {{verse|36|26}}And the king commanded Jerahmeel the king's son, and Seraiah the son of Azriel, and Shelemiah the son of Abdeel, to take Baruch the scribe and Jeremiah the prophet; but the {{sc|Lord}} hid them.

{{verse|36|27}}Then the word of the {{sc|Lord}} came to Jeremiah, after that the king had burned the roll, and the words which Baruch wrote at the mouth of Jeremiah, saying: {{verse|36|28}}'Take thee again another roll, and write in it all the former words that were in the first roll, which Jehoiakim the king of Judah hath burned. {{verse|36|29}}And concerning Jehoiakim king of Judah thou shalt say: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: Thou hast burned this roll, saying: Why hast thou written therein, saying: The king of Babylon shall certainly come and destroy this land, and shall cause to cease from thence man and beast? {{verse|36|30}}Therefore thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} concerning Jehoiakim king of Judah: He shall have none to sit upon the throne of David; and his dead body shall be cast out in the day to the heat, and in the night to the frost. {{verse|36|31}}And I will visit upon him and his seed and his servants their iniquity; and I will bring upon them, and upon the inhabitants of Jerusalem, and upon the men of Judah, all the evil that I have pronounced against them, but they hearkened not.'

{{verse|36|32}}Then took Jeremiah another roll, and gave it to Baruch the scribe, the son of Neriah; who wrote therein from the mouth of Jeremiah all the words of the book which Jehoiakim king of Judah had burned in the fire; and there were added besides unto them many like words.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|1:1}}1}}{{sc|In}} the eighth month, in the second year of Darius, came the word of the {{sc|Lord}} unto Zechariah the son of Berechiah, the son of Iddo, the prophet, saying: {{verse|1|2}}'The {{sc|Lord}} hath been sore displeased with your fathers. {{verse|1|3}}Therefore say thou unto them: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts: Return unto Me, saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts, and I will return unto you, saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts. {{verse|1|4}}Be ye not as your fathers, unto whom the former prophets proclaimed, saying: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts: Return ye now from your evil ways, and from your evil doings; but they did not hear, nor attend unto Me, saith the {{sc|Lord}}, {{verse|1|5}}Your fathers, where are they? and the prophets, do they live for ever? {{verse|1|6}}But My words and My statutes, which I commanded My servants the prophets, did they not overtake your fathers? so that they turned and said: Like as the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts purposed to do unto us, according to our ways, and according to our doings, so hath He dealt with us.'

{{verse|1|7}}Upon the four and twentieth day of the eleventh month, which is the month Shebat, in the second year of Darius, came the word of the {{sc|Lord}} unto Zechariah the son of Berechiah, the son of Iddo, the prophet, saying—{{verse|1|8}}I saw in the night, and behold a man riding upon a red horse, and he stood among the myrtle-trees that were in the bottom; and behind him there were horses, red, sorrel, and white. {{verse|1|9}}Then said I: 'O my lord, what are these?' And the angel that spoke with me said unto me: 'I will show thee what these are.' {{verse|1|10}}And the man that stood among the myrtle-trees answered and said: 'These are they whom the {{sc|Lord}} hath sent to walk to and fro through the earth.' {{verse|1|11}}And they answered the angel of the {{sc|Lord}} that stood among the myrtle-trees, and said: 'We have walked to and fro through the earth, and, behold, all the earth sitteth still, and is at rest.' {{verse|1|12}}Then the angel of the {{sc|Lord}} spoke and said: 'O {{sc|Lord}} of hosts, how long wilt Thou not have compassion on Jerusalem and on the cities of Judah, against which Thou hast had indignation these threescore and ten years?' {{verse|1|13}}And the {{sc|Lord}} answered the angel that spoke with me with good words, even comforting words—{{verse|1|14}}so the angel that spoke with me said unto me : 'Proclaim thou, saying: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts: I am jealous for Jerusalem and for Zion with a great jealousy; {{verse|1|15}}and I am very sore displeased with the nations that are at ease; for I was but a little displeased, and they helped for evil. {{verse|1|16}}Therefore thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: I return to Jerusalem with compassions: My house shall be built in it, saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts, and a line shall be stretched forth over Jerusalem. {{verse|1|17}}Again, proclaim, saying: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts: My cities shall again overflow with prosperity; and the {{sc|Lord}} shall yet comfort Zion, and shall yet choose Jerusalem.'

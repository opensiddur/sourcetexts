{{verse|16|5}}And when king David came to Bahurim, behold, there came out thence a man of the family of the house of Saul, whose name was Shimei, the son of Gera; he came out, and kept on cursing as he came. {{verse|16|6}}And he cast stones at David, and at all the servants of king David; and all the people and all the mighty men were on his right hand and on his left. {{verse|16|7}}And thus said Shimei when he cursed: 'Begone, begone, thou man of blood, and base fellow; {{verse|16|8}}the {{sc|Lord}} hath returned upon thee all the blood of the house of Saul, in whose stead thou hast reigned; and the {{sc|Lord}} hath delivered the kingdom into the hand of Absalom thy son; and, behold, thou art taken in thine own mischief, because thou art a man of blood.'

{{verse|16|9}}Then said Abishai the son of Zeruiah unto the king: 'Why should this dead dog curse my lord the king? let me go over, I pray thee, and take off his head.' {{verse|16|10}}And the king said: 'What have I to do with you, ye sons of Zeruiah? So let him curse, because the {{sc|Lord}} hath said unto him: Curse David; who then shall say: Wherefore hast thou done so?' {{verse|16|11}}And David said to Abishai, and to all his servants: 'Behold, my son, who came forth of my body, seeketh my life; how much more this Benjamite now? let him alone, and let him curse; for the {{sc|Lord}} hath bidden him. {{verse|16|12}}It may be that the {{sc|Lord}} will look on mine eye, and that the {{sc|Lord}} will requite me good for his cursing of me this day.' {{verse|16|13}}So David and his men went by the way; and Shimei went along on the hill-side over against him, and cursed as he went, and threw stones at him, and cast dust. {{verse|16|14}}And the king, and all the people that were with him, came weary; and he refreshed himself there.

{{verse|16|15}}And Absalom, and all the people, the men of Israel, came to Jerusalem, and Ahithophel with him. {{verse|16|16}}And it came to pass, when Hushai the Archite, David's friend, was come unto Absalom, that Hushai said unto Absalom: 'Long live the king, long live the king.' {{verse|16|17}}And Absalom said to Hushai: 'Is this thy kindness to thy friend? why wentest thou not with thy friend?' {{verse|16|18}}And Hushai said unto Absalom: 'Nay; but whom the {{sc|Lord}}, and this people, and all the men of Israel have chosen, his will I be, and with him will I abide. {{verse|16|19}}And again, whom should I serve? should I not serve in the presence of his son? as I have served in thy father's presence, so will I be in thy presence.'

{{verse|16|20}}Then said Absalom to Ahithophel: 'Give your counsel what we shall do.' {{verse|16|21}}And Ahithophel said unto Absalom: 'Go in unto thy father's concubines, that he hath left to keep the house; and all Israel will hear that thou art abhorred of thy father; then will the hands of all that are with thee be strong.' {{verse|16|22}}So they spread Absalom a tent upon the top of the house; and Absalom went in unto his father's concubines in the sight of all Israel.—{{verse|16|23}}Now the counsel of Ahithophel, which he counselled in those days, was as if a man inquired of the word of God; so was all the counsel of Ahithophel both with David and with Absalom.

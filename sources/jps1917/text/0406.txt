' {{verse|15|5}}And it was so, that when any man came nigh to prostrate himself before him, he put forth his hand, and took hold of him, and kissed him. {{verse|15|6}}And on this manner did Absalom to all Israel that came to the king for judgment; so Absalom stole the hearts of the men of Israel.

{{verse|15|7}}And it came to pass at the end of forty years, that Absalom said unto the king: 'I pray thee, let me go and pay my vow, which I have vowed unto the {{sc|Lord}}, in Hebron. {{verse|15|8}}For thy servant vowed a vow while I abode at Geshur in Aram, saying: If the {{sc|Lord}} shall indeed bring me back to Jerusalem, then I will serve the {{sc|Lord}}.' {{verse|15|9}}And the king said unto him: 'Go in peace.' So he arose, and went to Hebron. {{verse|15|10}}But Absalom sent spies throughout all the tribes of Israel, saying: 'As soon as ye hear the sound of the horn, then ye shall say: Absalom is king in Hebron.' {{verse|15|11}}And with Absalom went two hundred men out of Jerusalem, that were invited, and went in their simplicity; and they knew not any thing. {{verse|15|12}}And Absalom sent for Ahithophel the Gilonite, David's counsellor, from his city, even from Giloh, while he offered the sacrifices. And the conspiracy was strong; for the people increased continually with Absalom.

{{verse|15|13}}And there came a messenger to David, saying: 'The hearts of the men of Israel are after Absalom.' {{verse|15|14}}And David said unto all his servants that were with him at Jerusalem: 'Arise, and let us flee; for else none of us shall escape from Absalom; make speed to depart, lest he overtake us quickly, and bring down evil upon us, and smite the city with the edge of the sword.' {{verse|15|15}}And the king's servants said unto the king: 'Behold, thy servants are ready to do whatsoever my lord the king shall choose.' {{verse|15|16}}And the king went forth, and all his household after him. And the king left ten women, that were concubines, to keep the house. {{verse|15|17}}And the king went forth, and all the people after him; and they tarried in Beth-merhak. {{verse|15|18}}And all his servants passed on beside him; and all the Cherethites, and all the Pelethites, and all the Gittites, six hundred men that came after him from Gath, passed on before the king.

{{verse|15|19}}Then said the king to Ittai the Gittite: 'Wherefore goest thou also with us? return, and abide with the king; for thou art a foreigner, and also an exile from thine own place. {{verse|15|20}}Whereas thou camest but yesterday, should I this day make thee go up and down with us, seeing I go whither I may? return thou, and take back thy brethren with thee in kindness and truth.' {{verse|15|21}}And Ittai answered the king, and said: 'As the {{sc|Lord}} liveth, and as my lord the king liveth, surely in what place my lord the king shall be, whether for death or for life, even there also will thy servant be.' {{verse|15|22}}And David said to Ittai: 'Go and pass over.'

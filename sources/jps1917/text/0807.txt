{{dropinitial|{{anchor|17:1}}17}} A Prayer of David.


:Hear the right, O {{sc|Lord}}, attend unto my cry;
:Give ear unto my prayer from lips without deceit.
:{{verse|17|2|align=float}}Let my judgment come forth from Thy presence; 
:Let Thine eyes behold equity.
:{{verse|17|3|align=float}}Thou hast tried my heart, Thou hast visited it in the night;
:Thou hast tested me, and Thou findest not
:That I had a thought which should not pass my mouth.
:{{verse|17|4|align=float}}As for the doings of men, by the word of Thy lips
:I have kept me from the ways of the violent.
:{{verse|17|5|align=float}}My steps have held fast to Thy paths,
:My feet have not slipped.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|17|6|align=float}}As for me, I call upon Thee, for Thou wilt answer me, O God;
:Incline Thine ear unto me, hear my speech.
:{{verse|17|7|align=float}}Make passing great Thy mercies, O Thou that savest by Thy right hand
:From assailants them that take refuge in Thee.
:{{verse|17|8|align=float}}Keep me as the apple of the eye,
:Hide me in the shadow of Thy wings,
:{{verse|17|9|align=float}}From the wicked that oppress,
:My deadly enemies, that compass me about.
:{{verse|17|10|align=float}}Their gross heart they have shut tight,
:With their mouth they speak proudly.
:{{verse|17|11|align=float}}At our every step they have now encompassed us;
:They set their eyes to cast us down to the earth.
:{{verse|17|12|align=float}}He is like a lion that is eager to tear in pieces,
:And like a young lion lurking in secret places.
{{dhr|100%}}
:{{verse|17|13|align=float}}Arise, O {{sc|Lord}}, confront him, cast him down;
:Deliver my soul from the wicked, by Thy sword;
:{{verse|17|14|align=float}}From men, by Thy hand, O {{sc|Lord}},
:From men of the world, whose portion is in this life,
:And whose belly Thou fillest with Thy treasure;
:Who have children in plenty,
:And leave their abundance to their babes.
:{{verse|17|15|align=float}}As for me, I shall behold Thy face in righteousness;
:I shall be satisfied, when I awake, with Thy likeness.

{{verse|15|8}}And when Asa heard these words, even the prophecy of Oded the prophet, he took courage, and put away the detestable things out of all the land of Judah and Benjamin, and out of the cities which he had taken from the hill-country of Ephraim; and he renewed the altar of the {{sc|Lord}}, that was before the porch of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|15|9}}And he gathered all Judah and Benjamin, and them that sojourned with them out of Ephraim and Manasseh, and out of Simeon; for they fell to him out of Israel in abundance, when they saw that the {{sc|Lord}} his God was with him. {{verse|15|10}}So they gathered themselves together at Jerusalem in the third month, in the fifteenth year of the reign of Asa. {{verse|15|11}}And they sacrificed unto the {{sc|Lord}} in that day, of the spoil which they had brought, seven hundred oxen and seven thousand sheep. {{verse|15|12}}And they entered into the covenant to seek the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers, with all their heart and with all their soul; {{verse|15|13}}and that whosoever would not seek the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, should be put to death, whether small or great, whether man or woman. {{verse|15|14}}And they swore unto the {{sc|Lord}} with a loud voice, and with shouting, and with trumpets, and with horns. {{verse|15|15}}And all Judah rejoiced at the oath; for they had sworn with all their heart, and sought Him with their whole desire; and He was found of them; and the {{sc|Lord}} gave them rest round about.

{{verse|15|16}}And also Maacah the mother of Asa the king, he removed her from being queen, because she had made an abominable image for an Asherah; and Asa cut down her image, and made dust of it, and burnt it at the brook Kidron. {{verse|15|17}}But the high places were not taken away out of Israel; nevertheless the heart of Asa was whole all his days. {{verse|15|18}}And he brought into the house of God the things that his father had hallowed, and that he himself had hallowed, silver, and gold, and vessels. {{verse|15|19}}And there was no more war unto the five and thirtieth year of the reign of Asa.

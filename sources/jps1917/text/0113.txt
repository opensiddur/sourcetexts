{{verse|chapter=21|verse=35}}And if one man's ox hurt another's, so that it dieth; then they shall sell the live ox, and divide the price of it; and the dead also they shall divide. {{verse|chapter=21|verse=36}}Or if it be known that the ox was wont to gore in time past, and its owner hath not kept it in; he shall surely pay ox for ox, and the dead beast shall be his own.

{{verse|chapter=21|verse=37}}If a man steal an ox, or a sheep, and kill it, or sell it, he shall pay five oxen for an ox, and four sheep for a sheep. {{dropinitial|{{anchor+|22:1|22}}}}{{verse|chapter=22|verse=1|id=}}If a thief be found breaking in, and be smitten so that he dieth, there shall be no bloodguiltiness for him. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=2}}If the sun be risen upon him, there shall be bloodguiltiness for him—he shall make restitution; if he have nothing, then he shall be sold for his theft. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=3}}If the theft be found in his hand alive, whether it be ox, or ass, or sheep, he shall pay double.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=4}}If a man cause a field or vineyard to be eaten, and shall let his beast loose, and it feed in another man's field; of the best of his own field, and of the best of his own vineyard, shall he make restitution.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=5}}If fire break out, and catch in thorns, so that the shocks of corn, or the standing corn, or the field are consumed; he that kindled the fire shall surely make restitution.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=6}}If a man deliver unto his neighbour money or stuff to keep, and it be stolen out of the man's house; if the thief be found, he shall pay double. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=7}}If the thief be not found, then the master of the house shall come near unto <ref name="judges">That is, the judges.</ref>God, to see whether he have not put his hand unto his neighbour's goods. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=8}}For every matter of trespass, whether it be for ox, for ass, for sheep, for raiment, or for any manner of lost thing, whereof one saith: 'This is it,' the cause of both parties shall come before <ref name="judges" />God; he whom <ref name="judges" />God shall condemn shall pay double unto his neighbour.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=9}}If a man deliver unto his neighbour an ass, or an ox, or a sheep, or any beast, to keep, and it die, or be hurt, or driven away, no man seeing it; {{verse|chapter=22|verse=10}}the oath of the {{sc|Lord}} shall be between them both, to see whether he have not put his hand unto his neighbour's goods; and the owner thereof shall accept it, and he shall not make restitution. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=11}}But if it be stolen from him, he shall make restitution unto the owner thereof. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=12}}If it be torn in pieces, let him bring it for witness; he shall not make good that which was torn.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=13}}And if a man borrow aught of his neighbour, and it be hurt, or die, the owner thereof not being with it, he shall surely make restitution. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=14}}If the owner thereof be with it, he shall not make it good; if it be a hireling, he loseth his hire.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=15}}And if a man entice a virgin that is not betrothed, and lie with her, he shall surely pay a dowry for her to be his wife. {{verse|chapter=22|verse=16}}If her father utterly refuse to give her unto him, he shall pay money according to the dowry of virgins.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=17}}Thou shalt not suffer a sorceress to live.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=18}}Whosoever lieth with a beast shall surely be put to death.

{{verse|chapter=22|verse=19}}He that sacrificeth unto the gods, save unto the {{sc|Lord}} only, shall be utterly destroyed.

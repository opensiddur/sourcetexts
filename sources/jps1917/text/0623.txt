<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh||JEREMIAH|{{larger|28}}.1}}{{rule}}</noinclude>Me. {{verse|27|6}}And now have I given all these lands into the hand of Nebuchadnezzar the king of Babylon, My servant; and the beasts of the field also have I given him to serve him. {{verse|27|7}}And all the nations shall serve him, and his son, and his son's son, until the time of his own land come; and then many nations and great kings shall make him their bondman. {{verse|27|8}}And it shall come to pass, that the nation and the kingdom which will not serve the same Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon, and that will not put their neck under the yoke of the king of Babylon, that nation will I visit, saith the {{sc|Lord}}, with the sword, and with the famine, and with the pestilence, until I have consumed them by his hand. {{verse|27|9}}But as for you, hearken ye not to your prophets, nor to your diviners, nor to your dreams, nor to your soothsayers, nor to your sorcerers, that speak unto you, saying: Ye shall not serve the king of Babylon; {{verse|27|10}}for they prophesy a lie unto you, to remove you far from your land; and that I should drive you out and ye should perish. {{verse|27|11}}But the nation that shall bring their neck under the yoke of the king of Babylon, and serve him, that nation will I let remain in their own land, saith the {{sc|Lord}}; and they shall till it, and dwell therein.'

{{verse|27|12}}And I spoke to Zedekiah king of Judah according to all these words, saying: 'Bring your necks under the yoke of the king of Babylon, and serve him and his people, and live. {{verse|27|13}}Why will ye die, thou and thy people, by the sword, by the famine, and by the pestilence, as the {{sc|Lord}} hath spoken concerning the nation that will not serve the king of Babylon? {{verse|27|14}}And hearken not unto the words of the prophets that speak unto you, saying: Ye shall not serve the king of Babylon, for they prophesy a lie unto you. {{verse|27|15}}For I have not sent them, saith the {{sc|Lord}}, and they prophesy falsely in My name; that I might drive you out, and that ye might perish, ye, and the prophets that prophesy unto you.'

{{verse|27|16}}Also I spoke to the priests and to all this people, saying: 'Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: Hearken not to the words of your prophets that prophesy unto you, saying: Behold, the vessels of the {{sc|Lord's}} house shall now shortly be brought back from Babylon; for they prophesy a lie unto you. {{verse|27|17}}Hearken not unto them; serve the king of Babylon, and live; wherefore should this city become desolate? {{verse|27|18}}But if they be prophets, and if the word of the {{sc|Lord}} be with them, let them now make intercession to the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts, that the vessels which are left in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and in the house of the king of Judah, and at Jerusalem, go not to Babylon. {{verse|27|19}}For thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts concerning the pillars, and concerning the sea, and concerning the bases, and concerning the residue of the vessels that remain in this city, {{verse|27|20}}which Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon took not, when he carried away captive Jeconiah the son of Jehoiakim, king of Judah, from Jerusalem to Babylon, and all the nobles of Judah and Jerusalem; {{verse|27|21}}yea, thus saith the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts, the God of Israel, concerning the vessels that remain in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and in the house of the king of Judah, and at Jerusalem: {{verse|27|22}}They shall be carried to Babylon, and there shall they be, until the day that I remember them, saith the {{sc|Lord}}, and bring them up, and restore them to this place.'

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|19:1}}19}}And Ahab told Jezebel all that Elijah had done, and withal how he had slain all the prophets with the sword. {{verse|19|2}}Then Jezebel sent a messenger unto Elijah, saying: 'So let the gods do [to me], and more also, if I make not thy life as the life of one of them by to-morrow about this time.' {{verse|19|3}}And when he saw that, he arose, and went for his life, and came to Beer-sheba, which belongeth to Judah, and left his servant there. {{verse|19|4}}But he himself went a day's journey into the wilderness, and came and sat down under a broom-tree; and he requested for himself that he might die; and said: 'It is enough; now, O {{sc|Lord}}, take away my life; for I am not better than my fathers.' {{verse|19|5}}And he lay down and slept under a broom-tree; and, behold, an angel touched him, and said unto him: 'Arise and eat.' {{verse|19|6}}And he looked, and, behold, there was at his head a cake baked on the hot stones, and a cruse of water. And he did eat and drink, and laid him down again. {{verse|19|7}}And the angel of the {{sc|Lord}} came again the second time, and touched him, and said: 'Arise and eat; because the journey is too great for thee.' {{verse|19|8}}And he arose, and did eat and drink, and went in the strength of that meal forty days and forty nights unto Horeb the mount of God.

{{verse|19|9}}And he came thither unto a cave, and lodged there; and, behold, the word of the {{sc|Lord}} came to him, and He said unto him: 'What doest thou here, Elijah?' {{verse|19|10}}And he said: 'I have been very jealous for the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of hosts; for the children of Israel have forsaken Thy covenant, thrown down Thine altars, and slain Thy prophets with the sword; and I, even I only, am left; and they seek my life, to take it away.' {{verse|19|11}}And He said: 'Go forth, and stand upon the mount before the {{sc|Lord}}.' And, behold, the {{sc|Lord}} passed by, and a great and strong wind rent the mountains, and broke in pieces the rocks before the {{sc|Lord}}; but the {{sc|Lord}} was not in the wind; and after the wind an earthquake; but the {{sc|Lord}} was not in the earthquake; {{verse|19|12}}and after the earthquake a fire; but the {{sc|Lord}} was not in the fire; and after the fire a still small voice. {{verse|19|13}}And it was so, when Elijah heard it, that he wrapped his face in his mantle, and went out, and stood in the entrance of the cave. And, behold, there came a voice unto him, and said: 'What doest thou here, Elijah?' {{verse|19|14}}And he said: 'I have been very jealous for the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of hosts; for the children of Israel have forsaken Thy covenant, thrown down Thine altars, and slain Thy prophets with the sword; and I, even I only, am left; and they seek my life, to take it away.'

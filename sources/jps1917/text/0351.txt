Now Israel went out against the Philistines to battle, and pitched beside Eben-ezer; and the Philistines pitched in Aphek. {{verse|4|2}}And the Philistines put themselves in array against Israel; and when the battle was spread, Israel was smitten before the Philistines; and they slew of the army in the field about four thousand men. {{verse|4|3}}And when the people were come into the camp, the elders of Israel said: 'Wherefore hath the {{sc|Lord}} smitten us to-day before the Philistines? Let us fetch the ark of the covenant of the {{sc|Lord}} out of Shiloh unto us, that He may come among us, and save us out of the hand of our enemies.' {{verse|4|4}}So the people sent to Shiloh, and they brought from thence the ark of the covenant of the {{sc|Lord}} of hosts, who sitteth upon the cherubim; and the two sons of Eli, Hophni and Phinehas, were there with the ark of the covenant of God. {{verse|4|5}}And when the ark of the covenant of the {{sc|Lord}} came into the camp, all Israel shouted with a great shout, so that the earth rang. {{verse|4|6}}And when the Philistines heard the noise of the shout, they said: 'What meaneth the noise of this great shout in the camp of the Hebrews?' And they said: 'Woe unto us! for there was not such a thing yesterday and the day before. {{verse|4|8}}Woe unto us! who shall deliver us out of the hand of these mighty gods? these are the gods that smote the Egyptians with all manner of plagues and in the wilderness. {{verse|4|9}}Be strong, and quit yourselves like men, O ye Philistines, that ye be not servants unto the Hebrews, as they have been to you; quit yourselves like men, and fight.' {{verse|4|10}}And the Philistines fought, and Israel was smitten, and they fled every man to his tent; and there was a very great slaughter; for there fell of Israel thirty thousand footmen. {{verse|4|11}}And the ark of God was taken; and the two sons of Eli, Hophni and Phinehas, were slain.

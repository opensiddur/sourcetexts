{{dropinitial|{{anchor|2:1}}2}} And in the second year of the reign of Nebuchadnezzar Nebuchadnezzar dreamed dreams; and his spirit was troubled, and his sleep broke from him. {{verse|2|2}}Then the king commanded to call the magicians, and the enchanters, and the sorcerers, and the Chaldeans, to tell the king his dreams. So they came and stood before the king. {{verse|2|3}}And the king said unto them: 'I have dreamed a dream, and my spirit is troubled to know the dream.' {{verse|2|4}}Then spoke the Chaldeans to the king in Aramaic: 'O king, live for ever! tell thy servants the dream, and we will declare the interpretation.' {{verse|2|5}}The king answered and said to the Chaldeans: 'The thing is certain with me; if ye make not known unto me the dream and the interpretation thereof, ye shall be cut in pieces, and your houses shall be made a dunghill. {{verse|2|6}}But if ye declare the dream and the interpretation thereof, ye shall receive of me gifts and rewards and great honour; only declare unto me the dream and the interpretation thereof.' {{verse|2|7}}They answered the second time and said: 'Let the king tell his servants the dream, and we will declare the interpretation.' {{verse|2|8}}The king answered and said: 'I know of a truth that ye would gain time, inasmuch as ye see the thing is certain with me, {{verse|2|9}}that, if ye make not known unto me the dream, there is but one law for you; and ye have agreed together to speak before me lying and corrupt words, till the time be changed; only tell me the dream, and I shall know that ye can declare unto me the interpretation thereof.' {{verse|2|10}}The Chaldeans answered before the king, and said: 'There is not a man upon the earth that can declare the king's matter; forasmuch as no great and powerful king hath asked such a thing of any magician, or enchanter, or Chaldean. {{verse|2|11}}And it is a hard thing that the king asketh, and there is none other that can declare it before the king, except the gods, whose dwelling is not with flesh.' {{verse|2|12}}For this cause the king was angry and very furious, and commanded to destroy all the wise men of Babylon. {{verse|2|13}}So the decree went forth, and the wise men were to be slain; and they sought Daniel and his companions to be slain.

{{verse|2|14}}Then Daniel returned answer with counsel and discretion to Arioch the captain of the king's guard, who was gone forth to slay the wise men of Babylon; {{verse|2|15}}he answered and said to Arioch the king's captain: 'Wherefore is the decree so peremptory from the king?' Then Arioch made the thing known to Daniel. {{verse|2|16}}Then Daniel went in, and desired of the king that he would give him time, that he might declare unto the king the interpretation.

{{verse|2|17}}Then Daniel went to his house, and made the thing known to Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah, his companions; {{verse|2|18}}that they might ask mercy of the God of heaven concerning this secret; that Daniel and his companions should not perish with the rest of the wise men of Babylon. {{verse|2|19}}Then was the secret revealed unto Daniel in a vision of the night.

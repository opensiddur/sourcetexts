{{dropinitial|{{anchor|9:1}}9}}Then the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'Go in unto Pharaoh, and tell him: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of the Hebrews: Let My people go, that they may serve Me. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=2}}For if thou refuse to let them go, and wilt hold them still, {{verse|chapter=9|verse=3}}behold, the hand of the {{sc|Lord}} is upon thy cattle which are in the field, upon the horses, upon the asses, upon the camels, upon the herds, and upon the flocks; there shall be a very grievous murrain. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=4}}And the {{sc|Lord}} shall make a division between the cattle of Israel and the cattle of Egypt; and there shall nothing die of all that belongeth to the children of Israel.' {{verse|chapter=9|verse=5}}And the {{sc|Lord}} appointed a set time, saying: 'To-morrow the {{sc|Lord}} shall do this thing in the land.' {{verse|chapter=9|verse=6}}And the {{sc|Lord}} did that thing on the morrow, and all the cattle of Egypt died; but of the cattle of the children of Israel died not one. {{verse|chapter=9|verse=7}}And Pharaoh sent, and, behold, there was not so much as one of the cattle of the Israelites dead.

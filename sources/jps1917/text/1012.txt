{{verse|4|4}}Again, I considered all labour and all excelling in work, that it is a man's rivalry with his neighbour. This also is vanity and a striving after wind.

:{{verse|4|5|align=float}}The fool foldeth his hands together,
:And eateth his own flesh.
:{{verse|4|6|align=float}}Better is a handful of quietness,
:Than both the hands full of labour and striving after wind.

{{verse|4|7}}Then I returned and saw vanity under the sun. {{verse|4|8}}There is one that is alone, and he hath not a second; yea, he hath neither son nor brother; yet is there no end of all his labour, neither is his eye satisfied with riches: 'for whom then do I labour, and bereave my soul of pleasure?' This also is vanity, yea, it is a grievous business. {{verse|4|9}}Two are better than one; because they have a good reward for their labour. {{verse|4|10}}For if they fall, the one will lift up his fellow; but woe to him that is alone when he falleth, and hath not another to lift him up. {{verse|4|11}}Again, if two lie together, then they have warmth; but how can one be warm alone? {{verse|4|12}}And if a man prevail against him that is alone, two shall withstand him; and a threefold cord is not quickly broken.

{{verse|4|13}}Better is a poor and wise child than an old and foolish king, who knoweth not how to receive admonition any more. {{verse|4|14}}For out of prison he came forth to be king; although in his kingdom he was born poor. {{verse|4|15}}I saw all the living that walk under the sun, that they were with the child, the second, that was to stand up in his stead. {{verse|4|16}}There was no end of all the people, even of all them whom he did lead; yet they that come after shall not rejoice in him. Surely this also is vanity and a striving after wind.

{{verse|4|17}}Guard thy foot when thou goest to the house of God, and be ready to hearken: it is better than when fools give sacrifices; for they know not that they do evil.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|5:1}}5}} Be not rash with thy mouth, and let not thy heart be hasty to utter a word before God; for God is in heaven, and thou upon earth; therefore let thy words be few.

:{{verse|5|2|align=float}}For a dream cometh through a multitude of business;
:And a fool's voice through a multitude of words.

{{verse|5|3}}When thou vowest a vow unto God, defer not to pay it; for He hath no pleasure in fools; pay that which thou vowest. {{verse|5|4}}Better is it that thou shouldest not vow, than that thou shouldest vow and not pay. {{verse|5|5}}Suffer not thy mouth to bring thy flesh into guilt, neither say thou before the messenger, that it was an error; wherefore should God be angry at thy voice, and destroy the work of thy hands? {{verse|5|6}}For through the multitude of dreams and vanities there are also many words; but fear thou God.

{{verse|5|7}}If thou seest the oppression of the poor, and the violent perverting of justice and righteousness in the state, marvel not at the matter; for one higher than the high watcheth, and there are higher than they. {{verse|5|8}}But the profit of a land every way is a king that maketh himself servant to the field.

{{verse|5|9}}He that loveth silver shall not be satisfied with silver; nor he that loveth abundance, with increase; this also is vanity. {{verse|5|10}}When goods increase, they are increased that eat them; and what advantage is there to the owner thereof, saving the beholding of them with his eyes?

{{verse|chapter=17|verse=3}}What man soever there be of the house of Israel, that killeth an ox, or lamb, or goat, in the camp, or that killeth it without the camp, {{verse|chapter=17|verse=4}}and hath not brought it unto the door of the tent of meeting, to present it as an offering unto the {{sc|Lord}} before the tabernacle of the {{sc|Lord}}, blood shall be imputed unto that man; he hath shed blood; and that man shall be cut off from among his people. {{verse|chapter=17|verse=5}}To the end that the children of Israel may bring their sacrifices, which they sacrifice in the open field, even that they may bring them unto the {{sc|Lord}}, unto the door of the tent of meeting, unto the priest, and sacrifice them for sacrifices of peace-offerings unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=17|verse=6}}And the priest shall dash the blood against the altar of the {{sc|Lord}} at the door of the tent of meeting, and make the fat smoke for a sweet savour unto the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=17|verse=7}}And they shall no more sacrifice their sacrifices unto the satyrs, after whom they go astray. This shall be a statute for ever unto them throughout their generations.

{{verse|chapter=17|verse=8}}And thou shalt say unto them: Whatsoever man there be of the house of Israel, or of the strangers that sojourn among them, that offereth a burnt-offering or sacrifice, {{verse|chapter=17|verse=9}}and bringeth it not unto the door of the tent of meeting, to sacrifice it unto the {{sc|Lord}}, even that man shall be cut off from his people.

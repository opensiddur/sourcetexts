{{verse|chapter=31|verse=13}}And Moses, and Eleazar the priest, and all the princes of the congregation, went forth to meet them without the camp. {{verse|chapter=31|verse=14}}And Moses was wroth with the officers of the host, the captains of thousands and the captains of hundreds, who came from the service of the war. {{verse|chapter=31|verse=15}}And Moses said unto them: 'Have ye saved all the women alive? {{verse|chapter=31|verse=16}}Behold, these caused the children of Israel, through the counsel of Balaam, to revolt so as to break faith with the {{sc|Lord}} in the matter of Peor, and so the plague was among the congregation of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=31|verse=17}}Now therefore kill every male among the little ones, and kill every woman that hath known man by lying with him. {{verse|chapter=31|verse=18}}But all the women children, that have not known man by lying with him, keep alive for yourselves. {{verse|chapter=31|verse=19}}And encamp ye without the camp seven days; whosoever hath killed any person, and whosoever hath touched any slain, purify yourselves on the third day and on the seventh day, ye and your captives. {{verse|chapter=31|verse=20}}And as to every garment, and all that is made of skin, and all work of goats' hair, and all things made of wood, ye shall purify.'

{{verse|chapter=31|verse=21}}And Eleazar the priest said unto the men of war that went to the battle: 'This is the statute of the law which the {{sc|Lord}} hath commanded Moses: {{verse|chapter=31|verse=22}}Howbeit the gold, and the silver, the brass, the iron, the tin, and the lead, {{verse|chapter=31|verse=23}}every thing that may abide the fire, ye shall make to go through the fire, and it shall be clean; nevertheless it shall be purified with the water of sprinkling; and all that abideth not the fire ye shall make to go through the water. {{verse|chapter=31|verse=24}}And ye shall wash your clothes on the seventh day, and ye shall be clean, and afterward ye may come into the camp.'

{{verse|chapter=31|verse=25}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Moses, saying: {{verse|chapter=31|verse=26}}'Take the sum of the prey that was taken, both of man and of beast, thou, and Eleazar the priest, and the heads of the fathers' houses of the congregation; {{verse|chapter=31|verse=27}}and divide the prey into two parts: between the men skilled in war, that went out to battle, and all the congregation; {{verse|chapter=31|verse=28}}and levy a tribute unto the {{sc|Lord}} of the men of war that went out to battle: one soul of five hundred, both of the persons, and of the beeves, and of the asses, and of the flocks; {{verse|chapter=31|verse=29}}take it of their half, and give it unto Eleazar the priest, as a portion set apart for the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|chapter=31|verse=30}}And of the children of Israel's half, thou shalt take one drawn out of every fifty, of the persons, of the beeves, of the asses, and of the flocks, even of all the cattle, and give them unto the Levites, that keep the charge of the tabernacle of the {{sc|Lord}}.' {{verse|chapter=31|verse=31}}And Moses and Eleazar the priest did as the {{sc|Lord}} {{hws|com|commanded}}<noinclude>{{c|203}}</noinclude>

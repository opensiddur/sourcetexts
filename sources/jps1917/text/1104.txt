{{verse|20|4}}And it came to pass after this, that there arose war at Gezer with the Philistines; then Sibbecai the Hushathite slew Sippai, of the sons of the giants; and they were subdued. {{verse|20|5}}And there was again war with the Philistines; and Elhanan the son of Jair slew Lahmi the brother of Goliath the Gittite, the staff of whose spear was like a weaver's beam. {{verse|20|6}}And there was again war at Gath, where was a man of great stature, whose fingers and toes were four and twenty, six [on each hand], and six [on each foot]; and he also was born unto the giant. {{verse|20|7}}And when he taunted Israel, Jonathan the son of Shimea David's brother slew him. {{verse|20|8}}These were born unto the giant in Gath; and they fell by the hand of David and by the hand of his servants.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|21:1}}21}} And Satan stood up against Israel, and moved David to number Israel. {{verse|21|2}}And David said to Joab and to the princes of the people: 'Go, number Israel from Beer-sheba even to Dan; and bring me word, that I may know the sum of them.' {{verse|21|3}}And Joab said: 'The {{sc|Lord}} make His people a hundred times so many more as they are; but, my lord the king, are they not all my lord's servants? why doth my lord require this thing? why will he be a cause of guilt unto Israel?' {{verse|21|4}}Nevertheless the king's word prevailed against Joab. Wherefore Joab departed and went throughout all Israel, and came to Jerusalem. {{verse|21|5}}And Joab gave up the sum of the numbering of the people unto David. And all they of Israel were a thousand thousand and a hundred thousand men that drew sword; and Judah was four hundred threescore and ten thousand men that drew sword. {{verse|21|6}}But Levi and Benjamin he did not number among them; for the king's word was abominable to Joab.

{{verse|21|7}}And God was displeased with this thing; therefore He smote Israel. {{verse|21|8}}And David said unto God: 'I have sinned greatly, in that I have done this thing; but now, put away, I beseech Thee, the iniquity of Thy servant; for I have done very foolishly.' {{verse|21|9}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke unto Gad, David's seer, saying: {{verse|21|10}}'Go and speak unto David, saying: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: I offer thee three things; choose thee one of them, that I may do it unto thee.' {{verse|21|11}}So Gad came to David, and said unto him: 'Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: Take which thou wilt: {{verse|21|12}}either three years of famine; or three months to be swept away before thy foes, while the sword of thine enemies overtaketh thee; or else three days the sword of the {{sc|Lord}}, even pestilence in the land, and the angel of the {{sc|Lord}} destroying throughout all the borders of Israel. Now therefore consider what answer I shall return to Him that sent me.' {{verse|21|13}}And David said unto Gad: 'I am in a great strait; let me fall now into the hand of the {{sc|Lord}}, for very great are His mercies; and let me not fall into the hand of man.'

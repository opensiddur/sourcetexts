{{verse|12|10}}And Jehoiada the priest took a chest, and bored a hole in the lid of it, and set it beside the altar, on the right side as one cometh into the house of the {{sc|Lord}}; and the priests that kept the threshold put therein all the money that was brought into the house of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|12|11}}And it was so, when they saw that there was much money in the chest, that the king's scribe and the high priest came up, and they put up in bags and counted the money that was found in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|12|12}}And they gave the money that was weighed out into the hands of them that did the work, that had the oversight of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}; and they paid it out to the carpenters and the builders, that wrought upon the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, {{verse|12|13}}and to the masons and the hewers of stone, and for buying timber and hewn stone to repair the breaches of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and for all that was laid out for the house to repair it. {{verse|12|14}}But there were not made for the house of the {{sc|Lord}} cups of silver, snuffers, basins, trumpets, any vessels of gold, or vessels of silver, of the money that was brought into the house of the {{sc|Lord}}; {{verse|12|15}}for they gave that to them that did the work, and repaired therewith the house of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|12|16}}Moreover they reckoned not with the men, into whose hand they delivered the money to give to them that did the work; for they dealt faithfully. {{verse|12|17}}The forfeit money, and the sin money, was not brought into the house of the {{sc|Lord}}; it was the priests.

{{verse|12|18}}Then Hazael king of Aram went up, and fought against Gath, and took it; and Hazael set his face to go up to Jerusalem. {{verse|12|19}}And Jehoash king Judah took all the hallowed things that Jehoshaphat, and Jehoram, and Ahaziah, his fathers, kings of Judah, had dedicated, and his own hallowed things, and all the gold that was found in the treasures of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and of the king's house, and sent it to Hazael king of Aram; and he went away from Jerusalem.

{{verse|12|20}}Now the rest of the acts of Joash, and all that he did, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Judah? {{verse|12|21}}And his servants arose, and made a conspiracy, and smote Joash at Beth-millo, on the way that goeth down to Silla. {{verse|12|22}} For Jozacar the son of Shimeath, and Jehozabad the son of Shomer, his servants, smote him, and he died; and they buried him with his fathers in the city of David; and Amaziah his son reigned in his stead.

{{verse|2|10}}And David slept with his fathers, and was buried in the city of David. {{verse|2|11}}And the days that David reigned over Israel were forty years: seven years reigned he in Hebron, and thirty and three years reigned he in Jerusalem. {{verse|2|12}}And Solomon sat upon the throne of David his father; and his kingdom was established firmly.

{{verse|2|13}}Then Adonijah the son of Haggith came to Bath-sheba the mother of Solomon. And she said: 'Comest thou peaceably?' And he said: 'Peaceably.' {{verse|2|14}}He said moreover: 'I have somewhat to say unto thee.' And she said: 'Say on.' {{verse|2|15}}And he said: 'Thou knowest that the kingdom was mine, and that all Israel set their faces on me, that I should reign; howbeit the kingdom is turned about, and is become my brother's; for it was his from the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|2|16}}And now I ask one petition of thee, deny me not.' And she said unto him: 'Say on.' {{verse|2|17}}And he said: 'Speak, I pray thee, unto Solomon the king—for he will not say thee nay—that he give me Abishag the Shunammite to wife.' {{verse|2|18}}And Bath-sheba said: 'Well; I will speak for thee unto the king.'

{{verse|2|19}}Bath-sheba therefore went unto king Solomon, to speak unto him for Adonijah. And the king rose up to meet her, and bowed down unto her, and sat down on his throne, and caused a throne to be set for the king's mother; and she sat on his right hand. {{verse|2|20}}Then she said: 'I ask one small petition of thee; deny me not.' And the king said unto her: 'Ask on, my mother; for I will not deny thee.' {{verse|2|21}}And she said: 'Let Abishag the Shunammite be given to Adonijah thy brother to wife.' {{verse|2|22}}And king Solomon answered and said unto his mother: 'And why dost thou ask Abishag the Shunammite for Adonijah? ask for him the kingdom also; for he is mine elder brother; even for him, and for Abiathar the priest, and for Joab the son of Zeruiah.' {{verse|2|23}}Then king Solomon swore by the {{sc|Lord}}, saying: 'God do so to me, and more also, if Adonijah have not spoken this word against his own life. {{verse|2|24}}Now therefore as the {{sc|Lord}} liveth, who hath established me, and set me on the throne of David my father, and who hath made me a house, as He promised, surely Adonijah shall be put to death this day.' {{verse|2|25}}And king Solomon sent by the hand of Benaiah the son of Jehoiada; and he fell upon him, so that he died.

{{verse|2|26}}And unto Abiathar the priest said the king: 'Get thee to Anathoth, unto thine own fields; for thou art deserving of death; but I will not at this time put thee to death, because thou didst bear the ark of the Lord {{sc|God}} before David my father, and because thou wast afflicted in all wherein my father was afflicted.' {{verse|2|27}}So Solomon thrust out Abiathar from being priest unto the {{sc|Lord}}; that the word of the {{sc|Lord}} might be fulfilled, which He spoke concerning the house of Eli in Shiloh.

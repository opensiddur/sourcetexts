<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh||SECOND KINGS|{{larger|16}}.15}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{verse|15|32}}In the second year of Pekah the son of Remaliah king of Israel began Jotham the son of Uzziah king of Judah to reign. {{verse|15|33}}Five and twenty years old was he when he began to reign; and he reigned sixteen years in Jerusalem; and his mother's name was Jerusha the daughter of Zadok. {{verse|15|34}}And he did that which was right in the eyes of the {{sc|Lord}}; he did according to all that his father Uzziah had done. {{verse|15|35}}Howbeit the high places were not taken away; the people still sacrificed and offered in the high places. He built the upper gate of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|15|36}}Now the rest of the acts of Jotham, and all that he did, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Judah? {{verse|15|37}}In those days the {{sc|Lord}} began to send against Judah Rezin the king of Aram, and Pekah the son of Remaliah. {{verse|15|38}}And Jotham slept with his fathers, and was buried with his fathers in the city of David his father; and Ahaz his son reigned in his stead.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|16:1}}16}}In the seventeenth year of Pekah the son of Remaliah Ahaz the son of Jotham king of Judah began to reign. {{verse|16|2}}Twenty years old was Ahaz when he began to reign; and he reigned sixteen years in Jerusalem; and he did not that which was right in the eyes of the {{sc|Lord}} his God, like David his father. {{verse|16|3}}But he walked in the way of the kings of Israel, yea, and made his son to pass through the fire, according to the abominations of the heathen, whom the {{sc|Lord}} cast out from before the children of Israel. {{verse|16|4}}And he sacrificed and offered in the high places, and on the hills, and under every leafy tree.

{{verse|16|5}}Then Rezin king of Aram and Pekah son of Remaliah king of Israel came up to Jerusalem to war; and they besieged Ahaz, but could not overcome him. {{verse|16|6}}At that time Rezin king of Aram recovered Elath to Aram, and drove the Jews from <ref>Heb. ''Eloth.''</ref>Elath; and the Edomites came to Elath, and dwelt there, unto this day.

{{verse|16|7}}So Ahaz sent messengers to Tiglath-pileser king of Assyria, saying: 'I am thy servant and thy son; come up, and save me out of the hand of the king of Aram, and out of the hand of the king of Israel, who rise up against me.' {{verse|16|8}}And Ahaz took the silver and gold that was found in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and in the treasures of the king's house, and sent it for a present to the king of Assyria. {{verse|16|9}}And the king of Assyria hearkened unto him; and the king of Assyria went up against Damascus, and took it, and carried the people of it captive to Kir, and slew Rezin.

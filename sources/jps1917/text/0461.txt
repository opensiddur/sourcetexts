{{verse|22|24}}Then Zedekiah the son of Chenaanah came near, and smote Micaiah on the check, and said: 'Which way went the spirit of the {{sc|Lord}} from me to speak unto thee?' {{verse|22|25}}And Micaiah said: 'Behold, thou shalt see on that day, when thou shalt go into an inner chamber to hide thyself.' {{verse|22|26}}And the king of Israel said: 'Take Micaiah, and carry him back unto Amon the governor of the city, and to Joash the king's son; {{verse|22|27}}and say: Thus saith the king: Put this fellow in the prison, and feed him with scant bread and with scant water, until I come in peace.' {{verse|22|28}}And Micaiah said: 'If thou return at all in peace, the {{sc|Lord}} hath not spoken by me.' And he said: 'Hear, ye peoples, all of you.'

{{verse|22|29}}So the king of Israel and Jehoshaphat the king of Judah went up to Ramoth-gilead. {{verse|22|30}}And the king of Israel said unto Jehoshaphat: 'I will disguise myself, and go into the battle; but put thou on thy robes.' And the king of Israel disguised himself, and went into the battle. {{verse|22|31}}Now the king of Aram had commanded the thirty and two captains of his chariots, saying: 'Fight neither with small nor great, save only with the king of Israel.' {{verse|22|32}}And it came to pass, when the captains of the chariots saw Jehoshaphat, that they said: 'Surely it is the king of Israel'; and they turned aside to fight against him; and Jehoshaphat cried out. {{verse|22|33}}And it came to pass, when the captains of the chariots saw that it was not the king of Israel, that they turned back from pursuing him. {{verse|22|34}}And a certain man drew his bow at a venture, and smote the king of Israel between the lower armour and the breastplate; wherefore he said unto the driver of his chariot: 'Turn thy hand, and carry me out of the host; for I am sore wounded.' {{verse|22|35}}And the battle increased that day; and the king was stayed up in his chariot against the Arameans, and died at even; and the blood ran out of the wound into the bottom of the chariot. {{verse|22|36}}And there went a cry throughout the host about the going down of the sun, saying: 'Every man to his city, and every man to his country.'

{{verse|22|37}}So the king died, and was brought to Samaria; and they buried the king in Samaria. {{verse|22|38}}And they washed the chariot by the pool of Samaria; and the dogs licked up his blood; the harlots also washed themselves there; according unto the word of the {{sc|Lord}} which He spoke.

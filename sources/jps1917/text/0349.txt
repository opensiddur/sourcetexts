{{verse|2|11}}And Elkanah went to Ramah to his house. And the child did minister unto the {{sc|Lord}} before Eli the priest.

{{verse|2|12}}Now the sons of Eli were base men; they knew not the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|2|13}}And the custom of the priests with the people was, that, when any man offered sacrifice, the priest's servant came, while the flesh was in seething, with a flesh-hook of three teeth in his band; {{verse|2|14}}and he struck it into the pan, or kettle, or caldron, or pot; all that the flesh-hook brought up the priest took therewith. So they did unto all the Israelites that came thither in Shiloh. {{verse|2|15}}Yea, before the fat was made to smoke, the priest's servant came, and said to the man that sacrificed: 'Give flesh to roast for the priest; for he will not have sodden flesh of thee, but raw.' {{verse|2|16}}And if the man said unto him: 'Let the fat be made to smoke first of all, and then take as much as thy soul desireth'; then he would say: 'Nay, but thou shalt give it me now; and if not, I will take it by force.' {{verse|2|17}}And the sin of the young men was very great before the {{sc|Lord}}; for the men dealt contemptuously with the offering of the {{sc|Lord}}.

{{verse|2|18}}But Samuel ministered before the {{sc|Lord}}, being a child, girded with a linen ephod. {{verse|2|19}}Moreover his mother made him a little robe, and brought it to him from year to year, when she came up with her husband to offer the yearly sacrifice. {{verse|2|20}}And Eli would bless Elkanah and his wife, and say: 'The {{sc|Lord}} give thee seed of this woman for the loan which was lent to the {{sc|Lord}}.' And they would go unto their own home. {{verse|2|21}}So the {{sc|Lord}} remembered Hannah, and she conceived, and bore three sons and two daughters. And the child Samuel grew before the {{sc|Lord}}.

{{verse|2|22}}Now Eli was very old; and he heard all that his sons did unto all Israel, and how that they lay with the women that did service at the door of the tent of meeting. {{verse|2|23}}And he said unto them: 'Why do ye such things? for I hear evil reports concerning you from all this people. {{verse|2|24}}Nay, my sons; for it is no good report which I hear the {{sc|Lord's}} people do spread abroad. {{verse|2|25}}If one man sin against another, God shall judge him; but if a man sin against the {{sc|Lord}}, who shall entreat for him?' But they hearkened not unto the voice of their father, because the {{sc|Lord}} would slay them. {{verse|2|26}}And the child Samuel grew on, and increased in favour both with the {{sc|Lord}}, and also with men.

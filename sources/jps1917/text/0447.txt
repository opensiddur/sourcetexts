{{verse|13|33}}After this thing Jeroboam returned not from his evil way, but made again from among all the people priests of the high places; whosoever would, he consecrated him, that he might be one of the priests of the high places. {{verse|13|34}}And by this thing there was sin unto the house of Jeroboam, even to cut it off, and to destroy it from off the face of the earth.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|14:1}}14}}At that time Abijah the son of Jeroboam fell sick. {{verse|14|2}}And Jeroboam said to his wife: 'Arise, I pray thee, and disguise thyself, that thou be not known to be the wife of Jeroboam; and get thee to Shiloh; behold, there is Ahijah the prophet, who spoke concerning me that I should be king over this people. {{verse|14|3}}And take with thee ten loaves, and biscuits, and a cruse of honey, and go to him; he will tell thee what shall become of the child.' {{verse|14|4}}And Jeroboam's wife did so, and arose, and went to Shiloh, and came to the house of Ahijah. Now Ahijah could not see; for his eyes were set by reason of his age. {{verse|14|5}}Now the {{sc|Lord}} had said unto Ahijah: 'Behold, the wife of Jeroboam cometh to inquire of thee concerning her son; for he is sick; thus and thus shalt thou say unto her; for it will be, when she cometh in, that she will feign herself to be another woman.'

{{verse|14|6}}And it was so, when Ahijah heard the sound of her feet, as she came in at the door, that he said: 'Come in, thou wife of Jeroboam; why feignest thou thyself to be another? for I am sent to thee with heavy tidings. {{verse|14|7}}Go, tell Jeroboam: Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel: Forasmuch as I exalted thee from among the people, and made thee prince over My people Israel, {{verse|14|8}}and rent the kingdom away from the house of David, and gave it thee; and yet thou hast not been as My servant David, who kept My commandments, and who followed Me with all his heart, to do that only which was right in Mine eyes; {{verse|14|9}}but hast done evil above all that were before thee, and hast gone and made thee other gods, and molten images, to provoke Me, and hast cast Me behind thy back; {{verse|14|10}}therefore, behold, I will bring evil upon the house of Jeroboam, and will cut off from Jeroboam every man-child, and him that is shut up and him that is left at large in Israel, and will utterly sweep away the house of Jeroboam, as a man sweepeth away dung, till it be all gone. {{verse|14|11}}Him that dieth of Jeroboam in the city shall the dogs eat; and him that dieth in the field shall the fowls of the air eat; for the {{sc|Lord}} hath spoken it. {{verse|14|12}}Arise thou therefore, get thee to thy house; and when thy feet enter into the city, the child shall die. {{verse|14|13}}And all Israel shall make lamentation for him, and bury him; for he only of Jeroboam shall come to the grave; because in him there is found some good thing toward the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, in the house of Jeroboam. {{verse|14|14}}Moreover the {{sc|Lord}} will raise Him up a king over Israel, who shall cut off the house of Jeroboam that day. But what is it even then? {{verse|14|15}}for the {{sc|Lord}} will smite Israel, as a reed is shaken in the water; and He will root up Israel out of this good land, which He gave to their fathers, and will scatter them beyond the River; because they have made their Asherim, provoking the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|14|16}}And He will give Israel up because of the sins of Jeroboam, which he hath sinned, and wherewith he hath made Israel to sin.'

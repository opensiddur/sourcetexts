{{dropinitial|{{anchor|18:1}}18}} Now Jehoshaphat had riches and honour in abundance; and he allied himself with Ahab by marriage. {{verse|18|2}}And after a lapse of years he went down to Ahab to Samaria. And Ahab killed sheep and oxen for him in abundance, and for the people that were with him, and persuaded him to go up with him to Ramoth-gilead. {{verse|18|3}}And Ahab king of Israel said unto Jehoshaphat king of Judah: 'Wilt thou go with me to Ramoth-gilead?' And he answered him: 'I am as thou art, and my people as thy people; and we will be with thee in the war.'

{{verse|18|4}}And Jehoshaphat said unto the king of Israel: 'Inquire, I pray thee, at the word of the {{sc|Lord}} to-day.' {{verse|18|5}}Then the king of Israel gathered the prophets together, four hundred men, and said unto them: 'Shall we go to Ramoth-gilead to battle, or shall I forbear?' And they said: 'Go up; for God will deliver it into the hand of the king.' {{verse|18|6}}But Jehoshaphat said: 'Is there not here besides a prophet of the {{sc|Lord}}, that we might inquire of him?' {{verse|18|7}}And the king of Israel said unto Jehoshaphat: 'There is yet one man by whom we may inquire of the {{sc|Lord}}; but I hate him; for he never prophesieth good concerning me, but always evil; the same is Micaiah the son of Imla.' And Jehoshaphat said: 'Let not the king say so.' {{verse|18|8}}Then the king of Israel called an officer, and said: 'Fetch quickly Micaiah the son of Imla.' {{verse|18|9}}Now the king of Israel and Jehoshaphat the king of Judah sat each on his throne, arrayed in their robes, and they sat in a threshing-floor at the entrance of the gate of Samaria; and all the prophets were prophesying before them. {{verse|18|10}}And Zedekiah the son of Chenaanah made him horns of iron, and said: 'Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: With these shalt thou gore the Arameans, until they be consumed.' {{verse|18|11}}And all the prophets prophesied so, saying: 'Go up to Ramoth-gilead, and prosper; for the {{sc|Lord}} will deliver it into the hand of the king.'

<noinclude><pagequality level="3" user="Outlier59" />{{rh||SECOND SAMUEL|{{larger|5}}.12}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{verse|4|5}}And the sons of Rimmon the Beerothite, Rechab and Baanah, went, and came about the heat of the day to the house of Ish-bosheth, as he took his rest at noon. {{verse|4|6}}And they came thither into the midst of the house, as though they would have fetched wheat; and they smote him in the groin; and Rechab and Baanah his brother escaped. {{verse|4|7}}Now when they came into the house, as he lay on his bed in his bed-chamber, they smote him, and slew him, and beheaded him, and took his head, and went by the way of the Arabah all night. {{verse|4|8}}And they brought the head of Ish-bosheth unto David to Hebron, and said to the king: 'Behold the head of Ish-bosheth the son of Saul thine enemy, who sought thy life; and the {{sc|Lord}} hath avenged my lord the king this day of Saul, and of his seed.' {{verse|4|9}}And David answered Rechab and Baanah his brother, the sons of Rimmon the Beerothite, and said unto them: 'As the {{sc|Lord}} liveth, who hath redeemed my soul out of all adversity, {{verse|4|10}}when one told me, saying: Behold, Saul is dead, and he was in his own eyes as though he brought good tidings, I took hold of him, and slew him in Ziklag, instead of giving a reward for his tidings. {{verse|4|11}}How much more, when wicked men have slain a righteous person in his own house upon his bed, shall I not now require his blood of your hand, and take you away from the earth?' {{verse|4|12}}And David commanded his young men, and they slew them, and cut off their hands and their feet, and hanged them up beside the pool in Hebron. But they took the head of Ish-bosheth, and buried it in the grave of Abner in Hebron.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|5:1}}5}}Then came all the tribes of Israel to David unto Hebron, and spoke, saying: 'Behold, we are thy bone and thy flesh. {{verse|5|2}}In times past, when Saul was king over us, it was thou that didst lead out and bring in Israel; and the {{sc|Lord}} said to thee: Thou shalt feed My people Israel, and thou shalt be prince over Israel.' {{verse|5|3}}So all the elders of Israel came to the king to Hebron; and king David made a covenant with them in Hebron before the {{sc|Lord}}; and they anointed David king over Israel.

{{verse|5|4}}David was thirty years old when he began to reign, and he reigned forty years. {{verse|5|5}}In Hebron he reigned over Judah seven years and six months; and in Jerusalem he reigned thirty and three years over all Israel and Judah.

{{verse|5|6}}And the king and his men went to Jerusalem against the Jebusites, the inhabitants of the land, who spoke unto David, saying: 'Except thou take away the blind and the lame, thou shalt not come in hither'; thinking: 'David cannot come in hither.' {{verse|5|7}}Nevertheless David took the stronghold of Zion; the same is the city of David. {{verse|5|8}}And David said on that day: 'Whosoever smiteth the Jebusites, and getteth up to the gutter, and [taketh away] the lame and the blind, that are hated of David's soul—.' Wherefore they say: 'There are the blind and the lame; he cannot come into the house.' {{verse|5|9}}And David dwelt in the stronghold, and called it the city of David. And David built round about from Millo and inward. {{verse|5|10}}And David waxed greater and greater; for the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of hosts, was with him.

{{verse|5|11}}And Hiram king of Tyre sent messengers to David, and cedar-trees, and carpenters, and masons; and they built David a house. {{verse|5|12}}And David perceived that the {{sc|Lord}} had established him king over Israel, and that He had exalted his kingdom for His people Israel's sake.

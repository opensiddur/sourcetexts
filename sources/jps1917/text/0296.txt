{{verse|10|34}}And Joshua passed from Lachish, and all Israel with him, unto Eglon; and they encamped against it, and fought against it. {{verse|10|35}}And they took it on that day, and smote it with the edge of the sword, and all the souls that were therein he utterly destroyed that day, according to all that he had done to Lachish.

{{verse|10|36}}And Joshua went up from Eglon, and all Israel with him, unto Hebron; and they fought against it. {{verse|10|37}}And they took it, and smote it with the edge of the sword, and the king thereof, and all the cities thereof, and all the souls that were therein; he left none remaining, according to all that he had done to Eglon; but he utterly destroyed it, and all the souls that were therein.

{{verse|10|38}}And Joshua turned back, and all Israel with him, to Debir; and fought against it. {{verse|10|39}}And he took it, and the king thereof, and all the cities thereof; and they smote them with the edge of the sword, and utterly destroyed all the souls that were therein; he left none remaining; as he had done to Hebron, so he did to Debir, and to the king thereof; as he had done also to Libnah, and to the king thereof.

{{verse|10|40}}So Joshua smote all the land, the hill-country, and the South, and the Lowland, and the slopes, and all their kings; he left none remaining; but he utterly destroyed all that breathed, as the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, commanded. {{verse|10|41}}And Joshua smote them from Kadesh-barnea even unto Gaza, and all the country of Goshen, even unto Gibeon. {{verse|10|42}}And all these kings and their land did Joshua take at one time, because the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, fought for Israel. {{verse|10|43}}And Joshua returned, and all Israel with him, unto the camp to Gilgal.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|11:1}}11}}And it came to pass, when Jabin king of Hazor heard thereof, that he sent to Jobab king of Madon, and to the king of Shimron, and to the king of Achshaph, {{verse|11|2}}and to the kings that were on the north, in the hill-country and in the Arabah south of Chinneroth, and in the Lowland, and in the regions of Dor on the west, {{verse|11|3}}to the Canaanite on the east and on the west, and the Amorite, and the Hittite, and the Perizzite, and the Jebusite in the hill-country, and the Hivite under Hermon in the land of Mizpah. {{verse|11|4}}And they went out, they and all their hosts with them, much people, even as the sand that is upon the sea-shore in multitude, with horses and chariots very many. {{verse|11|5}}And all these kings met together, and they came and pitched together at the waters of Merom, to fight with Israel.

{{verse|11|6}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Joshua: 'Be not afraid because of them; for to-morrow at this time will I deliver them up all slain before Israel; thou shalt hough their horses, and burn their chariots with fire.' {{verse|11|7}}So Joshua came, and all the people of war with him, against them by the waters of Merom suddenly, and fell upon them. {{verse|11|8}}And the {{sc|Lord}} delivered them into the hand of Israel, and they smote them, and chased them unto great Zidon, and unto Misrephoth-maim, and unto the valley of Mizpeh eastward; and they smote them, until they left them none remaining. {{verse|11|9}}And Joshua did unto them as the {{sc|Lord}} bade him; he houghed their horses, and burnt their chariots with fire.

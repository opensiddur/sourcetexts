<noinclude><pagequality level="1" user="Prosody" />{{rh||SECOND CHRONICLES|{{larger|11}}.18}}{{rule}}</noinclude>{{verse|10|12}}So Jeroboam and all the people came to Rehoboam the third day, as the king bade, saying: 'Come to me again the third day.' {{verse|10|13}}And the king answered them roughly; and king Rehoboam forsook the counsel of the old men, {{verse|10|14}}and spoke to them after the counsel of the young men, saying: '[My father] made your yoke heavy, but I will add thereto; my father chastised you with whips, but I will chastise you with scorpions.' {{verse|10|15}}So the king hearkened not unto the people; for it was brought about of God, that the {{sc|Lord}} might establish His word, which He spoke by the hand of Ahijah the Shilonite to Jeroboam the son of Nebat.

{{verse|10|16}}And when all Israel [saw] that the king hearkened not unto them, the people answered the king, saying: 'What portion have we in David? neither have we inheritance in the son of Jesse; every man to your tents, O Israel; now see to thine own house, David.' So all Israel departed unto their tents. {{verse|10|17}}But as for the children of Israel that dwelt in the cities of Judah, Rehoboam reigned over them. {{verse|10|18}}Then king Rehoboam sent Hadoram, who was over the levy; and the children of Israel stoned him with stones, so that he died. And king Rehoboam made speed to get him up to his chariot, to flee to Jerusalem. {{verse|10|19}}So Israel rebelled against the house of David unto this day.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|11:1}}11}} And when Rehoboam was come to Jerusalem, he assembled the house of Judah and Benjamin, a hundred and fourscore thousand chosen men, that were warriors, to fight against Israel, to bring the kingdom back to Rehoboam. {{verse|11|2}}But the word of the {{sc|Lord}} came to Shemaiah the man of God, saying: {{verse|11|3}}'Speak unto Rehoboam the son of Solomon, king of Judah, and to all Israel in Judah and Benjamin, saying: {{verse|11|4}}Thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: Ye shall not go up, nor fight against your brethren; return every man to his house, for this thing is of Me.' So they hearkened unto the words of the {{sc|Lord}}, and returned from going against Jeroboam.

{{verse|11|5}}And Rehoboam dwelt in Jerusalem, and built cities for defence in Judah. {{verse|11|6}}He built even Beth-lehem, and Etam, and Tekoa, {{verse|11|7}}and Beth-zur, and Soco, and Adullam, {{verse|11|8}}and Gath, and Mareshah, and Ziph, {{verse|11|9}}and Adoraim, and Lachish, and Azekah, {{verse|11|10}}and Zorah, and Aijalon, and Hebron, which are in Judah and in Benjamin, fortified cities. {{verse|11|11}}And he fortified the strongholds, and put captains in them, and store of victual, and oil and wine. {{verse|11|12}}And in every city he put shields and spears, and made them exceeding strong. And Judah and Benjamin adhered to him.

{{verse|11|13}}And the priests and the Levites that were in all Israel presented themselves to him out of all their border. {{verse|11|14}}For the Levites left their open land and their possession, and came to Judah and Jerusalem; for Jeroboam and his sons cast them off, that they should not execute the priest's office unto the {{sc|Lord}}; {{verse|11|15}}and he appointed him priests for the high places, and for the satyrs, and for the calves which he had made. {{verse|11|16}}And after them, out of all the tribes of Israel, such as set their hearts to seek the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, came to Jerusalem to sacrifice unto the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of their fathers. {{verse|11|17}}So they strengthened the kingdom of Judah, and made Rehoboam the son of Solomon strong, three years; for they walked three years in the way of David and Solomon.

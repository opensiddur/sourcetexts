{{dropinitial|{{anchor|13:1}}13}}And, behold, there came a man of God out of Judah by the word of the {{sc|Lord}} unto Beth-el; and Jeroboam was standing by the altar to offer. {{verse|13|2}}And he cried against the altar by the word of the {{sc|Lord}}, and said: 'O altar, altar, thus saith the {{sc|Lord}}: Behold, a son shall be born unto the house of David, Josiah by name; and upon thee shall he sacrifice the priests of the high places that offer upon thee, and men's bones shall they burn upon thee.' {{verse|13|3}}And he gave a sign the same day saying: 'This is the sign which the {{sc|Lord}} hath spoken: Behold, the altar shall be rent, and the ashes that are upon it shall be poured out.' {{verse|13|4}}And it came to pass, when the king heard the saying of the man of God, which he cried against the altar in Beth-el, that Jeroboam put forth his hand from the altar, saying: 'Lay hold on him.' And his hand, which he put forth against him, dried up, so that he could not draw it back to him. {{verse|13|5}}The altar also was rent, and the ashes poured out from the altar, according to the sign which the man of God had given by the word of the {{sc|Lord}}. {{verse|13|6}}And the king answered and said unto the man of God: 'Entreat now the favour of the {{sc|Lord}} thy God, and pray for me, that my hand may be restored me.' And the man of God entreated the {{sc|Lord}}, and the king's hand was restored him, and became as it was before. {{verse|13|7}}And the king said unto the man of God: 'Come home with me, and refresh thyself, and I will give thee a reward.' {{verse|13|8}}And the man of God said unto the king: 'If thou wilt give me half thy house, I will not go in with thee, neither will I eat bread nor drink water in this place. {{verse|13|9}}For it so was charged me by the word of the {{sc|Lord}}, saying: Thou shalt eat no bread, nor drink water, neither return by the way that thou camest.' {{verse|13|10}}So he went another way, and returned not by the way that he came to Beth-el.

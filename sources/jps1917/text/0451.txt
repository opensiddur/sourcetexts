{{verse|16|20}}Now the rest of the acts of Zimri, and his treason that he wrought, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?

{{verse|16|21}}Then were the people of Israel divided into two parts: half of the people followed Tibni the son of Ginath, to make him king; and half followed Omri. {{verse|16|22}}But the people that followed Omri prevailed against the people that followed Tibni the son of Ginath; so Tibni died, and Omri reigned.

{{verse|16|23}}In the thirty and first year of Asa king of Judah began Omri to reign over Israel, and reigned twelve years; six years reigned he in Tirzah. {{verse|16|24}}And he bought the hill Samaria of Shemer for two talents of silver; and he built on the hill, and called the name of the city which he built, after the name of Shemer, the owner of the hill, Samaria. {{verse|16|25}}And Omri did that which was evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}}, and dealt wickedly above all that were before him. {{verse|16|26}}For he walked in all the way of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, and in his sins wherewith he made Israel to sin, to provoke the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, with their vanities.

{{verse|16|27}}Now the rest of the acts of Omri which he did, and his might that he showed, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel? {{verse|16|28}}And Omri slept with his fathers, and was buried in Samaria; and Ahab his son reigned in his stead.

{{verse|16|29}}And in the thirty and eighth year of Asa king of Judah began Ahab the son of Omri to reign over Israel; and Ahab the son of Omri reigned over Israel in Samaria twenty and two years. {{verse|16|30}}And Ahab the son of Omri did that which was evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}} above all that were before him. {{verse|16|31}}And it came to pass, as if it had been a light thing for him to walk in the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, that he took to wife Jezebel the daughter of Ethbaal king of the Zidonians, and went and served Baal, and worshipped him. {{verse|16|32}}And he reared up an altar for Baal in the house of Baal, which he had built in Samaria. {{verse|16|33}}And Ahab made the Asherah; and Ahab did yet more to provoke the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, than all the kings of Israel that were before him.

{{verse|16|34}}In his days did Hiel the Bethelite build Jericho; with Abiram his first-born he laid the foundation thereof, and with his youngest son Segub he set up the gates thereof; according to the word of the {{sc|Lord}}, which He spoke by the hand of Joshua the son of Nun.

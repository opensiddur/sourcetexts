{{dropinitial|{{anchor|3:1}}3}}And the child Samuel ministered unto the {{sc|Lord}} before Eli. And the word of the {{sc|Lord}} was precious in those days; there was no frequent vision. {{verse|3|2}}And it came to pass at that time, when Eli was laid down in his place—now his eyes had begun to wax dim, that he could not see—{{verse|3|3}}and the lamp of God was not yet gone out, and Samuel was laid down to sleep in the temple of the {{sc|Lord}}, where the ark of God was, {{verse|3|4}}that the {{sc|Lord}} called Samuel; and he said: 'Here am I.' {{verse|3|5}}And he ran unto Eli, and said: 'Here am I; for thou didst call me.' And he said: 'I called not; lie down again.' And he went and lay down. {{verse|3|6}}And the {{sc|Lord}} called yet again Samuel. And Samuel arose and went to Eli, and said: 'Here am I; for thou didst call me.' And he answered: 'I called not, my son; lie down again.' {{verse|3|7}}Now Samuel did not yet know the {{sc|Lord}}, neither was the word of the {{sc|Lord}} yet revealed unto him. {{verse|3|8}}And the {{sc|Lord}} called Samuel again the third time. And he arose and went to Eli, and said: 'Here am I; for thou didst call me.' And Eli perceived that the {{sc|Lord}} was calling the child. {{verse|3|9}}Therefore Eli said unto Samuel: 'Go, lie down; and it shall be, if thou be called, that thou shalt say: Speak, {{sc|Lord}}; for Thy servant heareth.' So Samuel went and lay down in his place. {{verse|3|10}}And the {{sc|Lord}} came, and stood, and called as at other times: 'Samuel, Samuel.' Then Samuel said: 'Speak; for Thy servant heareth.' {{verse|3|11}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said to Samuel: 'Behold, I will do a thing in Israel, at which both the ears of every one that heareth it shall tingle. {{verse|3|12}}In that day I will perform against Eli all that I have spoken concerning his house, from the beginning even unto the end. {{verse|3|13}}For I have told him that I will judge his house for ever, for the iniquity, in that he knew that his sons did bring a curse upon themselves, and he rebuked them not. {{verse|3|14}}And therefore I have sworn unto the house of Eli, that the iniquity of Eli's house shall not be expiated with sacrifice nor offering for ever.' {{verse|3|15}}And Samuel lay until the morning, and opened the doors of the house of the {{sc|Lord}}. And Samuel feared to tell Eli the vision. {{verse|3|16}}Then Eli called Samuel, and said: 'Samuel, my son.'

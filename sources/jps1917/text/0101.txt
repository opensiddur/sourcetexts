{{verse|chapter=12|verse=29}}And it came to pass at midnight, that the {{sc|Lord}} smote all the first-born in the land of Egypt, from the first-born of Pharaoh that sat on his throne unto the first-born of the captive that was in the dungeon; and all the first-born of cattle. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=30}}And Pharaoh rose up in the night, he, and all his servants, and all the Egyptians; and there was a great cry in Egypt; for there was not a house where there was not one dead. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=31}}And he called for Moses and Aaron by night and said: 'Rise up, get you forth from among my people, both ye and the children of Israel; and go, serve the {{sc|Lord}}, as ye have said. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=32}}Take both your flocks and your herds, as ye have said, and be gone; and bless me also.' {{verse|chapter=12|verse=33}}And the Egyptians were urgent upon the people, to send them out of the land in haste; for they said: 'We are all dead men.' {{verse|chapter=12|verse=34}}And the people took their dough before it was leavened, their kneading-troughs being bound up in their clothes upon their shoulders. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=35}}And the children of Israel did according to the word of Moses; and they asked of the Egyptians jewels of silver, and jewels of gold, and raiment. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=36}}And the {{sc|Lord}} gave the people favour in the sight of the Egyptians, so that they let them have what they asked. And they despoiled the Egyptians.


{{verse|chapter=12|verse=37}}And the children of Israel journeyed from Rameses to Succoth, about six hundred thousand men on foot, beside children. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=38}}And a mixed multitude went up also with them; and flocks, and herds, even very much cattle. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=39}}And they baked unleavened cakes of the dough which they brought forth out of Egypt, for it was not leavened; because they were thrust out of Egypt, and could not tarry, neither had they prepared for themselves any victual. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=40}}Now the time that the children of Israel dwelt in Egypt was four hundred and thirty years. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=41}}And it came to pass at the end of four hundred and thirty years, even the selfsame day it came to pass, that all the host of the {{sc|Lord}} went out from the land of Egypt. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=42}}It was a night of watching unto the {{sc|Lord}} for bringing them out from the land of Egypt; this same night is a night of watching unto the {{sc|Lord}} for all the children of Israel throughout their generations.

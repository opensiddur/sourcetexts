{{verse|chapter=4|verse=25}}When thou shalt beget children, and children's children, and ye shall have been long in the land, and shall deal corruptly, and make a graven image, even the form of any thing, and shall do that which is evil in the sight of the {{sc|Lord}} thy God, to provoke Him; {{verse|chapter=4|verse=26}}I call heaven and earth to witness against you this day, that ye shall soon utterly perish from off the land whereunto ye go over the Jordan to possess it; ye shall not prolong your days upon it, but shall utterly be destroyed. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=27}}And the {{sc|Lord}} shall scatter you among the peoples, and ye shall be left few in number among the nations, whither the {{sc|Lord}} shall lead you away. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=28}}And there ye shall serve gods, the work of men's hands, wood and stone, which neither see, nor hear, nor eat, nor smell. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=29}}But from thence ye will seek the {{sc|Lord}} thy God; and thou shalt find Him, if thou search after Him with all thy heart and with all thy soul. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=30}}In thy distress, when all these things are come upon thee, in the end of days, thou wilt return to the {{sc|Lord}} thy God, and hearken unto His voice; {{verse|chapter=4|verse=31}}for the {{sc|Lord}} thy God is a merciful God; He will not fail thee, neither destroy thee, nor forget the covenant of thy fathers which He swore unto them. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=32}}For ask now of the days past, which were before thee, since the day that God created man upon the earth, and from the one end of heaven unto the other, whether there hath been any such thing as this great thing is, or hath been heard like it? {{verse|chapter=4|verse=33}}Did ever a people hear the voice of God speaking out of the midst of the fire, as thou hast heard, and live? {{verse|chapter=4|verse=34}}Or hath God assayed to go and take Him a nation from the midst of another nation, by trials, by signs, and by wonders, and by war, and by a mighty hand, and by an outstretched arm, and by great terrors, according to all that the {{sc|Lord}} your God did for you in Egypt before thine eyes? {{verse|chapter=4|verse=35}}Unto thee it was shown, that thou mightest know that the {{sc|Lord}}, He is God; there is none else beside Him. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=36}}Out of heaven He made thee to hear His voice, that He might instruct thee; and upon earth He made thee to see His great fire; and thou didst hear His words out of the midst of the fire. {{verse|chapter=4|verse=37}}And because He loved thy fathers, and chose their seed after them, and brought thee out with His presence, with His great power, out of Egypt, {{verse|chapter=4|verse=38}}to drive out nations from before thee greater and mightier than thou, to bring thee in, to give thee their land for an inheritance, as it is this day; {{verse|chapter=4|verse=39}}know this day, and lay it to thy heart, that the {{sc|Lord}}, He is God in heaven above and upon the earth beneath; there is none else.<noinclude>{{c|218}}</noinclude>

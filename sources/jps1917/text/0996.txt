{{dropinitial|{{anchor|2:1}}2}}And Naomi had a kinsman of her husband's, a mighty man of valour, of the family of Elimelech, and his name was Boaz. {{verse|2|2}}And Ruth the Moabitess said unto Naomi: 'Let me now go to the field, and glean among the ears of corn after him in whose sight I shall find favour.' And she said unto her: 'Go, my daughter.' {{verse|2|3}}And she went, and came and gleaned in the field after the reapers; and her hap was to lighten the portion of the field belonging unto Boaz, who was of the family of Elimelech. {{verse|2|4}}And, behold, Boaz came from Beth-lehem, and said unto the reapers: 'The {{sc|Lord}} be with you.' And they answered him: 'The {{sc|Lord}} bless thee.' {{verse|2|5}}Then said Boaz unto his servant that was set over the reapers: 'Whose damsel is this?' {{verse|2|6}}And the servant that was set over the reapers answered and said: 'It is a Moabitish damsel that came back with Naomi out of the field of Moab; {{verse|2|7}}and she said: Let me glean, I pray you, and gather after the reapers among the sheaves; so she came, and hath continued even from the morning until now, save that she tarried a little in the house.' {{verse|2|8}}Then said Boaz unto Ruth: 'Hearest thou not, my daughter? Go not to glean in another field, neither pass from hence, but abide here fast by my maidens. {{verse|2|9}}Let thine eyes be on the field that they do reap, and go thou after them; have I not charged the young men that they shall not touch thee? and when thou art athirst, go unto the vessels, and drink of that which the young men have drawn.' {{verse|2|10}}Then she fell on her face, and bowed down to the ground, and said unto him: 'Why have I found favour in thy sight, that thou shouldest take cognizance of me, seeing I am a foreigner?' {{verse|2|11}}And Boaz answered and said unto her: 'It hath fully been told me, all that thou hast done unto thy mother-in-law since the death of thy husband; and how thou hast left thy father and thy mother, and the land of thy nativity, and art come unto a people that thou knewest not heretofore. {{verse|2|12}}The {{sc|Lord}} recompense thy work, and be thy reward complete from the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, under whose wings thou art come to take refuge.' {{verse|2|13}}Then she said: 'Let me find favour in thy sight, my lord; for that thou hast comforted me, and for that thou hast spoken to the heart of thy handmaid, though I be not as one of thy handmaidens.' {{verse|2|14}}And Boaz said unto her at meal-time: 'Come hither, and eat of the bread, and dip thy morsel in the vinegar.'

{{verse|9|10}}And it came to pass at the end of twenty years, wherein Solomon had built the two houses, the house of the {{sc|Lord}} and the king's house—{{verse|9|11}}now Hiram the king of Tyre had furnished Solomon with cedar-trees and cypress-trees, and with gold, according to all his desire—that then king Solomon gave Hiram twenty cities in the land of Galilee. {{verse|9|12}}And Hiram came out from Tyre to see the cities which Solomon had given him: and they pleased him not. {{verse|9|13}}And he said: 'What cities are these which thou hast given me, my brother?' And they were called the land of Cabul, unto this day. {{verse|9|14}}And Hiram sent to the king sixscore talents of gold.

{{verse|9|15}}And this is the account of the levy which king Solomon raised; to build the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and his own house, and Millo, and the wall of Jerusalem, and Hazor, and Megiddo, and Gezer. {{verse|9|16}}Pharaoh king of Egypt had gone up, and taken Gezer, and burnt it with fire, and slain the Canaanites that dwelt in the city, and given it for a portion unto his daughter, Solomon's wife. {{verse|9|17}}And Solomon built Gezer, and Beth-horon the nether, {{verse|9|18}}and Baalath, and Tadmor in the wilderness, in the land, {{verse|9|19}}and all the store-cities that Solomon had, and the cities for his chariots, and the cities for his horsemen, and that which Solomon desired to build for his pleasure in Jerusalem, and in Lebanon, and in all the land of his dominion. {{verse|9|20}}All the people that were left of the Amorites, the Hittites, the Perizzites, the Hivites, and the Jebusites, who were not of the children of Israel; {{verse|9|21}}even their children that were left after them in the land, whom the children of Israel were not able utterly to destroy, of them did Solomon raise a levy of bondservants, unto this day. {{verse|9|22}}But of the children of Israel did Solomon make no bondservants; but they were the men of war, and his servants, and his princes, and his captains, and rulers of his chariots and of his horsemen.

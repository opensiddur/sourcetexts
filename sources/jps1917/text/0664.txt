{{verse|52|12}}Now in the fifth month, in the tenth day of the month, which was the nineteenth year of king Nebuchadrezzar, king of Babylon, came Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard, who stood before the king of Babylon, into Jerusalem; {{verse|52|13}}and he burned the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and the king's house; and all the houses of Jerusalem, even every great man's house, burned he with fire. {{verse|52|14}}And all the army of the Chaldeans, that were with the captain of the guard, broke down all the walls of Jerusalem round about. {{verse|52|15}}Then Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard carried away captive of the poorest sort of the people, and the residue of the people that remained in the city, and those that fell away, that fell to the king of Babylon, and the residue of the multitude. {{verse|52|16}}But Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard left of the poorest of the land to be vinedressers and husbandmen. {{verse|52|17}}And the pillars of brass that were in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, and the bases and the brazen sea that were in the house of the {{sc|Lord}}, did the Chaldeans break in pieces, and carried all the brass of them to Babylon. {{verse|52|18}}The pots also, and the shovels, and the snuffers, and the basins, and the pans, and all the vessels of brass wherewith they ministered, took they away. {{verse|52|19}}And the cups, and the fire-pans, and the basins, and the pots, and the candlesticks, and the pans, and the bowls—that which was of gold, in gold, and that which was of silver, in silver—the captain of the guard took away. {{verse|52|20}}The two pillars, the one sea, and the twelve brazen bulls that were under the bases, which king Solomon had made for the house of the {{sc|Lord}}—the brass of all these vessels was without weight. {{verse|52|21}}And as for the pillars, the height of the one pillar was eighteen cubits; and a line of twelve cubits did compass it; and the thickness thereof was four fingers; it was hollow. {{verse|52|22}}And a capital of brass was upon it; and the height of the one capital was five cubits, with network and pomegranates upon the capital round about, all of brass; and the second pillar also had like unto these, and pomegranates. {{verse|52|23}}And there were ninety and six pomegranates on the outside; all the pomegranates were a hundred upon the network round about.

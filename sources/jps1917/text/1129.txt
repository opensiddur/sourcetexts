And there were wars between Rehoboam and Jeroboam continually. {{verse|12|16}}And Rehoboam slept with his fathers, and was buried in the city of David; and Abijah his son reigned in his stead.

{{dropinitial|{{anchor|13:1}}13}} In the eighteenth year of king Jeroboam began Abijah to reign over Judah. {{verse|13|2}}Three years reigned he in Jerusalem; and his mother's name was Micaiah the daughter of Uriel of Gibeah. And there was war between Abijah and Jeroboam. {{verse|13|3}}And Abijah joined battle with an army of valiant men of war, even four hundred thousand chosen men; and Jeroboam set the battle in array against him with eight hundred thousand chosen men, who were mighty men of valour. {{verse|13|4}}And Abijah stood up upon mount Zemaraim, which is in the hill-country of Ephraim, and said: 'Hear me, O Jeroboam and all Israel; {{verse|13|5}}ought ye not to know that the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of Israel, gave the kingdom over Israel to David for ever, even to him and to his sons by a covenant of salt? {{verse|13|6}}Yet Jeroboam the son of Nebat, the servant of Solomon the son of David, rose up, and rebelled against his lord. {{verse|13|7}}And there were gathered unto him vain men, base fellows that strengthened themselves against Rehoboam the son of Solomon, when Rehoboam was young and faint-hearted, and could not withstand them. {{verse|13|8}}And now ye think to withstand the kingdom of the {{sc|Lord}} in the hand of the sons of David; and ye are a great multitude, and there are with you the golden calves which Jeroboam made you for gods. {{verse|13|9}}Have ye not driven out the priests of the {{sc|Lord}}, the sons of Aaron, and the Levites, and have made you priests after the manner of the peoples of other lands? so that whosoever cometh to consecrate himself with a young bullock and seven rams, the same becometh a priest of them that are no gods. {{verse|13|10}}But as for us, the {{sc|Lord}} is our God, and we have not forsaken Him; and we have priests ministering unto the {{sc|Lord}}, the sons of Aaron, and the Levites in their work; {{verse|13|11}}and they burn unto the {{sc|Lord}} every morning and every evening burnt-offerings and sweet incense; the showbread also set they in order upon the pure table; and the candlestick of gold with the lamps thereof, to burn every evening; for we keep the charge of the {{sc|Lord}} our God; but ye have forsaken Him. {{verse|13|12}}And, behold, God is with us at our head, and His priests with the trumpets of alarm to sound an alarm against you. O children of Israel, fight ye not against the {{sc|Lord}}, the God of your fathers; for ye shall not prosper.'

{{verse|chapter=11|verse=31}}And there went forth a wind from the {{sc|Lord}}, and brought across quails from the sea, and let them fall by the camp, about a day's journey on this side, and a day's journey on the other side, round about the camp, and about two cubits above the face of the earth. {{verse|chapter=11|verse=32}}And the people rose up all that day, and all the night, and all the next day, and gathered the quails; he that gathered least gathered ten heaps; and they spread them all abroad for themselves round about the camp. {{verse|chapter=11|verse=33}}While the flesh was yet between their teeth, ere it was chewed, the anger of the {{sc|Lord}} was kindled against the people, and the {{sc|Lord}} smote the people with a very great plague. {{verse|chapter=11|verse=34}}And the name of that place was called Kibroth-hattaavah, because there they buried the people that lusted. {{verse|chapter=11|verse=35}}From <ref>That is, ''The graves of lust.''</ref>Kibroth-hattaavah the people journeyed unto Hazeroth; and they abode at Hazeroth.

And the {{sc|Lord}} heard it.— {{verse|chapter=12|verse=3}}Now the man Moses was very meek, above all the men that were upon the face of the earth.— {{verse|chapter=12|verse=4}}And the {{sc|Lord}} spoke suddenly unto Moses, and unto Aaron, and unto Miriam: 'Come out ye three unto the tent of meeting.' And they three came out. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=5}}And the {{sc|Lord}} came down in a pillar of cloud, and stood at the door of the Tent, and called Aaron and Miriam; and they both came forth. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=6}}And He said: 'Hear now My words: if there be a prophet among you, I the {{sc|Lord}} do make Myself known unto him in a vision, I do speak with him in a dream. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=7}}My servant Moses is not so; he is trusted in all My house; {{verse|chapter=12|verse=8}}with him do I speak mouth to mouth, even manifestly, and not in dark speeches; and the similitude of the {{sc|Lord}} doth he behold; wherefore then were ye not afraid to speak against My servant, against Moses?' {{verse|chapter=12|verse=9}}And the anger of the {{sc|Lord}} was kindled against them; and He departed. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=10}}And when the cloud was removed from over the Tent, behold, Miriam was leprous, as white as snow; and Aaron looked upon Miriam; and, behold, she was leprous. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=11}}And Aaron said unto Moses: 'Oh my lord, lay not, I pray thee, sin upon us, for that we have done foolishly, and for that we have sinned. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=12}}Let her not, I pray, be as one dead, of whom the flesh is half consumed when he cometh out of his mother's womb.' {{verse|chapter=12|verse=13}}And Moses cried unto the {{sc|Lord}}, saying: 'Heal her now, O God, I beseech Thee.'

{{verse|chapter=12|verse=14}}And the {{sc|Lord}} said unto Moses: 'If her father had but spit in her face, should she not hide in shame seven days? let her be shut up without the camp seven days, and after that she shall be brought in again.' {{verse|chapter=12|verse=15}}And Miriam was shut up without the camp seven days; and the people journeyed not till Miriam was brought in again. {{verse|chapter=12|verse=16}}And afterward the people journeyed from Hazeroth, and pitched in the wilderness of Paran.

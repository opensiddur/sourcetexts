{{verse|9|22}}And Abimelech was prince over Israel three years. {{verse|9|23}}And God sent an evil spirit between Abimelech and the men of Shechem; and the men of Shechem dealt treacherously with Abimelech; {{verse|9|24}}that the violence done to the threescore and ten sons of Jerubbaal might come, and that their blood might be laid upon Abimelech their brother, who slew them, and upon the men of Shechem, who strengthened his hands to slay his brethren. {{verse|9|25}}And the men of Shechem set liers-in-wait for him on the tops of the mountains, and they robbed all that came along that way by them; and it was told Abimelech.

And he said to Abimelech: 'Increase thine army, and come out.' {{verse|9|30}}And when Zebul the ruler of the city heard the words of Gaal the son of Ebed, his anger was kindled. {{verse|9|31}}And he sent messengers unto Abimelech in Tormah, saying: 'Behold, Gaal the son of Ebed and his brethren are come to Shechem; and, behold, they will incite the city against thee. {{verse|9|32}}Now therefore, up by night, thou and the people that are with thee, and lie in wait in the field. {{verse|9|33}}And it shall be, that in the morning, as soon as the sun is up, thou shalt rise early, and set upon the city; and, behold, when he and the people that are with him come out against thee, then mayest thou do to them as thou shalt be able.'
